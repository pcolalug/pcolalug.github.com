Title: Re: Gaming... new to it, and fairly new to Linux. Do I have a chance?
Post by: rugmonster on December 09, 2005, 04:19:23 pm
Quick answer...

Open a terminal, and assuming you have the installer in your home directory, type the following.

Code:
$ sh armyops250-linux.run

Otherwise, use the command "cd" to "change directories" to the place the file is located.

Also, I second Dan on Punkbuster. It is included with most online games because it keeps people from cheating. The folks who make Punkbuster are very well known and respected. Maybe it's good to be that paranoid, but relax a little bit. And this leads me into my next point.

Don't be afraid of Google. If you would have searched for punkbuster or how to install AA on Linux, you would have easily found it on the first page of your search. Learning Linux and UNIX is all about reading documentation. People's patience within the community is pretty high, but they also don't put up with people not doing a some of the leg work. No one wants to be a Google proxy. I haven't learned anything about Linux and UNIX from a book that isn't otherwise available on the Internet.

Try just shooting the terrorists, and not the good guys.  (also don't shoot any unarmed ppl).

They just put this in the game to try and get you in the habit of only shooting the opposing force.  Trust me, the lil dress down the sgt gives you is nothing compared to the flak you'll take online for shooting teammates.  Take your time, and only shoot terrorists that are armed.  (skinny, dark = enemy.  cammo, backpack = friendly) 
Alot of people  fuss about the training you go through, but most of it really does help you in game, all it takes is a little patience.

Honestly, I found AA itself to be quite easy to install.  Getting PB up and running takes a little more time, but isn't that hard.
