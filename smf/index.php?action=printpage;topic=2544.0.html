Title: Best Distro for AMD64
Post by: rugmonster on August 03, 2005, 03:31:56 pm
I know, I know...there is no "best" distro, but I'm sort of getting frustrated with the state of the AMD64 world. Unfortunately, there are still a lot of things that I use that aren't quite x86-64 ready (ex. OpenOffice.org2, Flash w/ Firefox, w32codecs). There seems to be two solutions going around. The more popular is using lib32 and lib64 directories for 32-bit and 64-bit libraries. The other is to load the 32bit applications in a chroot jail along with their libs.

SuSE handled the issue of lib32 and lib64 fairly seemlessly. I got what I needed installed and working, but things got very crufty and SuSE 9.3 just felt a little sluggish, especially compared to Slackware on my x86 laptop.

Since Slackware and FRG seemed so nice on the laptop, I found SLAMD64 and installed it. Unfortunately, it didn't install X or anything I really needed and I was stuck running installpkg over and over and over. This was a bit more work than I wanted. I never even looked at how they address the 32bit applications and libraries.

Next was Ubuntu Hoary. Apparently, they take the chroot route and to be honest, I don't want to fool with that. I'm looking at Gentoo again, but they too appear to use the clunky chroot environment for running 32bit apps. *sigh*

So, am I stuck with SuSE for a hassle free 64-bit environment? Any suggestions?
