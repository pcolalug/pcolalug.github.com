Aren't you guys lucky I got out of combat training early today and decided to browse the forums.

Yippee...kernel time!!!

Okay, once you have your kernel patch, you need to get your kernel source...be it from Mandrake or from kernel.org. *Hint: Mandrake kernels will have added support for things you probably use, such as Supermount and other desktop enhancements.* Basically, it is a patched kernel already. Anywho, you get your patch and your kernel source.

The short and sweet of it is to run inside of either the top directory of your kernel source or the directory above the source:
Code:
patch -p0 <name of patch>


You may need to look in the patch file itself to figure out exactly what place in the directory structure it is looking for...be it above the kernel source or in the base directory of it. As a rule of thumb, do your patching in another directory besides /usr/src/linux and also backup of the originals should you need them.

You will then need to recompile your kernel. In the kernel configuration, you will probably find the option to include the functions the patch provides. If not, don't worry too much. Test it out and see what happens. For more information on kernel compilation, see the Kernel HOWTO. I will give this warning though. Not all patches work like they should, so if you have problems with it, check with the author or appropriate mailing lists/websites...or us if you like.
