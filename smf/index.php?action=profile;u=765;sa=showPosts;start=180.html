195  Club Stuff / Introductions / Introduction to me on: August 03, 2007, 10:49:06 am
I've been on Windows all my life.  Well, the majority of it anyways.  My first computer experience was on our old Magitronic we got around '93 with DOS and Windows 3.11 where I'd play games and once in a very blue moon get online.  When we upgraded to Windows 95, I didn't really like it but I eventually accepted it.  I hated the computers at school because there was no right-click and they were more fancy than efficient.  I later came to despise these Apple computers.  In '98, we got a Dell Pentium 2 with Windows 98.  A great computer at the time.   My mother still uses it.  I was on this computer until 2006, when in late April, my parents bought me and my sister, who was going to college, laptops.  I have a Dell Inspiron E1505 with 1 gig of Ram, a 100*gb HD, and an Intel Centrino Duo processor at 1.87 GHz.

When I got my laptop I was finally able to do things I couldn't have done before on the "family computer".  One of which is to toy around with Linux.  My first linux experience was with the Live CD of Ubuntu 6.  It was okay, but I didn't install it.  I was trying to get a friend with a fairly old computer of his own to install linux to replace his Windows 98SE which was riddled with viruses and spyware.  Eventually his computer broke and he gave me some of its componenets, which I put in a new computer, along with Freespire, another friend and I gave him.

That Christmas he got a laptop and hasn't used the computer we gave him, even though all the files on his old HDD such as IM logs and word documents are on it.

At school I joined a TV Production class for an elective and all the editing was done on an iMac.  I hated it just as much as I hated the macs from my elementary school.  Since we rarely used the computers to do editing, most of their time was devoted to surfing the web, so I decided to find a way to get linux on their in a similar fashion to the Live CD.  I then made a bootable USB drive to boot DSL.  It was okay, but had issues getting online.  I then tried a larger distro, SLAX.  It worked fine and gave me way more potential than the Novell Zenworks client at my school gave me (I could download files for instance).  As I showed this to a friend of mine, some girl came up and saw the Linux boot screen and said "Ooooh!  You're hacking!!"  It was quite funny.  It was at this moment I realised that I was a computer nerd because I laughed at how she used "hacking" in it's correct sense to me, but in her definition (hollywood's definition), she was completely wrong.

Nearly a year after I got my laptop I finally installed PCLinuxOS 2007 and in the process of partitioning rewrote my MBR and was locked out of windows for days.  But, I had some help from a member of this forum who encouraged me to join.  So, if you're reading this, thanks again for the help.  What won me over with PCLOS was the ease of use and integrated 3D Beryl support.  However, I felt that overall it was lacking.  So, after a month of research I finally installed openSUSE 10.2, which I now dual boot with Windows XP Pro.

My last linux venture was with my trusty USB drive, which I put Knoppix on.  This Knoppix can boot with the Beryl desktop, which is really cool in my opinion.  So, I always have a full 3D operating system wherever I go now.

So, that's pretty much my story.  I know that by comparison to many of you guys on the forum I am still a meager peasant rolling around in the golden mud that is the noobville for linux users, and I pray that thou hath mercy upon me when I inquire upon trivial subjects such as binary compiling and audio driver issues.
