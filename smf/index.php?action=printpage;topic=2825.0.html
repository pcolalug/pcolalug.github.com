Title: Fedora Core 5
Post by: danc on March 26, 2006, 09:49:06 am
Got a little bored yesterday and decided to give the new FC5 a look, mostly to see if Gnome 2.14 had anything exciting in it.  The installation was routine (don't care for the partitioner wizard), and was very slow (2 hours).  Things seemed to work fine, and I haven't seen any real noticeable changes in Gnome.  Getting the Nvidia drivers loaded was quite a chore, due to a documented glitch in the highly modified FC5 kernel.  Eventually found a fix with Google, but it was not a trivial task to install, and would probably not be accomplished by a newcomer to Linux.  I expect they will fix this with a new kernel release "soon".

Anyway, it seems to run fine, and surely looks very nice.  Default theme in Gnome is "Clearlooks" (modified), and things like icons and widgets, and especially fonts, are very smooth and pretty.  It also ships with KDE 3.5.1.  As usual with Fedora, a lot of multimedia stuff is not included (licensing issues), but can be found and installed fairly easily.  The applications seem very much up to date.  Haven't seen anything that really gets me too excited, and I won't be switching over to it, but it seems like a decent distro in most regards.  If you like Redhat/FC, you'll want to give this a look.

Dan
