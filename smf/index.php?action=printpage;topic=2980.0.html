Title: Re: basic need-to-know instructions please
Post by: rugmonster on August 18, 2006, 10:31:12 am
Ubuntu does a great job with them as well, but Debian more than likely won't be carrying much in the way of the commercial firmwares. It goes against their ideals of a free and open source operating system. You can get non-free packages through Debian repositories, but you have to specifically add them. Part of the reason I use Ubuntu on the laptop is because it did pick up my Prism54 card and had the firmware included so everything just worked.

Chances are, you just burned a bad disc, had bad media, or the drive trying to read the disc didn't like it. Make sure you do an md5sum on the iso. There will be an md5sum file in the same directory where you downloaded the iso. If that checks out okay, then try burning it again.
