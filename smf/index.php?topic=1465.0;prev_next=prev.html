After all the talk, I figured I would share my new system with you guys. Since I have a theme going of naming all of my systems after the Ender's Game series characters, I decided on Jane for this one. I mean all I need is a philotic connection and an ansible hooked up to this system and she should come to life.

Here are the specs:

AMD Athlon64 3200+ (2.0 Ghz 1MB L2 Cache)
Asus K8V Deluxe Motherboard
1 GB (512MB x 2) OCZ Performance Series PC3200 DDR
Sapphire ATI Radeon 9800XT w/ 256MB DDR
Creative Audigy2 ZS
Seagate 200GB 7200RPM ATA133 HD (ST3200822A)
Logitech Z-5300 5.1 Speakers
NEC LCD1760V-BK 17" LCD (2)
Kingwin KT-424-BK-WM Case
Antec TruePower 430 Watt Powersupply
Samsung 52x CD-RW
Sony Floppy and 16x DVD-ROM

Jane's CPU


Jane in all her glory

So far, the system has been amazing. I am running Windows XP SP1 on the system right now, but I'm going to eventually play around with Gentoo on here so I can get some use out of the 64bit capabilities. I have plenty of hard disk space to play with, but I'm in the process of reworking all of my systems, so it may be a while.

The only problem I have run into is with the stupid blue cold cathode light. I first bought a generic $35 400W power supply, but had some stability issues. I turned off the light and all runs great. I thought I was just really close to maxing out the power supply until I bought the Antec today. As soon as I turned the light on, the system started dying. The system would lock up or just shut off completely. I think the light I bought is bad, so I'll go pick up a replacement tomorrow along with some rounded IDE cables and some black zip ties.

As for performance, the system scored 19735 on 3DMark 2001 and 6516 on 3DMark 2003. I haven't run any benches with games yet, but I will soon. I can say this. I got FarCry today (w00t for first day of release) and I can run it pretty much maxed out with no jerkiness (I don't care about FPS as long as the game looks fluid). I signed up for the beta on the 64 bit version they will be releasing of it soon, so I'll have to measure some frame rates to compare the two.

Oh yeah, the CPU is running at a constant 45C and rises to 50C when under load and the GPU is at 66C almost all the time. This isn't overclocked, but there seems to be plenty of room to do so, but I won't be playing with that for a while. I really don't want to have a $250 or $400 paper weight.

In case you're curious, I ordered all this from Newegg and got great delivery time and very competitive prices. I'm very happy with this system and had a great time putting it all together. Thanks for the advice you guys gave me while I was researching it all. Once my new desk gets here on Friday, I'll be all set.

Edit:
I got a new light installed and so far it seems to be doing pretty well. I'll see how things go tonight. Here is a picture of the internals with a quick job of cable management using zip ties and rounded ide cables.

Looks great! Did Newegg give you some kind of token gift, like a t-shirt? I will have to buy something from them just to get a shirt.

I agree that the light problem is probably the light. It could be generating an RF field that is causing problems. Try pulling the light out of the box, but still connected. While moving the light around to different areas see if it will stay stable from certain points and not others. If so, then the light is producing RFI and either a relocation of the light, or possibly a new light should take care of the problem.
