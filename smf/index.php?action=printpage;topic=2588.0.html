Title: Engadget 1985 Flashback
Post by: danc on September 11, 2005, 09:39:36 pm
Ahhhhh yes, the good 'ole days.  I too was a Fidonet BBS sysop (1:115/321 in Chicago), from 1993-1996.  MSDOS with PCBoard software, and either Frontdoor or Intermail front-end.  This was on a 386SX-25, with either 4Mb or 8Mb of RAM (can't recall which).  Had a very complicated batch file to run the whole thing, exiting to doors and utilities, and sending/receiving Fidonet Echomail in the middle of the night (via my USR Courier Dual Standard modem at 28.8Kbps).  It was great fun.

I will always remember that period of time with great affection.  Some of my best computer accomplishments were during that time, including installing Linux for the first time (Slackware) from a bazillion floppies.  Actually got it up and running, but didn't stay with it as I didn't really know what to do with it.  There wasn't a readily accessible WWW, and no Google, to help out.  Anyway, that's a good trip down memory lane....   :lol:
