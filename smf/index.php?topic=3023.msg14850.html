First, let me say, thus far into my testing of xubuntu, I have been really impressed. 
See http://www.pcolalug.org/smf/index.php?topic=2993.0

However, I've run into a problem repeatedly that stems back to debian itself.

For some reason, debian developers can't resist mucking around with packages that do not fit their definition of "free software".

For instance, don't bother installing anything that requires java from the package management system.  Everything is linked and crosslinked with gcj (and it's multitude of dependencies).  For that reason, your better off getting Open office, azureus and others from their respective official download sites. 

Another thing that I came across today, while trying to burn the latest version of Knoppix, is the fact they've rewritten parts of cdrtools in their binary version, and introduced several nifty bugs in the process.
See http://cdrecord.berlios.de/old/private/cdrecord.html

Quote
Warning: do not use Debian binaries as they include many Debian specific bugs and still do not run correctly on Linux-2.6
Be careful with cdrecord derivates from Debian, Debian seems to ignore the new constraints for
SCSI pass through on Linux-2.6. As a result, that they still incorrectly believe that it is possible
to use SCSI pass through as non-root on Linux-2.6, they created a cdrecord variant that tries to hide
the problems instead of dealing with the problems.

To be fair, suse and redhat are also guilty of this to some degree.

You all know I don't have a high tolerence for this sort of thing in distributions.  I have to say, as nice as *buntu is, this is pushing it.
