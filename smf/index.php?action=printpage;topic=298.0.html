Post by: imnes on December 10, 2002, 08:20:45 pm
Well if you're wanting to learn something easy and forgiving, just for the sake of learning the basics about programming, go for QBasic in DOS/Win or tcl in linux.  I think they're by far the most relaxed and easy to pickup languages.

If you're feeling more adventurous, you could always try Kylix, it's linux version of Delphi / C++ Builder and they have a free version.  You can write simple console apps in Pascal code, and then point-and-click your way to some really neat XWindows apps without having to write much code.  Pretty fun to play around in.

And if the thought of pointers and objects isn't too scary, then go straight for C or C++.  There's by far more free online tutorials relaiting to the subject, and pretty much every linux/bsd distro will have the compiler installed by default.  This is the hardest way to go, but it's the language of choice for most geeks.

Have fun HaXor ;)

Normally, I don't jump in on things and rant when someone's asking for advice.  But, here's my $0.02 on it (at the risk of inciting a holy war).  Don't go picking up any of the Basics.  They're not good even for learning tools and will just flatout teach you bad programming habits.

But as for something easy and forgiving:  Python is an easy language to pick up on and is well worth the experience, as is Perl.. Though I'd wager to say Perl can quickly overwhelm with it's "More than one way" philosophy.

I wouldn't recommend jumping right into the deep end with C or C++, though.  Not until you've at least gotten a bit more comfortable with another language or two anyways.

Ok, here goes. It doesn't matter. Pick Javascript. Hell, you want some fun? Might I sugguest Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (http://mitpress.mit.edu/sicp/full-text/book/book.html) or, as its known in hackish, The Wizard Book (I know, I know, I just can't leave some things alone...Have I mentioned Gentoo (http://www.gentoo.org) this week?).

The truth of the matter is that learning a language will not make you a good programmer, nor will it prepare you to solve problems with programming. So many languages are good at so many different things. So, just pick one, any one, and concentrate more on how to think about making the bits dance to your tune.

I know this may not be too helpful, so here's the obligitory language sugguestion: Java. Its easy to pick up, is more object oriented than most, well documented, runs on most platforms, and there are tons of free development tools out there.

Oh, yeah, what crowe said. The "simple" languages like those in the "Basic" family will rot your brain and make you unfit for any computer-related occupation. These are to be avoided at all costs. Hmm. Ok, so it matters a bit.
