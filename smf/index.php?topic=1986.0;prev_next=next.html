I'm currently, happily, running Slackware 10 on my new gaming box instead of Gentoo, either version.  I don't consider myself a master of Linux, far from it, but I also don't consider myself an absolute newbie.  I like to think I understand a decent amount of what goes on with Linux, why, and how it differs from other major OSes (after all, we did spend a decent amount of time on Linux in "Operating Systems" back in college).

However, Gentoo proved to be nothing but a source of frustration to me.  Like in another thread I posted, to sum up, WHERE IS THE INSTALLER?  WHAT DO YOU MEAN THERE'S NO INSTALLER?

Seriously, even Debian, upon which this is based, has an installer.  I figured, "Okay, no installer, I'll just follow the handbook included on the LiveCD and everything will be peachy." Survey says?  EEENNNN!  Wrong!

I tried, and I mean REALLY tried, for over two hours, to follow that installation guide.  I found myself unable to accomplish the task.  I don't know why, it's not that I didn't understand the words printed there as much as I found it lacking in terms of, "Type this, run this with these options if you want, hit make," so on and so forth.  Perhaps I'm asking for a bit of spoonfeeding but given that Gentoo's been around awhile I don't think that's too much to ask for.



I really, REALLY wanted to try out Gentoo, I've heard positive things about it, but this is too much.  I actually had an easier time trying to get Debian up and running than this, and that's saying a lot considering it took me one and a half days to get Debian partially working.







Sorry about the rant, nobody out here IRL wants to hear me rant about Linux and other geek-things so it's left here.  Note, this is not bashing Gentoo as a distribution, I'm positive it's got power and must be very nice once it's running...but that's after you've gotten it running which is what I'm having a serious problem with.

It definitely sounds like your problem is with Links (or lynx). The handbook on the LiveCD is the exact same as the one on the web. Everyone here's right, though. Gentoo isn't for slapping on and trying one evening. I install from stage 1 everytime and have it down to a complete, functional desktop in about 2 - 2.5 days. My first successful install took a week.

Gentoo does give you some shortcuts,  but nothing like you'd prolly enjoy. For example, though I've never tried it, I could probably get a stage 3/GRP install up and running in a couple of hours, but where's the 1337 in that!?

Anyway, sorry for the bad experience. You might want to do a bit of research before trying certain distros. Most Gentoo'ers will tell you it ain't for you. Asking a Gentoo user if you should try it tends to be like talking to foriegn expats about Finland. Ask them if its a good idea to move there, they'll tell you you are insane. Ask them why they stay, and they'll tell you they'd never live any where else.
