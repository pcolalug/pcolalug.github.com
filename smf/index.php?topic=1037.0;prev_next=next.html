The CompTIA certifications don't hold a whole lot of weight with me. I haven't looked into the RHCE too much, but I want to get the LPIC. From everything I have read about the LPI series of certifications, they are hard to get, but definitely show you know your stuff. Go to their site and check out the tests they have available. I will be getting the O'Reilly's book that the library has, LPIC in a Nutshell next payday. Yes, it is out-dated due to a new release of the test, but it does have some good study material.

Along with having linux certifications, I think it would do you good to learn more of the other "unix-type" environments, such as the BSDs and Solaris. Sun has some good certifications, but Solaris doesn't always play nice on x86 hardware. I've always been told that to get a real Solaris experience, you need a Sparc...and make sure you get something that is new enough to run the newer versions of Solaris. I just paid the $20 to download the "free" release of Solaris 9 for x86 and I'll let you know how it goes. Back to the point, it seems that in the unix world, to be marketable, it is good to have a broad background. The core concepts are the same, but knowing the differences and having experience with them all helps.

Depends on where you want to work...
« Reply #3 on: July 28, 2003, 06:40:24 am »
I've had mixed experience with certification and Linux.  I think getting a certificate and deciding which one to get should mostly be driven by the type of company you'd like to work at.  Usually the only places that really want certification are pretty bureaucratic.  Many other linux houses will just put you through some solid technical screening to determine if you're up to the task of working there.  

 If you are definitely sold on getting the certification, deciding which one might be best done by perusing Monster and other job boards for a while to see who wants what.  I know that quite a few companies run Redhat exclusively because it offers business-backed support.  I think this helps increase the value of the RHCE cert since it will mean good things to a redhat-based company.  The RHCE is also pretty cool because you are tested against real boxes with real problems.   I can't comment on the LPIC because I don't know much about it. From what I can tell, it's just a computerized standardized test, so I dunno.  The O'Reilly courses seem to be novice-aimed, but the syllabi look pretty comprehensive.  I imagine the O'Reilly cert. coupled with some small business work experience would probably go over rather well.

Good luck!
