Title: What's Happening to Gnome
Post by: imnes on March 02, 2005, 09:12:42 pm
Gnome support has been steadily declining and I'm not really sure why.  If you haven't noticed, the only major distributions actively supporting Gnome currently (in their stable releases) are Ubuntu and Fedora.  

Gnome 2.10 is going to be released pretty soon, and all the other major distro's (SuSE, Mandrake, Slackware) are still shipping their old Gnome 2.6 packages.  In fact Slackware's gnome packages in the latest slackware release were last touched in May of last year, and the official word is Slackware 11 will not ship Gnome.  

Current slackware release also doesn't ship OpenOffice, FireFox, or Evolution.

Don't know what Gentoo comes with I haven't really looked, guess I should check that out.

In #slackware, Pat V was taking questions from people, and to the Gnome users asking about Gnome being completely removed in the next version his answer was if you want to run Gnome, go run Ubuntu.

Who else here runs Gnome, and what distro and version are you using?

I'm currently on Ubuntu Hoary.  (Although I may start running Linspire on here full time again once the new release supports my video card).
