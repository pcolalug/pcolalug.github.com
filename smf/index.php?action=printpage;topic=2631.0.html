Title: printer advice
Post by: dpeach on October 04, 2005, 10:08:02 pm
I have an HP Deskjet 812C (really old). There were some models that they made that did not work with Linux. I think the whole 700 series of inkjets did not. But, now with USB, I *think* that just about everything HP is putting out works in Linux. I would not buy though without checking out the link mentioned above.

For the record. I have had several laser printers. All of them worked flawlessly in Linux. Different brands and drivers. I have also plugged into several different lasers in my travels. I have yet to find one that did not work easily in Linux. That is not to say they are not out there, but I think it would be rare to find one.

As far as a printer/scanner/copier/fax/coffee maker, I have to give warning. I bought an HP scanner about a year ago. Based on what I read *all* HP USB scanners work in Linux...except like 2. I bought one of those two. So if you go for an all-in-one, make sure all the features of it are supported in Linux.
