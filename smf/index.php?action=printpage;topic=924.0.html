In case someone didn't know about this resource...  The Usenet newsgroups are a *GREAT* source of help on anything (and can be very amusing at times too...).  I read (and post in) the alt.os.linux.mandrake and alt.games.wolfenstein groups daily.

I've found a *LOT* of solutions to common problems this way, and you can really learn a lot about things you wouldn't even have thought of.

The software to use newsgroups is already on your machine (probably).  Probably the two most popular applications (GUI) for this are Pan and Knode (I much prefer Pan).  If you're a hardcore CLI user   :)  you can use slrn or tin (I've used slrn and it's pretty good).

Anyway, it's something fun to do, and useful.  Warning - this activity can be very time-consuming and addictive (like IRC)...   :wink:

If anyone would like more info, or help with getting started, let me know.

Dan
