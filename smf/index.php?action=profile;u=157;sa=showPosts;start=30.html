One quick way I look for hardware drivers is to boot up Knoppix. That is a live CD with very good hardware detection. I don't guarantee it will work, but it is a good start. Now go back to your Linux that you are having issues with and try to get the driver Knoppix was using to work for you.

By Live CD, I mean a distro that does not have to be installed on the hard drive. It runs from the CD and RAM. So nothing gets installed/damaged from your current install.
