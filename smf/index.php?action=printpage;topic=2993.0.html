In the past, I've tried many a linux distro, however each has eventually driven me back to slackware.  My roomie patrick lent me his laptop.  I had to find a distro that was both feature rich, and lightwieght.  Enter xubuntu!  I'm *deeply* impressed with this distro.  Here are my reasons why, in no particular order.

1.  Apt-get --  For the 98% of the time it works, apt-get is truely awesome.  It is by far the best package management system I have ever used.  I love the way you can hook into debian unstable's repositories and download anything you need in a flash.

2.  Plug and Play --  While not unique to linux in general, the plug and play capibilities of xubuntu rivals that of its much bigger breatheren.  For example, drivers for my network card (atheros based) were loaded during install.  I know it's not that hard to compile madwifi drivers, but being able to point and click your way to a complete wireless setup in mere minutes is nice.  Also, many features of the laptop such as suspend/resume work nicely (that's a first I've seen).

3.  Bundled software -- Or lack there of.  I love stand alone software that requires neither gnome or kde to run.  For example, you have the standard desktop fare such as firefox, thunderbird and gaim, as well as xfce exclusives such as thundar (a drop in replacement for gnome's nautilus file browser), and an xfce movie player that handles most codecs well (I hate ubuntu's policy of tightly bundling gstreamer).

If you're on the lookout for a feature rich featherwieght, you owe it to yourself to at least try xubuntu.  Do yourself a favor though, and grab the alternate install disks.  Not only will they install well on machines with less than 192mb of ram, it also has install options for LTSP, raid setup, and automated deployment.

Note, dan has brought another flavor called fluxbuntu to my attention.  However, it does not seem to be supported well, in fact the site is down right now.
