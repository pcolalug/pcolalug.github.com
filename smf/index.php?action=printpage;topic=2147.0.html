I think Steve has a valid point, though I would agree that it is not a problem. If I remember my electronics well enough (I can look this up if we need to debate it), if you have a power supply with the proper voltage, but higher amperage, then you could do this safely. The higher amperage would take care of the extra drain that the fan would require. But not be damaging to the unit like higher voltage would be (though there is an amazing tolerance in these components for higher or lower voltage. But the device is made to run at a particular voltage to maintain proper timing.)

In my feeble mind this is borne out by my truck. The amperage of the battery is much more than my dome lamp needs to operate. But yet the extra amperage does not destroy the lamp with too much current.

I would think that finding yourself a proper voltage power supply with a higher amperage will eliminate all concerns. Though, I am not concerned with the current set-up (mainly because it ain't mine to worry about).

Title: Overheating modem
Post by: danc on December 30, 2004, 10:23:59 pm
You're on the right track, dpeach...  A power supply (or a truck battery) doesn't "have" any amperage to it.  It supplies voltage, and is "capable" of supplying a certain amount of current flow (amperage).  If it's rated for 1 amp, it can supply a maximum of 1 amp of current flow.  But the amount of *actual* current flow is determined by the load on the power supply (in your example the dome light).  The load wants a certain amount of amperage, and if the power supply can provide it, it will.  In your example, the dome light doesn't ask for much, and the battery can easily supply it.

Think of current flow as being "pulled" from the power supply in the amount needed by the load, not "pushed" out of the power supply to the load.  As long as you don't try to pull more current out of it than it's capable of supplying, it won't burn up...

Dan

I finally got my fans in. First, I must say this wasn't nearly as hard as I thought it would be. Let me apologize if you are on a 56k connection since there are a lot of pictures here.

Below is the new fan. It's a Thermaltake 60x60x10mm fan meant for processor cooling.

(http://members.cox.net/dgivens4/router/newfan.jpg)

The hardest part was figuring out how to get the router open. It uses a screwless design, but I did void my warrantee in opening it. Basically, you tilt the front blue section down and it releases a latch on the inside and the front slides off. From there, you slide the bottom black shell forward and the top comes off. You can then unscrew a single screw holding the PCB in place and then slide the board back and free it from the bottom shell.

(http://members.cox.net/dgivens4/router/opened.jpg)

Here you can see where I soldered the fan's power in. The two pins in the middle directly above the DC input in the picture.

(http://members.cox.net/dgivens4/router/power.jpg)

Here, I used a Dremel to cut out a hole in the top where the ventilation holes were before just large enough for the fan to be held in place. Since there is plenty of room inside the case, I could set the fan down inside the case a good bit.

(http://members.cox.net/dgivens4/router/insidefan.jpg)

After mounting the fan, I soldered the power leads and used some superglue to make sure the fan stayed in place. I then reassembled the router.

(http://members.cox.net/dgivens4/router/nogrill.jpg)

Since there is a cat in the house who is quite curious, I decided to put a fan grill on the top of the router. I think it also makes for a much cleaner appearance.

(http://members.cox.net/dgivens4/router/grill.jpg)

And here you see it stacked on top of my modem, which will get similar treatment this weekend.

(http://members.cox.net/dgivens4/router/finishedrouter.jpg)

So, in about an hour, I setup a fan running off of the router's power to cool the internals blowing air into the case. The fan is pretty quiet, but I'm beginning to wonder if I should have put a rheostat or power switch in it. It's a quiet purr, so I'm not too worried about it. All seems stable so far. If I have a problem, I'll be sure to let you guys know.
