I'm not sure if things have changed or not, but back when I was the sysop, the only person allow to make changes to the forum layouts etc, was the sysop. Moderators could move messages around, archive old stuff off of a forum etc. but nobody had permissions to make changes to the boards. This comes from the fact that usually the sysop is the only person with physical access to the server and should something happen during a change like a system crash, the sysop is the only one with the ability to make things right.  However, like any organization, the sysop can't really act on his/her own, the elected executive board has to decide that the changes are in the clubs best interest.

Also, like any organization, just because someone is president it doesn't give them the rights to jump around the chain of command and make changes. ie. the President of a company wouldn't dare go into the server room and start deleting and adding users or configuring the email system. That's what they have a system administrator for.
