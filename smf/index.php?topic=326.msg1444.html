Well, I am already a payed member of the club and bought my Mandrake Distro. So, I'm not going to give them any more money.

I'm starting to have second thoughts about Mandrake's bussiness model. They come out about every 4 to 6 months and say how strapped they are for cash. They then ask for people to join the club and follow that up with a thank you for all of the members. I don't think that the way to run a bussiness is to constantly "beg" for contributions. If you're product isn't selling then make it better or anylize the way you do bussiness. I think that one of Mandrake's biggest shortfalls is that they release the free distro before they get it to the stores. If this was my Bussiness, I'd make sure it was on store shelves at least one week before I put it up for download or at least put it up for download the same day it hits the store shelves. Mandrake, on the other hand, puts the download up a good two to three weeks before you can buy it in the store or receive a payed for copy from their online store. The problem with this is that most people already have it downloaded and installed by the time they can purchase it. What percentage of people are going to buy something that they are already "legally" running? I don't think very many.

So, IMHO, if Mandrake wants to start makeing money, they need to start concentrating more on the bussiness side and less on the free side.

Yeah...what you said. I think they have great free software spirit, but they need to focus on the business market if they want to stay in business. They make one of the most popular distros out there, but for some reason don't seem to go after the business user. They either aren't pushing the products and services that would make them good money or they just don't have them. They should learn from Redhat and SuSE who have products that actually make them money. How hard would it be for them to make and market a server edition or other specialized edition. It seems their only money maker is the PowerPack which is aimed at the home user and then their MandrakeClub. They should know that the only way to make money is to target business. Home users aren't going to support you forever...especially when they can download it from home and get support on usenet and IRC or their friends at the local LUG

You are right about the releases. They don't have to announce that the release is final production before PowerPacks hit the shelves. Most of Mandrake's core audience has no interest in running an RCx. You always have the most problems getting access to a decent mirror in the days/weeks following a major release. Also, they could beat vendors such as cheapbytes.com from beating them to the punch. I got my copy of mdk9.0 from them weeks before PowerPacks were available. If they had packages out on shelves, I know people would be willing to drive down to their local CompUSA rather than try mirror after mirror and then wait for the download, then the burn (unless they are like me, but most aren't). This would also get people into their local stores which would then help bolster the economy. Mandrake could be responsible for the whole market turn around just by changing their release policy. Geez! Why don't they get it together?!

Here is  an idea that won't work now, but could have easily been the way to go 2 years ago.

Don't make it a free download.

I remember back in the dark ages (almost 2 years ago before I had broadband) that the best way to get a copy of a distro was to either buy it, or have a friend burn a legal copy. Make the software free to copy, but at least one person in each town will have to buy it. You just have to have the right friends to get your own free copy. Or 10 of you plop down a little bit of cash and make 10 copies. It is all legal according to their licensing, but they would sell a few copies this way.

This won't work now. If they stopped allowing you to do the download, you would have a negative uprising. I think people would steer away from Mandrake (or whatever distro) altogether.
