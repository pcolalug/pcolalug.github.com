Post by: dpeach on September 20, 2003, 07:40:50 pm
Well, I found a way to do what you want.

The presentation package is the one major disappointment to me in the OO.o package. Don't tell anyone, but I think the best program that Microsoft has ever made (and probably ever will) is PowerPoint. OO.o Presentations is not quite there. But...it does work, and I have done several presentations with it. It is just not as slick as PowerPoint. I don't know why they have not learned from PowerPoint. It is intuitive, it works, things are easy to figure out and do exactly what you want. That is not a description of any of their other software.

But, back to the question.

Open your presentation. Go to "Handout View." (This is the 5th icon from the top on the right hand side just above the scroll up arrow).

(If the page is already portriate, then skip this step.)
Right click on the page that has your slides up and tell it to go to portriate orientation.

Format | Modify Layout. Click the one that will give you three slides up.

Select the slides and move them over to the left.

This will give you exacly what you are looking for, but without the lines. You can add lines with the lines tool from the left toolbar. Once done, this should work everytime with that presentation.

When you print. Select Options at the bottom left. Select "Handouts."

There may be a way to make this your default handout layout, but I have not found it yet.

Hope this helps.
