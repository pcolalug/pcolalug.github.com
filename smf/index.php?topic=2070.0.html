As someone who works part time at an Electronics Retailer and also a Home Theater nut, I'd love to see which model you got.

Something to be careful about, if it's not a DLP television, I wouldn't do to much computing on it. Rear Projection, CRT, and LCD TVs can get burn in with comput/game screens being used on them too much. Also, if it's not DLP make sure and watch 4:3 TV shows "stretched" for the same reason. I've got a buddy at my full time job that never stretched the picture on his $3,000 TV and he now has permanent Black Bar ghost images on the sides of the screen.

Any questions, feel free to PM me or ask them here.

If you're thinking about getting into the Local HD OTA (High Definition shows Over The Airways for free) scene try visiting this link.

Samsung HCN5529W 55".  I was going to get the HCN5527W 55" but it turns out that was not in stock so for $150 I got the one with a built-in HDTV tuner.


I must confess, we don't watch ANY television but I might decide to look into getting an antennae if we want to watch something over the airways.

It's a CRT, rear-projection, but I'm not "too" worried about burn-in.  Computing would be kept to a minimum but it WILL be used for DVDs and gaming, primarily from my XBox.  The XBox, despite HUDs and so forth, I'm not too worried about because most games now have plenty of change, it's not like you'll have one image on the screen for an hour constantly, it's always switching (even in Halo the HUD changes all the time).  For DVDs I hear there might be very slight black bars on the screen but possibly not, so I suppose we'll just have to see.  I don't think anything we've got except one, possibly two DVDs we never watch are in 4:3 anyway.  I managed to convince the wife long ago of the superiority of widescreen and now we will most definitely reap what we've sewn.  


My only concern is that I've seen on two message boards of a few people having an issue with the power supply frying the mainboard.  There aren't any other "reviews" for this TV though and I figured if it was a real problem I'd find it all over the place.  As long as Samsung has a decent warranty on it though I don't mind, I've had EXCELLENT customer service from them so as long as they fix/replace something broken that's cool.

With this particular issue I only found it on TWO boards out of the entirety of google, so it might be an isolated issue.  And apparently it doesn't affect every one, only a few.  I dunno, it worries me that I'll get something broken but I suppose that's a chance with any big-ticket item like this.
