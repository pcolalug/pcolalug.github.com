Yep, I did both, and I've got it working like a charm.  Know what it was?  Something very, VERY easy to miss.

nVidia boards do NOT use UHCI, that's an Intel and VIA thing.  nVidia boards use OHCI and the nForce3-250 uses two OHCI interfaces and one EHCI interface for USB.  As I had no idea previously during my kernel config about how the USB interfaces are derived I just accepted what had already been there and figured if I ran into an issue I'd solve it.

Well how about that, guess I solved it.  


The answer came from "lspci -v" which will tell you, under stable settings like the 2.4.26 kernel in Slackware, what interfaces everything is using.  Absolute gem of a tool.

Anyway yeah, everything works now.  The tilt wheel will probably take some hard-coding to "right" and "left" but otherwise it scrolls, it tracks smoothly, the back and forward buttons work fine...I'm impressed.  It actually tracks better here in Linux-land than it does over in WindowsXP land.  Very cool.

EDIT:  after further investigation there needs to be some clarification.  The back and forward buttons seem to work in some programs, not in others (KwikShow works, Firefox doesn't) so it's going to take some hard-coding...but all the buttons are recognized so far to my testing which of course is the important thing.  More on hardcoding their responses when I get back home.


What saved me a bit of time was a user emes in ##linux on freenode who said he had a working MX1000 and had left the Generic PS/2 support in the kernel.  That was the final thing I was going to try so it saved me some hassle.  Whew, NOW I can FINALLY stick with a kernel build.
