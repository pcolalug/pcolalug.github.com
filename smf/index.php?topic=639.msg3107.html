Ok, the games so far are:

UT2k3 (possible using their counter-strike alike - I'll track this down)
RTcW - Enemy Territory (really excited about this one!)
Q3A

I'll post the announcement this evening with a list of games and point folks here so they can make game requests. Friday, if we have more than 3 or 4 games, I'll post a poll that'll run 'till the following Thursday (give us time to set up dedicated servers) and we'll run the top 4 winners. This will, in no way, prevent folks from bringing a game they like and hosting a game on their machine for interested parties. Let me know what you think of the plan.

I'd also like the folks to check out the offerings from GarageGames. Most run on Linux, have free demos and some sort of LAN play. They are a bit of a departure from the standard run and gun, and I thought it'd be a nice change of pace for some.

In case you haven't heard of GG, this is a company started by a couple of ex-Dynamix folks. They continue the development of the Torque engine that powered Tribes 2. They sell this very capable engine at $100 a developer (*mega* cheap for a commercial game engine) and have built a community of independent and hobbiest game makers. Lots of tips, code, and other support for the budding hacker hoping to start the next Id to the geek just messing around late at night. Also, should your game, once completed, show promise, GG will partner with you as publisher to help you get your game to the masses. Lots of neat (and not-so-neat) projects going on over there. Take some time and browse around the "Make Games" section if you have a few.
