With an x86 port of Apple, I think it would invite Linux users to try Apple's OS X, and increase the number of people who dual boot between Linux and Apple. I think it is early to guess what the move will do.  Switching to Intel has become a necessity because the PowerPC is just not going to keep up with Intel going forward.  If they don't make the switch, Apple could simply lose its ability to compete at all.

It isn't about taking market share from Microsoft, if market share is considered at all; it's about taking market share from everyone.

OS Xi will only run on Apple produced hardware.  I understand the Windows will run on the current test hardware available from Apple because it is fairly standard.

I find it interesting that two years ago, Steve Jobs touted the PowerPC, especially Altivec and touted that the PPC was much faster than Intel's processors.  For some things, it was and for other things it simply was not.

Ruggy, I am disappointed in you.  Why would anyone buy an Apple over building your own and running Linux?  You answer your own question, OS X.  Emulation will be easier as many developers have weighed in on because of Endian issues.  We could see a much better version of VirtualPC or even VMWare.

Getting back to why it could pull Linux users.  In my case, I wanted a UNIX based system with a decent user interface, MS Windows, and decent games.  Going forward, I am hopeful that Intel's ability to supply Apple with not just quality M chips, but a LOT OF THEM, allowing Apple to drop prices, increase speed, and reduce power consumption.  Not all Linux users and/or developers want to build their own boxes or recompile something to get sound working on their laptops new BIOS-less laptops.

I also don't think that this move will kill Linux by any stretch of the imagination.  There are folks like yourself that won't or don't want to leave it.  Those that don't understand why folks like myself pay Apple for their OS.  (Check out opendarwin.org when you get a free moment.)  Those that love to tweak that last little thing that you just can't do under OS X.

For example, I tried OS X Server on a dual G5 for about 4-5 months in my consulting biz.  At the end of the journey, I put OS X on the dual G5 and use it for graphics and development work, ordered a Dell Server and installed RHEL4.  Dual G5+OSX Server?  Close to $3k.  Single CPU Dell server that more than handles the load at this point plus RHEL 4? Close to $500 and is much more flexible.

Anyways, just my two cents.  Have fun folks.
