Quote from: dpeach on October 26, 2008, 08:45:49 pm
What do you mean "prepared for an interview?" I have not had a job interview for 18 years. What would you have to do to prepare? As far as IT jobs, what kind of questions would you be asked that might be a surprise to someone? I know an interview is not like a test that you can study for, but if someone did get an interview, is there anything they can do to get ready for it?

What I mean are mostly simple, common-sense things, but stuff that people *frequently* neglect to do.  Things like:  Is your suit clean, pressed, and ready to wear?  Proper belt, tie, shoes ready to go?  How recent is your haircut?  Do you know exactly how to get to the interview site, and how long it takes to get there? 

As for the interview content, yes there are things to think about there as well.  A major benefit to you as an applicant is to be able to demonstrate to the interviewer that you know something about the company/business you are applying for.  Things such as how many employees they have, the general history of the company such as when it was founded, how much it's grown, and even future expansion possibilities.  Other tidbits which might be helpful are things like how "green" the company is, and similar things.  These all may seem trivial when discussed here in this forum, but trust me, the interviewer will be impressed if you walk in there knowing something.  It shows that you are interested in the company, and have done some preparation for the interview.  That can often be the difference-maker between two equally qualified candidates.  In the specific example of IT-related jobs, I'm sure there are many things which could be brushed up on by the candidate, especially if by doing some research about the company first, you see a few things that the company does that you might be rusty on (such as a previously used programming language, or a hardware architecture that you've not used in a while).  It can make all the difference in the world as to your chances for getting the job.
