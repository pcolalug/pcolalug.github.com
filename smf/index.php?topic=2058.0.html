The X700 is set to be supported in upcoming releases of the binary driver. What this means is that you should still be able to use it; however, you won't have hardware 3d right away.

I've said it before about the ATI mobility chipsets, but ATI is not the one who produces the drivers for them. ATI leaves that up to the manufacturer of the mobility product (i.e. the laptop manufacturer) due to the nature of the modifications that are done to those products. This leaves the burden on those manufacturers such as HP and Dell to product Linux drivers for those mobility products. Since they rarely keep up on the Windows drivers, I don't think you can expect much love from them in the Linux world.
