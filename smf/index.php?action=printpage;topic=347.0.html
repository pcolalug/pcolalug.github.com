I did a lot of research on the web before making my purchase.  And I couldn't be happier.  The nvidia GeForce 3 Ti 500 64mb AGP.  (I think you can get it 128mb also if you want).  Best performance for the price, outperforms most of the GeForce 4 cards.  I got mine for less than $100 with shipping, so shop around and you're sure to find one.  This was a huge performance boost over my previous card, a Matrox Millenium G400 Dual Head 32mb AGP.

Post by: rugmonster on January 03, 2003, 09:40:53 am
Quote from: dpeach
I know that the MX200 is a PCI card (at least the ones I have seen). I have an AGP slot. Am I shorting myself by looking at a PCI card when I have AGP available to me?


No and Yes. Every card since the TNT2 has been primarily available as an AGP card. I am having a harder time finding a GF2 in PCI than anything. And yes, you will be shorting yourself in getting a PCI when you have AGP available. You will also be shorting yourself by getting any MX card. They are better than my TNT2, but it definitely isn't great by any measure. Here (http://www6.tomshardware.com/graphic/20021218/vgacharts-06.html) is a benchmark comparison of most all modern video cards. Those are benchmarks on high end systems, but there is an update coming for slower systems soon.

Quote from: imnes
The Ti are the way to go. A GeForce 3 Ti will outperform a GeForce 4 MX. (A GeForce 4 MX from what I've read is an overclocked GeForce 2 card). If you can afford a GeForce 4 Ti that might be a good way to go too, but last I checked they were a few hundred dollars for the nice ones.


Yes all around. The TI is the newer convention of naming. They originally only had the Geforce3, but they did some tweaking with the clock speeds and released two different models, one better and one worse, but not as bad as an MX. The only differences between the different models within the same chipset (ex Geforce 4 TI, Geforce3 and Geforce3 TI), with the exception of the GF4 MX which is really a overclocked Geforce 2 chipset, is the clockspeed of the GPU and the memory.

As far as buying a new video card, I would wait until the next Nvidia chipset comes out, the GeforceFX. Then it will be affordable to buy a Geforce 4 TI 4600. Right now, you can get a GF4 TI 4200 64MB for a little over $120 (check pricewatch), especially if you don't want Video In/Out, TV-Out and 8x AGP. Those with older motherboards won't be able to take advantage of 8X AGP anyhow, so it isn't drastically needed. I am expecting the GeforceFX to come out in the typical spring release schedule that Nvidia likes to keep, but prices will continue to drop until then.
