I use Ext2 and Ext3. I'm not hugely concerned with the speed increase that the other jfs's give. It isn't enough to really matter to me really. Ext3 is very stable for me.

Also, XFS and ReiserFS are good for different things. I can't remember which, but one is fast with small files and actions where the other is good with large files and actions. There is a trade off with each. I think you have to judge what you will be dealing with and make the decision based on your needs. I just like Ext3 because should I need to, I can use a bootdisk with Ext2 support and access my files without worrying about too much funkiness.

For your reading pleasure, of course:

Ext2 - Nothing special here.  We all know what this one is!  Nice and rather solid, but absolutely no journalling support.

Ext3 - The sucessor to Ext3.  Almost identical to ext2, save for metadeta journalling and ordered block writing, effectively giving it the capabilities of a journalling fs.

XFS - SGI's offering to the world of journalling filesystems; I've had some nasty experiences with this one.  Had a brown-out here while compiling something rather large.. System comes back up, my entire /home filesystem is poofed.  That's just my experience with it, though.. XFS is, however, supposed to be good at handling and moving large amounts of data.

ReiserFS - I used to run this for a while;  seems to handle small blocksizes better than ext2/3 by a rather noticeable difference, but has a tendency to choke when doing large, sequential reads and writes.

JFS - This is IBM's offering, if I remember correctly.  Does anyone have any experiences with this?

//crowe
