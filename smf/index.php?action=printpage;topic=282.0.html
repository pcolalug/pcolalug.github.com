Title: PC Inspector File Recovery
Post by: dpeach on December 04, 2002, 01:33:34 am
I had a drive go wierd this week. After several tests, I found out that the partition table was corrupt or missing. I needed to restore the partition table, or pull the data off before formatting. Since getting the partition table restored seemed to be impossible (based on two opinions that I respect), then getting the data off was my next choice.

Newton found this utility (http://www.pcinspector.de/file_recovery/UK/welcome.htm). It works in Windows (I am sure there is something like it in Linux).  This tool was able to see the drive though it was not "mountable." I then went in and pulled files off to my secondary hard drive. Since my drive was in tact but not bootable/mountable, I was able to get my files. If the drive has been fdisked, but not reformatted, they say you can still get the files off.

From what I could tell, it scans your drive for boot records/partition information, even though the partition table was not readable by the system. Then it builds it's own partition table (but does not restore the damaged drive). The purpose of the software is to just be able to get to your data. You cannot do any type of repair on the drive with this utility.

Saved my hide, I thought I would share.
