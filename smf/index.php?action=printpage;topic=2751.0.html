Title: REVIEW: Zalman VF700-CU VGA Cooler
Post by: rugmonster on January 02, 2006, 03:13:23 pm
Zalman VF700-CU VGA Cooler

(http://images10.newegg.com/NeweggImage/productimage/35-118-117-01.JPG)

I recently purchased the Zalman VF700-CU VGA Cooler from Newegg to replace the failing stock cooler on my Sapphire ATI 9800XT.  The stock cooler's fan was dying.  It made the commonly well known buzz, spun too slowly and worst of all, was making my video card unstable.  I was seeing temps of 98C under load.

Included in the package was the cooler, eight memory heat sinks, power adapter, thermal paste, screws and mounting bracket. 

(http://images10.newegg.com/NeweggImage/productimage/35-118-117-02.JPG)(http://images10.newegg.com/NeweggImage/productimage/35-118-117-05.JPG)

Installation went vary smoothly.  I wish I had taken pictures, but was being rude enough to the guests that were over.  Basically, you remove the old cooler by unscrewing the mounting screws and with a slight twist and a pull it pops off.  One thing that you will notice is the original thermal grease.  My GPU was covered with some old, nasty stuff.  To take care of the old thermal compound, I got the ArcticClean Thermal Material Remover and Surface Purifier.

(http://images10.newegg.com/NeweggImage/productimage/35-100-010-01.JPG)

Applying a few drops of the cleaner to the GPU and letting it sit for about a minute, I was able to wipe away all remnants of the old thermal grease.  I repeated with the cleaner on the memory as well as the bottom on the new heatsink.  On all of the metal surfaces, I applied the purifier part of the ArctiClean set, which is supposed to prep the surface for the thermal compound.

One note on the installation, I mounted the cooler prior to applying the memory heatsinks.  Yeah, that doesn't work too well since the fins on the heatsink cover a memory module on my card.  The heatsinks have an adhesive thermal pad on them and go on by applying pressure to them.

Rather than using included thermal compound, I used ArcticSilver 5.  You can't use very much of this stuff or else it actually acts as an insulator, which is baaaaad.  Mounting went very easily.  The included instructions were very straight forward.  To aid in removing the hot air coming off of the cooler, I put in an exhaust fan in a PCI slot below the video card. Some pictures...

(http://img262.imageshack.us/img262/4546/img16645pp.jpg)

(http://img288.imageshack.us/img288/5903/img16656lh.jpg)

What were the results?  Before, I was at 98C when I played Battlefield 2.  Now, without overclocking, I run at about 46C. Overclocked by ~10Mhz on GPU and memory each, I run about 48C.  I'm extremely pleased.  The other cool thing is that as the ArcticSilver bonds more with the cooler and GPU, I should see cooler temps.  To top everything off, I think my system is actually quieter now without the bad fan even though I have two more fans in the box.  Overall, I'm extremely pleased.
