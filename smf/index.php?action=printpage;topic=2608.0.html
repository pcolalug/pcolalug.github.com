I disagree that Linux will be the MS killer. The more I have been playing with and talking to people about OS X, the more I think that is the next big thing. The distribution doesn't matter. It's the kernel that matters and last time I checked, you can put whatever kernel you want on whatever distribution you care to run. If the kernel supports it for one distribution, then it will support it in every single other one.

I do agree that shopping for support is a good idea, but the same goes for Mac, Windows, DOS, etc. My point is that it has gotten to a point that you don't have to worry about hardware support as much as you did a couple of years ago.

I will say that my Radeon 9800 works extremely well with Linux. Have you seen the new installer that works almost exactly like the Nvidia installer? As long as you have the kernel headers installed and the typical build utils, you're good to go, exactly the same thing nvidia's drivers require. I haven't seen a sound card not work in recent times and almost all NICs are supported. Even the SATA controllers are getting supported.
