I have noticed that there is no shortcut to a good Gentoo installation, at least not on a fresh configuration. Vida Linux was one such shortcut that failed me.

The best way to install Gentoo is to get a good stage 2 or stage 3 (that's debatable), tweak it to your liking, then tar the whole thing into a backup. Then, if you ever have to re-install Gentoo, just scp the entire tar file to the computer, and unpack it.

18  Misc / Other Misc / Re: The "ONE" Question on: February 09, 2006, 09:53:03 am
I retract my disagreement, not on the basis of the car versus airplane line of thought, but on the basis of what is being assumed. The intent of the wording is to get people to realize that an airplane does not behave as a car, but we are forced to make assumptions about the magical conveyor belt-- that it can detect the speed of anything rolling on it and match it without breaking down. If this were rephrased to say the airplane were on a frictionless surface, then it would be easy to see that the airplane would take off-- it would just slide down the surface until it reached takeoff speed.

With the introduction of the magical conveyor belt, we are forced to assume that the belt can accelerate to inifinite speed, that the belt can accelerate before the plane can roll off, etc.

The simplest solution, without playing "what if that" and "what if this" is that the plane will take off.
19  Misc / Other Misc / Re: The "ONE" Question on: February 08, 2006, 10:12:08 pm
I still disagree. Wheels, by definition have little friction, mu, but they have rolling friction, mu sub r. This is the nature of wheels. If the plane can not roll to the point where air is rushing past the wings, it can not take off. Only V/STOL aircraft take off without air rushing past the wings. Since we are not told this is a V/STOL aircraft, or that the aircraft has 80 kips thrust per turbine, we can only assume that the aircraft will sit in place with its turbines at full blast until all its fuel is consumed.

There is no tranmission or drive axle in the airplane wheels, so anytime the engines generate thrust to propel the aircraft forward, the "conveyor belt runway" will oppose any forward motion of the aircraft. Thus, the aircraft can never propel itself to its takeoff velocity ~100kts.

My answer may be wrong, but it is the best I can do with the information I have. In order for the plane to take off, I would need to make assumptions that are against my nature to assume. I still think the plane will not take off.

I disagree. True, the wheels on the conveyor belt have no effect per se. However, if there is no wind across the wings, the plane will not take off. A commericial aircraft's turbines can not generate enough thrust to takeoff from a stand still. The airplane does not need to roll in order to generate speed; it rolls in order to generate differential air pressure between the cambered edge of the wing, and the relatively flat edge, thus creating lift.
