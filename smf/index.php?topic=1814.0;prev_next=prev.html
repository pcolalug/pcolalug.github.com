Thanks to Dan, danc, and David, dpeach, for the kind words concerning the IRC ssh workshop.  I actually had to stop myself after we hit the hour and a half mark.  There are pro's and con's to anything like that.

I found it easier to deal with various levels of experience by going one on one in private message rooms and I could multitask between them much easier.

On the downside, if you have to leave your computer to go do something else as it is much easier to get distracted at home, you can become quickly behind.  If you are in a classroom, it is probably easier to focus.  There is nothing you can do about home distractions, but if you decide that this delivery method is appealing to you and you give an IRC class, please eliminate those distractions.  It's no biggie if you are attending and something pulls you away, but it could be if you are giving the class.

Secondly, I should have been more organized with my material and provided visual aids and diagrams to follow along with prior to the class.  In this case, we could have used some of the diagrams from a previous meeting talk I gave.  For this particular session, I picked four things, typed up some exercises and played it by ear.

Also, I had technical difficulties connecting my server/laptop, including losing my Internet connection shortly after 9.  Not much y ou can do about that.

 I agree that this should not replace live talks; however, I think this method allows for more frequent and impromptu help or training.  I encourage every member of the group and especially the existing/incoming steering committee members to push IRC usage.  If if you just lurk, you might learn from just "listening" in.

Moving forward, for the next year, I would like this to be my contribution to the group.  I realize that I use the other UNIX, but we can be useful members of society.  Really.  Honestly.  Until January, I am fairly slammed, but I will do my best to help out pushing this style of training.

If you are a slow typer, don't sweat it.  If there had been questions after the class, I would have hung out for them and I offered to answer questions in the future on it  -- of course, everyone hanging in the IRC channels is helping as it is!

I recommend reading a bit on IRC at:

http://www.irchelp.org/irchelp/irctutorial.html
http://freenode.net/using_the_network.shtml

We are on irc.freenode.net in #pcolalug.


Finally, are there any further thoughts on what the membership would like to see in the way of tutorials...?  So far I have,

1.  Passwordless synchronization of files between boxes.  (rsync over ssh)
2.  Something samba related.
