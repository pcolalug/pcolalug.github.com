While I agree the games are important, the name does play a part. To me, Nintendo has struggled in a good naming convention. NES, SNES, Nintendo 64, Game Cube and now Wii. Even though it is boring, at least Sony has at least stayed consistant with the PlayStation line. Though, they can be confusing too with the abbreviations: PlayStation (PSX), PlayStation One (PS1), PlayStation 2, PlayStation Two (PS2), PlayStation 3.

But, the game console is all about the games. Like computers, you buy the one that runs the software you want to use. For me, Sony has me in their back pocket with the backwards compatability between PSX/PS1 and PlayStation 2/PS2. I did not buy my PSX until PlayStation 2 was already on the market. But I bought knowing that I would be able to continue to use my games from the original to the next generation of the console.

But yeah, Wii is a pretty stupid name. I think they ought to stick with the name Revolution. That is a good name and with MS choosing 360 as their console name, maybe Nintendo can use the similarity (of meaning) to their favor. They did have the name first. Though, it would probably always be looked at as Nintendo trying to play catch up. Though they seem to be ambivilous to MS. They have their market and it is not the same as Sony and MS market.
