Mediacom provides you with a modem and you pay a monthly rental fee for it.  (At least that's the way it was for me).  This worked out better than buying it because they had to replace mine several times.  I think their recent model of modem is finally being traded out, something about the power supply connection being faulty and causing the modem to spontaneously reset / lockup.  

You always have the option of buying your own.  I think people have had good luck with the black Toshiba modem that was offered with cox high speed internet.  

Assuming you have the choice, go with an ethernet based (not USB) modem and linux won't be an issue, the modem will just work through your ethernet port.

Most cable companies will have a list of cable modems that will work with their service. I think the industry standard is DOCSIS 1.0, with 1.1 on the way. My suggestion is to get a modem with the standard RJ-45 Ethernet connectivity, opposed to a USB one. Ethernet will make your life much easier as it is natively supported extremely well in Linux...no matter the distro. I have noticed in the past that different distros use different DHCP clients and not all of them work the same. DHCPCD is what I use with Cox and it works great, but if I want to use PUMP, I can't pull an IP. When I was on RoadRunner in Houston, I had to use PUMP instead of DHCPCD. I don't know why that is, but if you get your system setup and you try to pull an IP with a dhcp client and don't get one, try another one. Beyond that, let us know if we can help you out with anything more specific.

I'll have to agree with Rugmonster on this one -- go with a cable modem that has a standard Ethernet connection, rather than a USB one.  Not only is ethernet better supported under Linux, but it's quite a bit more versatile should you wish to reconfigure your network's layout later on down the road.

And just FYI, I'm also using a Toshiba cable modem, though mine is the older PCX1100U.  All the same, since I've never had a problem with the modem itself for as long as I've owned it.
