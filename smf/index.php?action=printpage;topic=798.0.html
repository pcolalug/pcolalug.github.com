The problem as I see it is that most of our members aren't gamers. We therefor have a small population to draw LAN participants from. The conclusion would be that to pull this off "in-group", so to speak, would be to limit the planned games to one or two and make sure everyone is playing the same thing at the same time. Also, I think many Linux users either don't have the money and/or interest in paying for and playing games. Their computer is entertaining enough in-and-of itself. Also, you find many folks have Linux installed on an older machine that just doesn't have the power to play many modern games.
