Title: Which phone / ISP should I use?
Post by: rugmonster on June 06, 2005, 05:09:49 am
Most residential ISP's will "reserve the right to monitor you activity" when in fact they rarely ever do unless there is a need. Keep in mind that every P2P software has a legitimate use somewhere in it. On that same token, most residential internet service will have a no server clause. This is just to make the majority of people who want to run a server buy a business account.

In the six year I've had cable internet from Mediacom, Roadrunner, Cox and now Knology, they all had monitoring and no-server clauses. Do I feel like they've looked into what I've downloaded or what servers (SSH/HTTPS/FTP) I'm running? Not at all. As long as you are downloading kiddie porn and giving law enforcement some reason to look into your activities, monitoring is going to be a non-issue. If you are so concerned about monitoring (which is going to happen to you anywhere a server keeps logs), then use some anonymizing software.

So really, it comes down to what's the best bang for the buck. I recommend cable internet and then get a Voice over IP phone. Doesn't matter from who as long as you aren't going to pay more than $25/month before taxes and at that price you should be getting unlimited long distance. Total internet/phone cost should be no more than $75/month. HTHs

Title: Which phone / ISP should I use?
Post by: rugmonster on June 16, 2005, 05:52:31 am
Yahoo DSL is for SBC customers (that's Southwestern Bell). NW Florida's baby bell is BellSouth. Product, prices, etc will more than likely be nearly the same as they are probably very similar products.

But why get a landline? You are paying so much more than you need to. For what you are going to spend on local and long distance phone service and DSL, you could get cable with voip and a cell phone. Plus, with the voip, you will get unlimited long distance. To get that through a traditional provider is usually at least $50 a month. Let's break that down...

Plain Jane Phone Line, no long distance... $30/month after taxes
DSL Service... $42.95 for 1.5M Down/256k Up from Bellsouth (plus taxes) (also think about the year or two contract)
Unlimited Long Distance through BellSouth...$24.99 (plus taxes)

Total for Landline = ~$100/month after taxes

Or, you can go with cable...
Cable Internet...$40 for somewhere between 2 and 4Mbps down/256k to 384k up. Cox even has a plan for $55 for a 5/768 plan. I had that in OKC and I believe it is available in Pensacola. God was that fast.
VoIP Service...$27 unlimited local and long distance anywhere you plug in the VoIP box. Plus Canada and Mexico (so you can call dpeach).

Total for Cable/VoIP = ~$70

$30 a month is $360 a year. To me, that's money that could have been spent another way.

But, it's up to you how you want to do it.
