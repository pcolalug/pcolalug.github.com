compiling you did do
Code:

make bzImage && make modules && make modules_install

correct?

after that you just either copy or move the System.map to /boot
then find your bzImage which should be in ./arch/i386/boot
just move it to /boot as whatever u wanna call it or vmlinuz if u dont want to change your /etc/lilo.conf
then run lilo command and ur ready to reboot.....if it fails with those steps then something might be wrong with the .config for your kernel

This is just my own observation, might be completely wrong...

By default the 2.6 kernels seem to disable everything and only enable by default drivers it detects you are currently using.  (And even those it defaults to compiling into the kernel, not as a module).  If you want all the modules you had under 2.4 kernel to be available, you need to copy the config from your old kernel to the new kernel source directory.  I think just something like:

cp /boot/config-ide.2.4.22 /usr/src/linux-2.6.4/.config

Then do your normal config and make routines.  Be sure to go and check all kernel options because some of the options may not carry directly over, and you'll need to configure alsa / oss sound yourself.

This makes sense if you look at your kernel config above, almost nothing is set to compile as a module.  

If you want something to work from, here's my .config for kernel 2.6.4 - at a minimum you'll need to alter the CPU type and sound stuff to match yours.  Otherwise I've left it fairly stock.  My sound card setup is ALSA Intel i810 audio, and cpu type is Pentium 4 with SMB enabled (because I have a hyperthreading processor I needed it).

I was gonna copy->paste but it's too big, here's a link to my config file.  
http://homeweb.org/~imnes/tmp/config-2.6.4

Note: compiling kernel 2.6 with all these modules / options enable takes a long long time.  Good luck.

Not sure it's related to your problem, but I always do NOT symlink the kernel, at least until things are working...

Copy the newly compiled bzImage to /boot/vmlinuz-2.6.1 and the System.map to /boot/System.map-2.6.1.

Then change the System.map symlink to point to this new one, and reference the /boot/vmlinuz-2.6.1 specifically in your bootloader config.
