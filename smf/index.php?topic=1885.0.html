I'm glad to know that some of the comforts are coming back to all of you so affected by the storm. Everytime I come back from a deployment, I realize what I have taken for granted such as hot showers, clean clothes, cable internet, air conditioning and reliable power. I'm sure everyone is having a similar experience now. If there is anything those of us outside the area can do to help, please let us know.

It seems that most of my friends and family came through the storm fine with minimal damage. With the exception of some flooding, which flood insurance will cover, there isn't much that won't take long to fix if it hasn't already been done. I've noticed the forums over at Pensacola News Journal's site have been a great resource for sharing information. I suggest you check it out if you are looking for something.

Best of luck and I hope to hear everyone is doing fine. It seems cable is coming back up now and I think we should start hearing from more folks. As a side note, my former employer, Escambia River Electric Cooperative, runs a dial up ISP. If you know someone who has power and phones, but no cable, they can get an account with them for $17.95 a month with the first month free. You can get their number from the EREC site.
