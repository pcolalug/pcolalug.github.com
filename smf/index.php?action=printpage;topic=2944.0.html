Title: Vyatta, the next thing in open source routers
Post by: rugmonster on July 25, 2006, 06:58:22 pm
Slashdot had an article linked about the new Vyatta open source router. I decided to check out their website and find out the specifics.

Basically, they are a commercial entity utilizing Linux to produce an open source based router operating system capable of running on commodity x86 hardware. They offer commercial support for corporate use. In the article slashdot linked (http://searchopensource.techtarget.com/qna/0,289202,sid39_gci1204142,00.html), with the currently available hardware, Vyatta can replace most midrange routers. For comparison, they cite the Cisco 2800 series to 7200 series. Considering the routers I've seen and what they are typically used for, I would agree with this statement, especially if they are basing their product off of the Linux kernel.

Here's what I found on the tech side.

In case you are in the need of a router, rather than paying a couple of grand for a low end router, look for a spare system that you could use with Vyatta. With the addition of a T1 card, you can have full WAN capabilities, or an ethernet card connected to a DSL or Cable modem, you can have a full WAN access ready router.
