Title: Interesting Palm m130 article
Post by: dpeach on August 19, 2002, 01:43:20 pm
Here is an article (http://rss.com.com/2100-1040-954357.html?type=pt&part=rss&tag=feed&subj=news) about the new Palm m130. Not too new. It came out in March I believe. They advertised it as a 16 bit color device. Come to find out it is only 12 bit.

How could they not know it was not 16 bit? They made it didn't they? I suspect that they knew all along, especially since they have already built a couple of others that are 16 bit. I think that they wanted to get a low end color device on the market without telling where they cut corners.

It bothers me that they were dishonest enough to do this in the first place. But the kicker is that they are not going to refund any of the purchase price, nor will they take them back in trade. Their comment is, "we're sorry." That's it.

Something is wrong here.

Their stock price is already down to $0.71. They have been a failing company for some time. This should do them in. Unfortunately, as we have seen in business recently (Enron, Worldcom, etc.) a company does not have to be honest to stay afloat. But one day it will catch up to them.

I don't dislike Palm necessarily. I have never owned one of their devices. I use Handspring products. But I don't dislike them. I do dislike an industry leader playing unfair and probably getting away with it...for now.

Handspring, by the way, reported their color device as only being 12 bit. This was back when 16 bit was already the standard.
