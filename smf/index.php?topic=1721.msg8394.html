Where to start...

I started using Linux around '98 or so.  My first experience was Red Hat.  Went that direction due to being fed up with the high price of M$ and the lack of stability.  I once spent a week trying to install Win98SE on machine after I wiped the hard drive of...Win98SE!  Not to mention the user manual seemed to get less and less with each new release.  I have tried Linux Mandrake, Slackware, and Fedora.  

Oddly enough the Unix I spent the most time with is FreeBSD.  I really liked that one.  Was with the Beastie for ~3 years.  Then I needed something that just worked out of the box due to demands on my time.  I also needed something that wouldn't scare my wife away.  So I am now on Mandrake 10.  I was shocked at how much it configures for you out of the box.  Very impressed how far Linux has come since I went to the beasite.

Currently I am attending graduate school after a ten year stint in the Navy.  One of the older students and the only one with two kids.  Been here for two years.  Currently I am in the process of getting ready for my Ph.D. qualifying exam.  Either pass that one or get a real job.  So not a lot of free time for me.  Whatever the outcome I am glad I came to school here.

I use Linux at work and at home.  I like the idea that I don't touch a windows machine for very long periods of time.  I actually have a hard time using one now.  I bet that sounds strange to most people.  Why do people who know I use Linux think I can help them with their Windows problems?

I am in the process of taking a crash course in C for a programming project at the university.  I have programmed in C++, and  MS BASIC.  I do real basic web pages with HTML.  By basic I mean it will render well in a text mode browser.  Old fashioned?  Most of the time I just use linux for desktop type stuff...mp3's, web browsing, e-mail, etc.  At work it is programming, data manipulation, LaTex, etc.  I work in the physics group.  We use a large amount of custom software for our needs, and we do some serious number crunching.

Not sure if I will ever make a meeting due to the drive.  However, you never know.  It would be nice to not be the only linux guy in the room for a change.

Wow I can't believe I wrote this much!
