In case you haven't seen, it's confirmed and AMD and ATi are merging.

ATi's motherboards and chipsets are already aimed primarily at AMD processors, so I'm a little confused as to how that can get any better. There are no compatibility issues that I know of between the two. Also, AMD doesn't make motherboards. ATi does. I think we will now see the highest performing motherboards for AMD processors coming from ATi, thus pushing sales of crossfire enabled motherboards.

Additionally, I would hope that AMD will push ATi to produce better drivers for their hardware, not only for Linux, but for Windows as well. I have always felt that all of the drivers that ATi has put out recently have sucked when compared to NVIDIA's on all platforms. Since AMD is a hardware manufacturer, I don't know if this will happen. ATi's graphics cards may benefit from better manufacturing, but that hardware can only go so fast with crappy drivers.
