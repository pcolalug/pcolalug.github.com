The Furniture Story....Don't try this at home!
« on: August 26, 2004, 03:51:29 pm »
We moved to México about a month ago. That's Old México. The real one. The one with the accent over the "e".

We took this opportunity to procure new furniture. Most of what we had was acquired over our 11 years of marriage as hand-me-downs, Goodwill store specials, or collected on trash day from peoples front yards. I love trash day.

For the first two weeks here we basically had no furniture. We used the boxes that we packed in and the folding chairs that we bought at Sam's for all our furniture needs. Yes, we have a Sam's here.

Last Thursday we bought our first "soft" furniture. It was a love seat and chair to go into our "Family" room. This is the room that houses the TV, PlayStation 2, and MythBox. You know, the room where we spend 90% of our free time. We have a formal living room that is still empty exept for the box that the telephone sits on.

The only problem with the family room is that to get the furniture in we have to pass through 2 doors that are only 24" wide each. There is another door at the end of the 26" wide hallway that only opens 25". In other words, there ain't no room to get the furniture in. We had suspected that it would be tight, but were not sure how tight till the furniture arrived on Friday. It would not fit at all. We even removed the legs in a futile attempt. Not even close.

There were three options. One was to decide that the furniture would not go in and put it into another room. Not really a good choice. Second was to take out a window that is sufficiently large to pass the furniture into. The third was to tear down the wall that separates that room from the narrow hallway.

The window idea was doable, but invloved actually removing the entire window, not just opening it. In and of itself, I didn't think it would be that hard. But, there are iron bars that go across the window. They are cemented into the wall. Which then made that not a good option. Idea three was to remove the hallway wall. That is the reason the back door does not open any wider, plus we would surely not have any problem with getting the furniture in if the wall were completely gone.

Did I mention that this is a rental house?

There were some obstacles to the wall idea, but that is the one that won out. The wall is constructed out of drywall/sheetrock. The only one I have ever seen in the whole country! Not being sure that I could find sheetrock to replace this with, we decided to pull the wall down delicately and reuse all the materials possible.

We tore the wall down on Friday afternoon. I made a quick trip (that is just an expression since nothing in México is quick) to Home Depot. Yes, we have one of those too, it has opened since we moved here. The purpose of the trip to Home Depot was to see if I could indeed procure the tape and mud that I would need to rebuild the wall. Fortunately they had what I needed. They even had a pretty good selection of drywall. I am not sure who they are going to sell that to.

The furniture now needed to come in. The main door to the hall is not useable since that is one of the 24" doors that we had to go through. The back door to the house will open to 36" with the wall out of the way.

Did I mention that this was on the second floor? The back door leads out to the balcony and is the access to my office. But there is no ground access here. We had to tie ropes around the new furniture and hoist it up to the second floor and then into the back door and into it's final resting place.

Saturday was spent in reconstruction. On Monday and Tuesday I finished texturing the wall. The other three walls in the room are concrete of course. They have a pretty heavy stucco texture on them. My texture is not that heavy, but it is better than what the wall looked like before.  Before, there was no texture on the wall. There was not a single seam where the tape was applied properly. One of the seams had some kind of brown contact cement holding it on. Of course it was not all under the tape, it was smeared all around the seam. The wall was really bad before, that is why my heart was not broken about redoing it.

Now all we are waiting on is for my wife to decide if she wants to keep the room the nice pistachio green color, or change it to something more to her liking.

What happens when we want to move out of the house and the furniture is going with us? Maybe by that time I will have learned how to work with concrete and we will be tearing out a window!
