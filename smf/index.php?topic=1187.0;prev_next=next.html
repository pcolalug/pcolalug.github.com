I don't think you need to worry about having a paperweight...  I've found the various camera tool applications to be not so great, at least for my camera (a Sony).  The easiest thing for me (and I think many others) has been to buy a small USB "card reader" for transferring the images from camera to computer.  These can be gotten for the various camera media, like Memory-Stick, Compact-Flash, etc..., and cost about $20.  You just attach the reader to a USB port, insert the camera media, and move/copy the images to the computer.  Plus this method doesn't use up the camera batteries.  Very simple and works great.  Let me know if you need any more info.

I talked with Chuck on the phone, and we got his card reader going.  Thought I'd post here how we did it in case others might find the info useful...

1.  As root, make a directory in /mnt, such as "/mnt/camera"
2.  Plug the card reader (with media inserted) into a USB port
3.  As root, try the command:  mount -t vfat /dev/sda1 /mnt/camera
4.  You should get no error messages.  Try to change directory (cd /mnt/camera) to the card reader, and try copying some files to/from it.
5.  If this all works, the next step is to make it easier to do the next time you want to use it (and to allow users other than root to do it).  To do this, continue to step 6.

6.  As root, add the following line to your /etc/fstab file.  I'm not totally sure if it matters where you add it, but I put mine after all other drives/partitions and before the /proc and swap entries at the end.  Here's the line to add:
Code:
/dev/sda1 /mnt/camera vfat iocharset=iso8859-1,codepage=850,umask=0,noauto,user,0 0

7.  After a reboot, you should be able to use your card reader by plugging it in and typing the command:  "mount /mnt/camera".

I know this works on Redhat 9 and Mandrake 9.1.  Hopefully it's helpful to some others.  

Dan

A little late to the party, but I would recommend the same thing. Get you a card reader. They transfer the data much faster than the camera. Or, at least in my case, it does. If you have a serial based camera, then you definitely want a USB card reader. You will be amazed at the speed.

I had my card reader working just fine with my Mandrake box. It recognized it as soon as I plugged it in and created a mount point for me (IIRC).

The only problem that I had was that after reading a card with it, everytime I booted up after that it required that the card be in the card reader. Otherwise it would not boot. There is a patch to the kernel for this. I solved the problem without recompiling my kernel. I don't use that card reader much on that computer, so I just unplug it after use and all works fine.

All in all, I would definitely recommend the card reader. I have one from Kodak that reads CF, MMC/SD and SM. I have devices that take all of the forms, so one reader covered all of them for me. I got it at Wal-Mart for about $30.
