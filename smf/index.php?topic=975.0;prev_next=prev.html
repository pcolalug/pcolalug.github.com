The difference in the AMD 64's as I see it are:

Opteron - aimed directly at server systems.  Can be run in multi-CPU configurations.  I think they're advertised with clock speeds around 1-1.4ghz.

The Athlon64 is not even pin-compatible withe the opteron, maybe less cache.  Is sold as a 3200 (I guess that's around a 3ghz).

The Athlon64 FX is for more graphics intensive users, 'Cinematic Computing' according to AMD.  I understand this is pin-compatible with the opteron and offered in multi-cpu configurations.

Of course all of this could be wrong just what I've picked up reading posts / reviews.

Price-wise the Athlon64 is around $400, the Athlon64FX is around $750, the Opteron I'm not sure because it's marketed with different speed ratings, but I think it's somewhere around $500 a chip.

At around $400 (maybe down to $300 by the time I buy?)  The Athlon64 is doable but I would like the option of running dual CPUs, and I think with the the regular Athlon64 it won't happen

Maybe someone else can correct me / fill in the gaps here...
