got some details about the lugfest schedule:

Quote

Hi Lloyd,

Great to hear from you!  I will try to answer your questions the best I
can.  I am guessing you read the schedule on the alabamalugfest.org
website.  The workshops and exhibitions are a time for us to do things
besides sit and listen to a lecture.  I will be giving a workshop on LTSP,
Jerry Carter will be giving a workshop on SAMBA, and Leroy will be giving
a presentation to small business owners on the business reasons to switch
to Linux.  Vendors will be set up in the mezzanine area promoting their
stuff.  
Lugwars is a tradition that dates back to the first Alabama LUGFest.  Each
LUG gets to choose 4-5 people and 1 leader (usually their pres) to battle
other LUGs in computer and general nerdiness trivia.  The winning LUG wins
the prize (TBA).
The VIP dinner with Jon Hall is by reservation.  We are still setting this
up, and I will make an announcement when it is ready.  The website will
also have this info, so keep watching it.
Sorry, no group discounts.  This hotel and the amenities are costing a
fortune.  We had to have 2 hardware auctions just to get started...

-Rick

 Hi Lloyd,

Things have finally gelled for the upcoming LUGFest 2005 here in Mobile.

I had been forwarded your comments and questions regarding this event from Rick Green, our PR Officer.

We have some exciting and informative guest speakers, and a day full of interesting exhibits, workshops, and events.

Highlights:

    * maddog Jon Hall to speak on the past, present, and future of Open Source Technology- he should know...
    * An update on Federal terror and cyber-crime busting by Special Agent Mike Eubanks
    * Management of Open Source community projects by Jerry Carter, SAMBA developer
    * LUG Wars, a Jeopardy-like competition between visiting LUG groups
    * LTSP Workshop- Linux Terminal Server Project- thin client heaven
    * Everything SAMBA- hosted by Jerry Carter, SAMBA developer and author of several technology books
    * Open Source for Business Today- discussion and demonstration of current office related Open Source Technology
    * Door prizes, vendor exhibits, etc.

We request that attendees pre-register at http://www.alabamalugfest.org. You can also read more about our guest speakers (check out the bio for Greg Bledsoe), the event schedule, and workshop info. If, for any reason, attendees wish to walk up rather than pre-register, it will be helpful if they send me an email so I can plan for capacity.

Thanks!
LeRoy A. Clark, Vice President
South Alabama Linux Users Group
