Title: pcmia wifi needed
Post by: rugmonster on September 06, 2005, 03:01:30 pm
I have a Netgear WG511 v2.0 that has a Prism54 chipset; however, later versions of that card, same model number, have different chipsets. I also have a Linksys WPC11 v4 with the Realtek 8180 chipset that works well with the various wireless tools such as Kismet, Airsnort and the like. This card is only 802.11b, but works extremely well for certain uses. There is always the option of NDISWrapper, but I would suggest finding a card that works natively.

By the way, I doubt you will find a wireless PCMCIA NIC that was an external antenna that isn't hacked. I have seen reports of folks soldering a pigtail onto a wireless PCMCIA nic, but results are not always good.

Title: Re: pcmia wifi needed
Post by: green_dragon37 on November 19, 2005, 11:12:15 pm
Just keep in mind that your cantennas and other home-brew antennas _may_ not permitted by the FCC. Some people interpret them as being illegal, some don't.  Home built devices are allowed, under CFR Title 47, Volume 1,Sec. 15.23.  Biggest thing is they don't want someone throwing random parts together and causing interference to other services using the same band of frequencies, like the Federal Government radiolocation service, or accidentally microwaving their cat!

You still have to comply with the power limits, unless you're a licensed Amateur Radio operator, in which case you can have alot of fun in the first 6 channels. (1.5Kw worth!)

--Ian

Here is the exact quote from the CFR:
Quote
Sec. 15.23  Home-built devices.

    (a) Equipment authorization is not required for devices that are not
marketed, are not constructed from a kit, and are built in quantities of
five or less for personal use.
    (b) It is recognized that the individual builder of home-built
equipment may not possess the means to perform the measurements for
determining compliance with the regulations. In this case, the builder
is expected to employ good engineering practices to meet the specified
technical standards to the greatest extent practicable. The provisions
of Sec. 15.5 apply to this equipment.
