Title: Upgrading to a different Distro
Post by: steve on May 14, 2004, 10:07:25 pm
OK, I currently have Mandrake 10.0 Community installed and have been running Mandrake distros for  4 or 5 years. I'm getting ready to install SuSE 9.1 over it.

My question is, should I wipe out my home directory or not? I don't usually slick my home directory and I will have a backup of it but I know how much of a pain it is to get all the permissions right if I restore from a backup. On the other side of the coin I've got a bunch of old settings and directories on there from apps that probably don't exist any more.

Any tips/suggestions?

Steve

Title: Upgrading to a different Distro
Post by: scottstancil on May 14, 2004, 10:16:55 pm
Of course, make your backup, but I wouldn't slick it.  At worst you can boot into single user mode or probably run level 3 and simply remove the majority of your dot files and dot directories, especially the KDE/Gnome stuff.

OTOH, I like to archive stuff off to CD/DVD from time to time and start fresh.

Scott

Title: Upgrading to a different Distro
Post by: dpeach on May 15, 2004, 12:22:31 am
I was curious about the same thing a couple of weeks ago when I moved to a new hard drive. I ended up making a copy of my home directory and have moved stuff over as I have needed it. I like the feeling of a clean install, but I don't want to have to redo everything.

Have a good move!
