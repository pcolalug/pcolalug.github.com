I look at all of the different Open Source projects as just another choice. True, if you've got an enhancement to an already well developped project you should submit it to that project. However, if that project isn't willing to include your program/source then by all means fork your own project.

Now why wouldn't an open source project want to include your enhancement.
1. They want to keep the size of their project down and see your addition as unecessary to their goals.
2. They think your enahancment has too many bugs.
3. Your enhancement is outside the scope of the purpose of their project.

Outside of starting a new project for educational purposes, a lot of projects are started to fill a void. Look through the "Live CDs" and see how many of them are duplicates of each other. Each one has it's own niche.

I'm looking for two live CDs I can use but haven't found either one. If I had the time I would probably make them myself, but I don't.

I've been looking for a Live CD that can be used for running diagnostics on a system. This should include memtest+, HD diags, CPU diags, Video Diags, etc. It should also have a program similar to "Ghost" on it for making a backup image of a hard drive to a bootable CD.

The other disk I'm looking for is probably not out there due to copyright issues. I'm looking for a bootable client to connect to a citrix server. Similar to the live cd for connecting to a Windows Terminal server but this one needs the citrix client on it, or simple intructions for adding the citrix client.

As far as me rolling one on my own, I don't have the time or the desire. You know the story, two jobs, a family, a house, etc.  I also don't want them that bad. They would just be nice to haves.....I'll keep looking for someone to start another project that satisfies "my" needs. Let me know if you find one that works for me. I've already gone through the live CD list. There are a couple I want to try but none that seem to do exactly what I want.

Oh, and before anyone shoots back that Knoppix has everything in it. Exactly! I prefer small, tight  distro that does what I want and not much more. Corporate Mod Boot was that way. I just ran out of time to tweak it.

PS.PS. If anyone knows of a good "Ghost" like program that's free and will backup even Windows drives to a bootable restore CD. I'd be interested. I don't want to have to be a programmer to use it though. I like simple menu driven apps.

Again, this isn't critical, that's why I can be picky.....
