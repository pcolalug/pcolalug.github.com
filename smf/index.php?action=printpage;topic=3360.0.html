Title: DD-WRT v24 is out.
Post by: L.Rentz on May 31, 2008, 07:19:07 am
A while back, say over a year ago Bhoff did a very good presentation on third party firmware for routers.  He went over several different vendors that were available.  I chose DD-WRT.  At that time, I had been meaning to get a new router to upgrade from my old Linksys wireless "B" that I had for quite awhile at that point.  I wanted to pump up the bandwidth a little and tighten up security considering my old linksis only had WEP.

With the different options on firmware I chose DD-WRT.  After looking to see what hardware DD-WRT supported, I went out and bought a Buffalo WHR-HP-G54.  It was inexpensive, like $70 bucks and It has an built in AMP to increase range.  Keep in mind this was probably a year or more ago.

The buffalo devices have a little different installation method if you are flashing them for the first time so I used DD-WRT's wiki to guide me thru the installation.   At that time version 23-v2 was the latest.  The installation was flawless.  I was amazed at all the options that were available to me.  The stock buffalo firmware didn't even come close to having the options DD-WRT had.  It was very easy to setup and secure.

The other day I noticed DD-WRT had released the latest version V24 final.  The upgrade was so easy.  Since I had already flashed from the stock buffalo firmware, all I had to do was download the version specific DD-WRT for my device and log into my router select upgrade firmware, select the firmware I downloaded an BAMM...  Anyway, I recommend checking it out if you are thinking about flashing your router to a third party firmware. You can turn your $60 router into a much more secure and expensive one with many more options available to you.

Anyway check http://dd-wrt.com/dd-wrtv3/index.php (http://dd-wrt.com/dd-wrtv3/index.php) out.  You will not be disappointed.

Lee
