Title: Re: School
Post by: rugmonster on December 01, 2006, 01:59:44 am
It all depends on what you want to do. The Network+ cert isn't that great. It's very low in value and for a lot less money, get a Network+ book and read up on it. You'll get just as much out of it as you would an online class. Network+ basically gets you enough knowledge to start learning networking.

While networking and Unix/Linux are related, they are different and you need to decide if you're going to specialize in one or the other to start with. They are both very broad areas, so pick one and stick with it for a while.

I agree with everyone else about the unix class. I wouldn't take a Unix class unless it were part of a Bachelor's CompSci or CIS program. A one off class isn't going to teach you enough to even make the time worth it, let alone the tuition. There are plenty of free and inexpensive print references. If you visit the meetings, you'll also pick up a good deal of knowledge.

You just have to be patient and play to the learning curve. Things are getting easier for those wanting to get into Linux and Unix, but to really understand what's going on underneath it all takes more time, same as with anything else.
