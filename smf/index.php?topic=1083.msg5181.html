With my upcoming trip back to the great big sand box looming close, I decided I would go more prepared this time. When a coworker gloated to me about purchasing a laptop for himself today, I started thinking. After a little debating and some rather hasty glances over my "deployed" budget, I decided to purchase one for myself.

Here are the specs:

Dell Inspiron 5150
3.06 Ghz Mobile Pentium 4
15" "SXGA+" TFT Display (max 1400x1050)
256MB 333DDR memory (yes, it will be upgraded later)
32MB GeForceFX Go5200
30 GB Hard Drive
Integrated NIC and Modem
24x CD-RW/DVD Combo Drive
Windows XP Home preloaded (soon to be replaced with win2k and Slackware 9.0)

For what I plan on doing with this thing, I'm pretty happy. It isn't the best, but it will do compared to what I will be working with out there. I'm sure it will run games better and it will make life better for the flight over. I just hope the battery life holds up...at least long enough for me to go to sleep across the Atlantic.

It must be said...Dude, I'm getting a Dell
