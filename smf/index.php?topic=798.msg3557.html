I did, but I was one of 3 people who had the retail version. Also, many folks showed up without rigs that would play these games. Yes, the first party didn't go smoothly, but I had fun. BTW, I can't recall any members who were at the first LAN party that have dropped out. There were some non-members there, but they never joined/came to any meetings. Could these be the folks you met?

The problem as I see it is that most of our members aren't gamers. We therefor have a small population to draw LAN participants from. The last LAN party was great because we played one game, the game was free, and the majority of folks had thier rigs set up before hand. The conclusion would be that to pull this off "in-group", so to speak, would be to limit the planned games to one or two and make sure everyone is playing the same thing at the same time. There just aren't enough members interested in gaming, in my experience, to warrent a large selection of games.

Also, I think many Linux users either don't have the money and/or interest in paying for and playing games. Their computer is entertaining enough in-and-of itself. Also, you find many folks have Linux installed on an older machine that just doesn't have the power to play many modern games.

Guess this turned into a bit of a diatribe, but this has engendered an interesting set of discussion points. How can we make our LAN events better and generate more participation?

I think that this last LAN party went over so well because we settled on one game. All but one of our memebers was able to run that on their systems.

We did offer several options before the party, but ended up with just one game. I think in the future we ought to have one game with one alternative. If we can decide beforehand which game will be our main one, people can get it installed and test before the big night.

It is nice to have everyone there that can help work out kinks. But if everyone is playing but you, no one wants to help figure out your problem. (I experienced that at the December party). If we know before the big night what we are going to play, we can get the problems worked out before we even show up.
