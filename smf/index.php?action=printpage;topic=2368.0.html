Title: Showing just how much of a Newbie I am
Post by: dpeach on April 07, 2005, 01:07:57 pm
Steven, Join the club! I am glad to hear I am not the only one having this problem. Mine started a little over a month ago. I am running Mandrake 10.1. For some reason it will not boot directly into X (the windowing environment). In my case it is caused by not loading my graphics card module (nvidia). I have tried all kinds of contortions to get it to load automatically. I have to manually log in, su to root, then modprobe the driver. Then switch back to my normal user and do "startx".

There may be a fix for you though. Go to the Mandrake Control Center. To get there go to the Launcher and choose System | Configuration | Configure your computer. Give it the root password when requested. Then on the screen that comes up go to Boot, then Autologin. Then tell it you want to automatically login and which user. That is one possible fix. If that does not do it, let us know and I can supply you with at least 2 other possibilities. (It depends on what the problem is and what caused this issue, neither of which we know the answers to).

Until we get this figured out, to shut down your computer when you get out of X (KDE) just type "halt" (without the quotes) then hit enter and it will halt the machine for you.
