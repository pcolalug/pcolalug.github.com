Title: Here's what PJC responded to my MCSE training inquiry...
Post by: microserf on February 10, 2004, 10:34:57 am
Our MCSE program is college credit and consist of 6 courses that must
be taken in sequence.  The courses are in an accelerated format of 5
weeks:  during the fall or spring term you take 3 classes of 5 weeks each.  
This enables you to complete in 2 terms, less than one year.  If you
are only wanting the MCSE training for the certification, you need to
have a good background in computers and networking to be exempt from the
prerequisites.

This fall (August 2004) we will offer the first three courses in the
morning and the second three courses in the evening.  Then in the spring
the first three courses will be offered in the evening and the second
three courses in the morning.  If you are able to go in the morning from
8:00 to 9:50 Monday thru Friday, you could start in August.  However,
if you have to work in the days, you could start the evening courses in
the Spring (January 2005).

One of our advantages, other than the courses being college credit, is
the cost.  Our courses are $55.70 per credit with a $25 lab fee per
course.  The total cost of our program is $1,152.60 plus books.  
Additionally, you get hands on lab training in loading, configuring, setting up,
etc. the various operating systems.

This looks very good to me, esp about the college credit, the price, and the hands on, which online wouldn't give me. I think the camaraderie of other peeps being in the same boat would also help w. future networking opptys in the future, as my previous college experience was able to provide.  What do ya'll think?
