I just got one of these, it's the "world's first laser mouse." I dunno if it's really the first but holy...this thing is amazing.  I haven't had an oppurtunity to boot up Linux yet (kind of waiting for my sound card to come in, and I've been playing HL2 a lot) so I don't know if I'm going to need to start some major reconfiguration of my mouse from the Intellimouse I was using but [edit]DARN[/edit]!


This thing is incredible.  It's wireless, features a lithium ion internal battery, a quick charging base station (they're not kidding.  Less than an hour for a full charge that lasts for days of gaming EASILY and still has juice to spare).  My main concern with wireless was delay and this it fixes with equal speed of USB hardwired.  I was impressed by the specs but after using it ingame and around the desktop I'm shocked with it.


To be perfectly blunt, I had to put all my sensitivity settings down around half or a third of what I was using.  To be brief, they aren't kidding when it says it's 20x more accurate than an optical.  I BREATHE and this thing moves practically (slight, very slight, exaggeration).  I'm still having to adjust as I'm used to using a mouse more with just my finger movements but I'm thinking about starting using a mouse with my whole arm for movement.

It's got a lot of buttons which regardless of whether or not all of them are able to be used in Linux it's impressive.  The only button that doesn't seem to work is the "Application Switch" button but that's fine, it's right in the center of a universal "forward" and "back" button set for browsers.  The scroll wheel tilts and rolls up and down as well as pushes down AND has momentary switch buttons on top and bottom called "cruise control." Essentially it's like rolling down or up but without all the "roll, roll, roll," just holding down the button.  Like using the down or up arrow on your keyboard but with the mouse.

The feel is unreal.  This is, by far, the most comfy mouse I've EVER used.  EVER.  It actually has a good-sized hump rather than being totally flat or somewhere inbetween which isn't natural for your hands.  No, this thing is perfect.


It's also sleek and beautiful.  Normally I'm not one for aesthetics but I'm thinking about some modding to my monitor and everything else to get it to match the simple but sleek style.


Bottom line this thing was worth every [edit]darn[/edit] penny of the $80 I got it for over at Office Depot and if you're a gamer you OWE it to yourself to get one of these.

Well, I'm using it in Linux now.  Turns out I didn't need to mod the xorg.conf or anything, it works.  Of course, side-to-side scrolling and "forward-and-back" doesn't work but I didn't implement those with the other one either.  It's cool, I'd like to use it, but I heard that especially with the forward and back buttons that if the program isn't expecting it that could turn sour real quick.

Side-to-side scrolling I don't have in WindowsXP since I uninstalled the Logitech driver anyway.  Why?  Because it was running a program in the background and I don't like more processes running than is absolutely necessary.  Besides, not like I had gotten used to it anyway.


I found, like you, that I had to turn my sensitivity down but after you get used to it you'll never go back.  In CS my big issue with the ball mouse was that to get it to move just a pixel or two, just a hair for a headshot, it was freakin' impossible.  Nevermind all the cleaning, the dragging, and everything else.  The optical fixed that, and the laser made it SMOOTH.  Like, REALLY smooth.  Smoother than a fresh coat of a wax on a 60's Stingray Corvette smooth.  It's really...unique.



Oh, and congrats on the optical!  After you start using it you'll never want to use anything less.
