A year or so ago, JR Arthur and some of my fellow OS students tried to get one going.  The time students and faculty had to devote to such a thing seemed to be a bigger factor than any sort of institutional support.  Also, getting equipment to use was solely up to the members.  As a result of theses issues (as well as a certain (former) UWF professor's refusal to allow mere students access to his Beowulf cluster...) the whole thing kind of tanked.

Several months ago, I emailed JR, trying to stir up some more interest along with another former student, but work got in the way and I never followed up. Maybe he would be interested in helping to get the word out?

By way of introduction, My name is Randall Burt.  I graduated with a degree in Computer Science from UWF a year ago, August.  I started using Free/Open Source software by happenstance when I was looking at building a firewall for my home.  I installed FreeBSD on a spare machine at work and have never looked back.  Currently, I am employed as a systems application developer with Dyntel.  At work, I use Windows 2000 and Visual Basic, and I think it is slowly killing me inside.  I like web and db programming, long walks on the beach, Qt, and Apache modules.  I run SuSE 8.0 at home with KDE 3.0.2.  I'm really glad I found you folks (through SquishedMosquito.com) and can't wait for the meeting (*and* I'm bringing a friend!).
