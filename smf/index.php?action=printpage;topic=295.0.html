Hello all!

It is nice to know that in LA (Lower Alabama) there are other people interested in the open source movement. I don't live in the Pcola area, but I grew up there and left the area about two years ago. When I come back for a visit, I will be stopping by for a meeting. In the mean time, I hope to throw my two cents in, help those I can and learn at the same time.

About my experience, I worked for two years after high school at the Escambia River Electric Cooperative in Jay for their ISP. We ran a number of Linux servers and I can say I learned about half of what I know about computers as a whole there. After moving to Houston in 2001, I continued doing remote admin for them and began running my own server at home. I now do net admin for the AF and got my position of Base Info Protection because of my unix background. True, 75% of my job is helping the other folks who don't know what their doing administer their NT servers, but I at least get the satisfaction of playing with some BSDi occasionally.

At home, I run a Debian system. I learned on Slackware (hooah) and Caldera, but moved on to Debian. I tried using Redhat and Mandrake off and on, but was never really impressed. Right now, I am using Mandrake 9.0, and am fairly impressed, but I miss apt-get upgrade. When I get home (because I'm currently deployed) I play to make my primary desktop a Gentoo system. I helped with the initial beta, but stopped working with D. Robbins shortly after it went to 1.0.

I hope to get to know you all (even though I know one or two of you :)) and look forward to being a regular.

Regards,
