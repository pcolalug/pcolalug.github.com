Title: Re: Jefferson Davis Community College Opening for Network Administrator
Post by: rugmonster on September 08, 2006, 11:59:16 pm
I would like to add one more off topic post about Alan's resume.

Have you thought about taking your resume and performance reports/awards/decs/etc to a professional resume writer that specializes in military veteran services? I'm probably going to spend the money to have someone who specializes in that sort of thing have a once over of my resume.

I've also been hearing this as a rule of thumb for lengths of resumes. If you have less than 5 years experience, keep it one page. If you have more than 5 and less than 10, two is appropriate. More than 10, you use what you need. With that in mind, keep things brief and it may be a good idea to still have a very brief one pager and an extended one. Also, don't think a single resume will cut it. Taylor your resume to the types of jobs you are applying for. For example, if I apply for a LAN/WAN network admin job working on Cisco routers and switches, I'm not going to be emphasizing all of my Windows experience. I'll be focusing on all of the stuff I know about Cisco and networking in general. Show your diversity of knowledge, but don't over saturate them with buzzwords.
