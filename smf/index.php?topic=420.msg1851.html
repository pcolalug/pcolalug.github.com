CRT or LCD?
« Reply #1 on: January 24, 2003, 08:21:25 am »
Here is a portion of a good article on Toms Hardware comparing LCD and CRT monitors. The article is actually over a year old so things have gotten better. It looks like all around CRTs are better than LCD monitors. However, you have to take into account the desk space and power consumption of the LCDs. Less power consumption also means less heat. Also, it's nice that you can hang the flat panels on the wall behind your desk rather than taking up dekstop space. Working at Best Buy I get to see some of the latest flat panels and yes, they have come a long way.

Another big advantage I've noticed is a the lack of distortion on the screen that you typically see with a CRT monitor. Of course, the higher end flat screen monitors don't have this problem either. Also, with a flat panel, you don't lose any screen space due to the case. In other words a 17" flat panel has 17" viewable while a 17" CRT only has about 16" viewable. That extra inch diagonal means a lot of extra display space.

My plans as far as my next monitor goes are that I will be buying a flat panel monitor.  

If you're considering buying one, make sure you do your homework first. There are a lot of things to look for in a flat panel monitor and you don't want to regret your pruchase.
