Title: If it weren't so tragic, it would be funny...
Post by: imnes on July 11, 2003, 09:32:53 pm
Anybody use GO mail?  (mail.go.com).  It's a popular free web mail service that has been around a long time.  I've been using it since high school, so you can imagine why I'm not in a hurry to change email addresses.

On July 7th, this message showed up on the login page...

Quote

We would like to inform you that we are expanding and upgrading our e-mail systems to ensure a more reliable e-mail experience. Many of the changes we are implementing are in response to your suggestions in recent customer surveys. Specifically, you can expect to see the following:
 
1.    PROMPT DELIVERY AND RECEIPT OF E-MAILS - We are focused on ensuring the prompt receipt and delivery of e-mails. This has been achieved in large part already, and will get even better.
 
2.    EXPANDED MAIL BOX STORAGE CAPACITY - We will be increasing the amount of free mailbox storage you are allocated by 50%, from 4 MB to 6 MB, which is more storage than is available from most free services in the market today. This new experience will be rolled out to all users by August.
 
3.    SPAM REDUCTION - We will continue to focus on spam reduction. This will include adding programs to block spam from our system, and focusing on the elimination of abusers of our spam policies.
 
4.    ATTACHMENTS - We have eliminated a problem which was preventing certain attachments from being accepted.
 
© Disney. All Rights Reserved.


Cool.  4mb was feeling kinda cramped.  And all the spam was annoying.  But here's the catch.  I think they broke something, or their spam filter is misconfigured.  Because once the upgrade happened, 100% of incoming mail stopped arriving.  Messages don't bounce or get an error, they just never make it to my inbox.  Now recently, I've been getting 60-80 messages a day (counting all the spam).  I haven't received a single message in going on 5 days now :(

This is really an annoying situation, I have correspondence going with people from eBay transactions, school projects, etc.  And I can't get my mail.  What's worse - I imagine that tech support address is something like (support@go.com).  Well, I guess that would mean I can send messages to them all day long to tell them it's broke and they'll never receive them.

Right now I'm just waiting it out,  not much more I can do.  I emailed support lines at every parent company listed on their page: Disney, DisneyOnline, ABCNews, ESPN, FamilyFun, ABC.com, Movies.com - some bounced but some went through so hopefully they're getting it all straightened out!

Just one more reason to get my own mail server working...
