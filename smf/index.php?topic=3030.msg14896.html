Slow down.

Port 25 outbound is blocked by Mediacom for various reasons and this has been the case for several years. You can use their SMTP server as a smart host, meaning you can use your local SMTP server, but set it up to forward mail through their SMTP server. If you are trying to use another SMTP server that doesn't belong to them, you won't be able to use it either. Instead, setup your POP3 or IMAP account normally, but setup SMTP to point to Mediacom's SMTP server. They will relay any domain for you as long as you are on their network.

And like I said, they were feeding you a line of B.S. when they told you it was a Yahoo problem. If no one else in the world has problems getting to those sites, it only means there is a problem with your ISP or your system.

If you are having a problem with your MTU, then you wouldn't be getting to any sites. MTU has to do with a lower level protocol (Ethernet) than TCP/IP.  If you had a true MTU problem, then none of your traffic would get ANYWHERE. IP and subsequently TCP and UDP all function at higher levels and have nothing to do with the MTU. They could care less about it. That is the beauty of encapsulated protocols.

Programs such as Fetchmail and your "YoSucker" use POP3 and/or IMAP to download mail. This would not solve your problem with Yahoo Mail. Would you like a Gmail account? You would get free POP3 access with it.
