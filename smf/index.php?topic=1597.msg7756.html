Best Buy, Circuit City, Walmart, etc. all work on supply and demand. The answer is yes to all of your questions. If they stock it on the shelves and it sells out, there's a good chance they'll order more next time and use more shelf space. When they are out of it in the store and you order from the store's web site the systems corelate your zipcode and put 2 and 2 together again justifying the demand. I can say for a fact that "EVERY" Best Buy gets a stock based on the demand in their area. I  know, I've been checking the computers for the SuSE 9.1 orders and it shows how much each store is getting.

As far as the GPL question goes, I'm all for sharing my purchased Linux, if it's legal. However, I'm also the first one to tell anyone that borrows it that I took the time to buy it and show the stores and distributors that there is a market for their product. I also sugest to them that if they like it, they should als buy it.
