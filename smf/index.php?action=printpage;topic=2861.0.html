Meh sry, mis-read that part, thought he was still having trouble.  Ubuntu's wifi setup is really fast and easy compared to all of the manual configuration you have to do with other distros.

From my experience setting up wifi in slack is kinda touch and go.  I will say that I *greatly* prefer the madwifi driver over the conexiant ndis driver.  At least with the madwifi you don't end up fighting with with third party software that 'tries' to autoconfigure you onto the network, but often fails miseribly.

Title: Re: Slack 10.2
Post by: danc on April 30, 2006, 02:26:59 pm
Quote from: Brent on April 30, 2006, 12:59:58 pm
Ubuntu's wifi setup is really fast and easy compared to all of the manual configuration you have to do with other distros.

Not much to it, really.  All I have to do on Slack is untar the madwifi tarball, do a "make" and a "make install", and then paste the following into my /etc/rc.d/rc.local:

Code:
ifconfig ath0 down
iwconfig ath0 essid "xxxxxxxx" channel 5 mode Managed key XXXXXXXXXX
sleep 1
ifconfig ath0 192.168.254.50 netmask 255.255.255.0
sleep 1
ifconfig ath0 up
sleep 1
route add default gw 192.168.254.254 dev ath0
