Hello!

Just want to make a quick post to introduce myself.  

My wife and I will be leaving California and moving to the Pensacola Area by mid August.  I'm looking forward to seeing what's going on in the area as far as Linux and Open Source goes.

I would have to describe myself as a late comer to PC's. Except for a Tandy TRS80 that my family bought way back in 1983, I hadn't used computers until I bought my first PC in 1998. My interest in computers started out in the Marine Corps where I fixed F/A-18 armament and navigational control computers.

After getting my first PC, I started getting curious, and I've never stopped trying to learn since then. Since that day not long ago, starting out as a fledgling, not knowing what I was doing, I now have several computer certifications.  Unfortunately, they are all Microsoft... lol

I've been using Linux since about 1999 or 2000. My first experience was with Debian 1.3. I had just gotten my first cable modem and a friend of mine showed me how one of my junk computers that was laying around gathering dust could be put to work as a firewall/router. Since then, my interest sparked and now, after 4 years I've still only begun to see just a little of the potential that Linux has. The proverbial tip of the iceberg.

Since then, I've used Debian, Red Hat, Mandrake, SuSe, IPCop (a Firewall distro,) Gentoo and Knoppix.

My personal favorite distro would have to be Knoppix. I've found a use for it in so may applications, from data recovery to testing bad computer diagnosis's. Of all the distros I've installed, I use Gentoo on all of my computers except my firewall.

I'm currently the secretary for the Fresno Open Source User's Group.  [Brief history] FOSUG is a break off of the FresnoLUG, we broke away at the beginning of this year due to lack of organization and effort of those in charge, last minute canceled meetings, presentation givers not showing up, etc.  Since then, Fresno LUG seems to have desolved away. [/Brief history]

Besides serving as Secretary, I've been teaching a Beginner's Linux class at the beginning of our meetings.  Through the hard work of the officers of the group, we’ve established regular monthly meetings with informative topics.  Our numbers went from a small handful of 4 to 6 people meeting each month to an all time high of 34 attendees.
