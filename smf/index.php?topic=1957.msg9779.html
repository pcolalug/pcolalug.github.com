Things like OpenGL (graphics), and OpenAL (sound) are cross platform already.  And there's always SDL for networking, keyboard/mouse/joystick io, so the software is there.  If companies would develop on these technologies from the start they would pretty much automatically have cross-platform titles with little more effort.

I don't think you could get by with just writing a directx library for Linux to compile against, I think most windows games are probably making use of other APIs available in windows that wouldn't be portable.  I guess though you could use winelib (maybe from winex with directx libraries) to recompile your title for Linux and have a native executable?  I'm not sure how that works...

This is all well and good, but the question remains, why would a company want to comile against Linux.

1. In the big scheme of things Linux has very little market share compared to Windows.
2. There's doubt whether Linux users would be willing to pay for games. (Again, Look at the Loki experience. They had some of the best selling games on the market at the time, ported to Linux and they still went bankrupt.)
3. It would mean extra work and money to port/recompile and test their games on Linux.
4. They're already making a ton of Money on the Windows platform.

The bottom line is that the incentive for the Big game companies just isn't there.
The only viable solution for Linux as a gaming system is to have current Open Source developpers get into developping games. Why don't the devloppers that work on Open Office or Gimp or Mozilla try writing an open source game? This is the question we should be asking. The bottom line is that we're not going to get the big game companies over to Linux, we have to use the resources we have.

My suggestion, start working on a Game yourself. Develop a strategy and story line for it and then invite developpers from the above mentioned apps. They are the ones that can do it.

Or, go back to the tried and true playing Games on a Windows platform or emulator.
