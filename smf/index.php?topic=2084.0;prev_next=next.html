The GPL states that you are obligated to provide source code and not to restrict others rights under the GPL. Nowhere ever does it state that you cannot charge money. Also, some of the programs and libraries provided with Linspire (as well as most other big name distros) are closed and/or proprietary.

You can get the liveCD version of Linspire for about $20 if you want to try it out. If its paying for stuff that's a problem, there are plenty of other free distros out there. Welcome to choice.

Linspire is free, in fact I can give away copies at the next meeting to anyone interested in it.  It's the Click and Run software service that's a subscription pay thing, around $40 a year now I think.  

What does this payment get you?  A really nice add/remove program interface with all software categorized, including user reviews and screenshots of the software http://www.linspire.com/warehouse .  This is really nice if you know you need a Bit Torrent program, but don't know if you want BitTorrent, BitTornado, Azureus, LTorrent, BTQueue, or one of the others.  

Also the money goes into developing new Linux software and sponsoring existing projects.  http://info.linspire.com/opensource/

Since they have control over who gets the software (for the most part) and have customers who are willing to pay, they are one of the few (maybe only) distributions to include legal licensed support of MP3 playback, DVD Playback, and Video codecs including Microsoft Windows Media 8 and 9.  They include Java 1.4.2, and Macromedia Flash plugins by default.

They also offer discounts on commercial software for members, we can buy StarOffice for $29.95 (instead of $75).  WineX ($14).

Just never having to even think about dependencies is enough to make me happy
