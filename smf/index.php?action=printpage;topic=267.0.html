Title: Paying for Linux
Post by: steve on November 27, 2002, 08:36:01 am
I think pretty much everyone knows how I feel about this subject, but, just to get it on record.

I bought RedHat 5.1, SuSE 5.0, Mandrake 8.2, Civilization: Call to Power, Sid Meier's Alpha Centauri, Sim City 3000 Unlimited, and Railroad Tycoon 2.

I also payed to join Mandrake's Club.

I believe in supporting Linux and believe that the only way it will grow is by commercial backing. Without commercial backing there's no retail marketing and without retail marketing you miss 90% of the population.  I would, one day, like to be able to walk into Walmart and see just as much Linux software as we currently see Windows software. This is not going to happen without financial support.

Two of the biggest complaints I've seen about Linux are a lack of polished applications and a lack of usefull applications for the home user. This will not happen without commercialization. We have to get companies like Sierra and Broderbund and other big commercial software companies interested. This won't happen if they believe there's no money t be made in Linux.

As you can see, I look at Linux as an Operating system rather than a link to tons of free software. Free software is the bonus but shouldn't be the decision behind running Linux. IMHO.

Steve
