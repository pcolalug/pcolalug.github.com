Good morning/afternoon/evening ... whatnot.

My name is Matt.  I am currently stationed in Guam, but am moving to Florida in May.  I hope I didn't register too early, but I got a little excited seeing that there was a LUG this close to my next base.  

My Linux experience is... well... long but limited.  I first started to tinker way back with Red Hat 5.0 (I still have the discs).  But that was before IBM liked Linux so much, so my mwave modem at the time did nothing, and I couldn't really get to do much.  The first distro that I ran on a machine exclusivly was SuSE 6.1.  I ran that for about a year until I built a half decent gaming machine and thus fell back into Windows (98 at the time, ugh).  I tried half a dozen distros, never being able to stray too far from Windows because of games and a really clunky usb dsl modem.  And then when I tried to around this time last year, since games are becoming easier on Linux these days, I tried Gentoo.  And then found out just how bad the ATI support for Linux was.  Went back to XP (shudder) for a while until around Christmas when I buckled and got an nVidia card, grabbed VidaLinux 1.1 (it's Gentoo with the Anaconda installer), and never looked back (well, I guess I do when I want to play Half-Life 2).  I Love this distro.  I do consider myself above a noob, but not quite a poweruser, and I am always looking to learn something new.

Anyways as I said, I hope I didn't register too early and cheese anyone off.  I look forward to chatting with you fine folks here, and hope to get to meet everyone in May.
