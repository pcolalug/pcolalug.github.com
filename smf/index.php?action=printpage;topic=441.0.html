Gulf Coast .NET Users Group Inaugural Meeting

A group of locals are establishing the Gulf Coast .NET Users’ Group (GCNUG), a non-profit organization of .NET programmers, IT professionals, and managers in the Northwest Florida/Southern Alabama area. Since Microsoft released the .NET suite of development technologies in early 2001, there have been a growing number of developers and other IT workers who are interested in learning more about .NET and discovering opportunities for deploying it. In our area, there is a healthy community of programmers who are interested in .NET. The GCNUG is intended to meet the needs of that community.

The GCNUG was created to provide the following services to its members:

· Provide in-depth technical presentations and seminars on Microsoft .NET Technology and Software Engineering by leading local, regional, and national .NET experts.
· Provide a means for establishing contacts with colleagues in the Northwest Florida, Southern Alabama, & Southern Mississippi Area.
· Foster camaraderie and a spirit of cooperation and learning among GCNUG members.
· Improve communication with Microsoft.
· Establish a channel for disseminating information about new techniques and tools related to .NET

The first meeting of the GCNUG will be on Tuesday, February 11, 2003 from 6:30 PM to 9:00 PM at:
University of West Florida
Small Business Development Center
401 East Chase St.
Suite 100
Pensacola, Florida 32501-6160

We expect an audience of 30-40 for our first meeting, and plan to stage GCNUG events on a quarterly basis. There will be no charge for this meeting.

Our speaker will be Joe Healy of Microsoft, who will present a seminar titled “Deploying .NET Applications”. The topics presented will be:

· Successful VB.NET deployment
· Installation
· Application manifests
· Versioning

The technologies discussed will be: ASP.NET, Web Services, Windows Forms, and Mobile applications

DOOR PRIZES: A Full Retail Copy of Visual Studio.NET will be given away as a door prize as well as other nice giveaways.

The Gulf Coast .NET Users’ Group has established a Website where anyone interested in attending this seminar and/or joining the GCNUG, can register. This site will also host group information, discussion groups, and other useful information. The Website is located at http://www.gulfcoastdotnet.org.

Post by: rugmonster on February 02, 2003, 03:43:56 pm
I look at it like this. You show your support for a product/company by using and recommending to others a product or products by said company. Free software projects have a greater sense of community surrounding their products because they are made by the community. No one pays for that product because it belongs to the community unless they feel motivated to donate to the project. In the free software world, the supporters are easy to identify because they are the ones who use and contribute to the software in one way or another.

The commercial world doesn't give us this option. They give us, the consumer, a product and we show our support by giving them money and recommendations to other consumers. Microsoft may try to encourage a community around a product or group of products, but those "supporters" will only be loyal to them until they find a better product with a better price or more features. Supporters of open source projects can either make suggestions to make their product better or make the change themselves. Nothing stops them from adding something to make their product exactly what they want from it to be other than their motivation and knowledge.

One thing I have noticed is you don't find near as many "evangelists" out there spreading the joy of closed source software, or any other commercial product for that matter, like you do with open source software projects. Those you do find are usually motivated by the all mighty dollar and are getting paid for their outgoing support. I'm not saying that everyone in free software is oblivious to money, I'm just saying that people don't feel the sort of allegiance to commercial entities when compared to free software products and projects.
