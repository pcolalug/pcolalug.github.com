well I originally made the post in the wrong thread since I wasn't paying attention, but just moving it over here and deleting the other:

I stand behind Antec out of experience  

put plenty of truepowers and smartpowers into machines and the only one that has given someone trouble was caused by lack of surge suppression or a stable UPS and even in that case, the psu failing didn't take the system with it as I've seen happen many times with cheap powersupplies(some more well known cheapy psu's that i've seen and heard of more failing than actually working are deer, allied, powmax, and a few others)

also antec is by far not the most expensive powersupply, but it stands to be one the most widely used quality powersupplys with the least amount failing....sure you will see the occasional antec dead or dying powersupplies but considering how much antec powersupplies there are out there in use, they stand pretty nicely as a quality powersupply....

want to see an expensive powersupply? price a PC Power & Cooling TurboCool 510, then compare the price to say an antec Truepower 550w, antec Truecontrol 550w, or antec Neopower 480w

I'd recommend just going for an antec truepower 430w
just from newegg,  (Avg Rating out of 177 voters) rating is full 5 stars

I know theres a few others in this forum who have had good luck with antec cases/powersupplies, or atleast 1-2 others

but read around some of the big tech review sites....antecs get high marks in their stable power output and their safety features to keep from taking hardware out with it even if it did fail
