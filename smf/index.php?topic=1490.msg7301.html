I let the bots do their thing, but mostly my system loads maps quickly which helps and my ping was like 25 most of last night so that helped to. Mainly, when I start out, I look for the fastest vehicle I can and rush the node. If two bots are already on a node, I go to the next strategic one in the chain and wait. I played a little onslaught when the demo first came out when I was in Baghdad with the guys I work with.

One thing that makes me mad about the bots, even though they are good, is they don't always link up in charging up a node. That is so crucial when you're playing online with a team. Also, charging vulnerable nodes after an attack helps a lot, but I play more offense than I do defense.

I also found that when you find your team on defense, you can easily change the flow of the game by two people attacking a vulnerable node in the other teams back field. Most times, these will be untended because the other team is trying to take your node. If you can take one of theirs, you have turned the momentum and it gets the other team shaken up a bit and they'll start making mistakes.

One more thing, I don't know if anyone has an Athlon64, but Atari has released a 64 bit build of the UT2k4 demo for linux. I now have even more reason to put together a Gentoo build on this system for my A64 and trying it out. I just hope I can get drivers...
