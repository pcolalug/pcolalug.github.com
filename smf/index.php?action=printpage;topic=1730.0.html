Title: Hurdles
Post by: on June 27, 2004, 01:04:20 pm
I'm going to play devil's advocate here. There are a massive amount of hurdles to overcome for a project like this. First you can't use a modified antenna that gives you coverage like that or the FCC will not be pleased, so that leaves using something FCC approved and already available for the purpose, that is going to be expensive.

Second, one antenna and access point won't possibly be able to service all the people who would want to use such a service. Even though there aren't a huge number of technically inclined people in this area, say for example you have 10,000 people who want to use the network, that number far exceeds what a single access point is capable of handling. So then you need to find proper sites for setting up these antennas, that is a huge problem in itself and probably means you will need to ask cell companies or other radio related companies for permission to use their towers (You can bet they have the good spots or at least know where they are)

Third, you would need the support of the various counties that the network would be available to. Getting more than one county to agree to something becomes exponentially difficult. This doesn't even consider the various clearances and other legalities that would have to be taken care of. Considering the greater community probably has a higher number of technophobes than computer geeks/nerds/enthusiasts I have a hard time believing that such a proposal would last very long.

Fourth, if the idea of free internet is also a consideration that brings a whole new world of hurdles and issues to contend with, not the least of which is who will provide the bandwidth? Surely it would have to be a company, and probably an ISP. But whichever company steps up for that job has to contend with a network that would be very much like the wild west...No way to police it and thus cover their own legal liabilities. That is a situation that would be a nightmare for even users of the network. Who knows what sort of virus could be let loose or other malicious activity would happen? There's not really a way for average users to protect against that. Some kid could spend his afternoons trying to break in to any computer that is connected to the network. Yes these are all issues that exist in any other environment, but they are also all issues that will be raised when proposing such an idea to anyone.

This is a long-winded post and I've only covered the tip of the iceburg. I personally think it is a great idea, but I'm convinced that it will take blood, sweat, and tears to make it happen, at least in any way outside of under the table.
