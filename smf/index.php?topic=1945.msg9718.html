And for those that don't know what this is: lots of industry leaders talking about Linux, UNIX, OS X, and BSD in large installation settings and how to combat things like spam, intrusion detection, future of NFS, etc.

I am going to the Technical Session portion of this conference and staying in the hotel hosting it if at all possible.  If anyone wants to go, let me know by Friday afternoon at 2:00 p.m.

The schedule:
November 16 travel to Atlanta, leaving about 6 p.m.
November 17 and 18 - attend the Tech Sessions and BOFs, coming back after close of conference that day, but maybe later.

If you want to go, it would be extremely helpful to know by Friday, October 22nd.  The cutoff registration dates snuck up on me.

Up to four can stay in a room and we split the room bill 'n' ways.
Gas is on me as I will be in a Hyundai Sonata and I am going anyways.
Food is up to you.

Conference costs:
http://www.usenix.org/events/lisa04/registration/

If you can swing student status via college or whatever, it is significantly cheaper.

Just to be clear, I really only want to stay for the Wednesday and Thursday sessions.  If there is interest in staying for Friday and I can fill the room with four people (instead of me paying $139 a night, four people splitting $169 a night is much nicer).

http://www.usenix.org/events/lisa04/tech/
