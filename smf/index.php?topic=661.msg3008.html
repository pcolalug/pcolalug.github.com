Check out the nForce2 motherboard's linux support before you buy.  My friend just got one (an Asus) - linux support is minimal for it.  I think he can't run his hard drives full speed, and no 3d acceleration (something about the AGP gart not having a driver, he has a Matrox G400 3D card - you might not have that problem if you're going with an nvidia card too).  I have the Via KT400 (or 400a I don't remember) and everything is working as expected.  No word on the onboard sound drivers he's using an external sound card.  The NICs work though.

And on your DVD, CDRW, CD -- I'm wishing I had spent the extra money and gotten a DVD-Recorder with my computer.  I have maybe 300 burned CD's - I could cut that down a lot if I were using 4.7gb or whatever DVD disks.  I was worried about compatibility - but I don't think I know anybody that doesn't have at least one DVD capable computer.

Just my 2 cents.  Good luck!

Don't have any experience with nforce2 or via chipsets, but have had good luck with a couple of ECS K7S5A motherboards (SIS chipset).  The built-in SIS900 NIC (10/100) works perfectly.  Haven't ever used the onboard sound (AC97?) in Linux, but it's crap in Windows.  I'd rather use a decent sound card, which are real cheap nowadays.

The rest of your rig sounds good, although I'd go with a single 512 memory stick instead of 2x256.  I think many of the MB's now only have 2 DDR slots, and that would leave you room for future expansion...  Also I prefer Western Digital HD's, but that's probably just a Ford vs Chevy issue these days.  I do think you'll be very pleased with a GF4 Ti (don't get the MX), and the new Nvidia drivers are sweet.  Good luck.
