Title: openbsd
Post by: rburt3 on July 03, 2004, 08:21:24 am
OpenBSD is excellent for boxen on the periphery of your network (routers, gateways, proxies, etc). Its touted as the most secure OS and they do go to great lengths to ensure things like buffer overflows are kept to a minimum. With the focus on security, as well as a Debian-like commitment to licensing, its not the most suitable for desktop use. Having said that, I do know someone who uses it as their desktop, but this dude doesn't spend a whole lot of time with GUI programs. Generally, I like to use OpenBSD for routers and infrastructure-type stuff, FreeBSD/Linux for servers and Linux for desktops. This gives me really nice Unix-like OS's everywhere and Linux has the best desktop experience of them all, IMHO. In the end, however, kilar is right on that if you didn't like FreeBSD, OpenBSD is probably too similar to warrent you giving it a shot on the desktop. Now, if you are building a router/firewall OTOH, I would suggest nothing else.
