Title: suse ipconfig question
Post by: rugmonster on August 21, 2005, 09:10:09 am
Code:
# dhcpcd -k eth0


That kills dhcpcd and tells it to send a DHCP_RELEASE to the dhcp server.

Now, as to what connection you are sharing. Is this cable or DSL or something else?

Cable, you can usually power cycle the modem when changing systems. The other option is to make the SuSE system have the same MAC address as your Windows desktop. Mind you, this will not be permanent, unless you add it to your network init script, but you could do something like this
Code:
# ifconfig eth0 hw ether xx:xx:xx:xx:xx:xx

With DSL, I think most of the modems actually include a router so you would just need a switch.

Finally, the best option is to get a Linksys WRT54G or GS and not even worry about dealing with DHCPCD or faking MAC addresses.

Post by: rugmonster on August 22, 2005, 02:18:24 pm
Depends on the chipset your wireless uses. Some chipsets have native support while others use NDISWRAPPER and the windows drivers.

For example, my wireless card uses the Prism chipset. I have to load a firmware so that when the module is loaded, the firmware is loaded to the card. From there, I configure the wireless connection either with the Gnome Network Manager or the iwconfig command.

Let us know what wireless NIC you are using. You can also do a search on Google and find out what path you should take, but if you are lost, let us know and we will help you out.
