Here come my guesses.....

#1: Assuming all your other software is staying the same, and you're just adding a new drive and mounting it as /home, then just copying all your files to the new parition should be fine.  

#2: KDE seems pretty good about letting you use old settings.  As long as you're not changing drastically (like kde 2 to kde 3.2 might not work).  I say try loading with your old settings and if you have any problems then you can delete your .kde and .qt directories.

#3:  I don't see anything wrong with putting your stuff onto fat32. But I've never used a fat32 partition in linux, would it still allow the long filenames, and weird filenames like .desktop?  

#4: Should work, just make sure to also share /etc on all computers, or just make sure your user-id and group-id numbers are the same.  Otherwise it will work fine from one computer but the group and user will be screwed up from the other computer.

To my knowledge, the way that an OS handles the FAT32 and the way it handles UNIX are two different systems (though I've heard that ext2 is supposed to be the basis for ext3 and reiserfs which means that ext2 is still a FAT32 system, but not quite).

As far as what we learned in class, FAT32 in terms of Windows pretty much means that having a "." in front of it should be okay but Windows might treat it weird because it's expecting a three character extension to define the type.

In a UNIX system (like what I imagine Linux uses) it has a different type of table and links are actually just pointers to a block on disk.  And when you do this it increases the counter for the number of links on the original file's descriptor table (which has all sorts of things such as initial block, final block, number of pointers of indirection, links, et al).


Assuming all of that, I wouldn't imagine that links would work in the same way, though they might work a different way.  It's very possible I'm wrong (we spent time on it, but only with regards to Windows and UNIX, not LINUX) and if I am please tell me otherwise so I understand a bit more about it.  Still, I don't see how the links would work since the idea differs vastly between linking in a UNIX FS and a FAT32 FS.
