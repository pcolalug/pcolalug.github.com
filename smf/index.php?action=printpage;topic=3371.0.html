Web Development and Server Administrator

Principle Duties and Essential Job Functions:

Performs systems analysis, design, integration, programming and implementation of applications in an object-oriented or GUI environment; and/or performs analysis of requirements and programs and other tools directly related to development of products for the Internet. Documents, designs and prepares technical reports, presentations, and briefs as required. Develops applications using scripting languages. May have responsibility for configuration and installation of tools and languages. Provides web site and page maintenance. Responsible for assuring the quality of all services and deliverables.

Basic Qualifications:

Prefer Bachelor’s with five (5) years, nine (9) years in lieu with H.S. Diploma.

Performs analysis of requirements and programs using HTML, Cold Fusion, script editors or other tools directly related to development of products for the web. Develops web applications, using languages including ASP, JavaScript, VBScript, Visual Basic 6, Visual Studio 2005.net, etc. Object-oriented development tools such as Microsoft Access, Visual Basic, or other visual/object-oriented tools can be used. Creates/maintains the validity and normalization of a SQL Server 2000 Server, writes ad hoc queries, and produces reports from the data. Provides server administration for web servers and SQL Server 2000 server, along with setting server configuration based on Gold Disk Standards, and preventive maintenance. Assists with SSAA requirements. Maintains and runs pre-built applications daily, weekly, and monthly and deliver data through secure FTP or transferred to secure FTP server for customer to download. 

Must have experience with Classic ASP, VBScript, JavaScript, HTML, SQL Server 2000 Servers, and/or IIS. Able to provide configuration based on Gold Disk Scans.

Desired knowledge/experience includes ColdFusion, ASP.Net, Visual Studio.net, and/or SQL Server 2005 Server. Ability to contribute to SSAA requirements.

Key Competencies: Classic ASP, JavaScript, VBScript, HTML, FTP, SQL Server and/or IIS.

Clearance: Ability to obtain and maintain a SECRET Clearance may be required in the future.

 

Active Directory Domain Administrator                                                                                         

Principle Duties and Essential Job Functions:

Provide full range of high-level enterprise administration for the Navy Training Network. Maintain organizational units and attributes across the enterprise. Provide expert level consultation on integration issues/solutions for the Windows 2003 NETC Training Enterprise Network.  Provide support to site administrators in regards to group policies, user account issues, etc. Assist in the maintenance of 100+ domain controllers from hardware, warranty status, and compliance with security requirements. Provide policy and procedure documentation, status reports, and periodic briefs as required by the government client. Assist in script development and testing as needed. Assist in the PKI implementation efforts.

Basic Qualifications:

Prefer Bachelor’s with six (6) years related experience or ten (10) years in lieu with H.S. Diploma.

Skills: Active Directory, Group Policies, DNS, WINS, and/or like enterprise administration functions. Security +, MCP, MCSA and/or MCSE certification desired.

Clearance: Possess or able to obtain and maintain a SECRET Clearance.


Functional Administrator & Trainer

Headline: Navy Education, Training, and Advancement System Professional Needed

Principle Duties and Essential Job Functions:

Participate in highly complex technology projects to help Navy field manpower, personnel, training, and education systems.   The right candidate will become a senior technical expert specialized in solving problems by integrating knowledge sharing and collaboration methods into information technology systems.  Provides direct customer support and training as needed while also assisting with testing of new products.  Provides training via live classroom instruction, electronic materials production, and distance learning methods.  Ensures Navy portal products provide seamless support of Navy-wide MPTE systems especially Sea Warrior systems including ILE, CMS, ETJ.   Maintains liaison with managers and technical personnel in NETC, NETPDTC, and user commands to mediate, negotiate, or secure agreement among various entities to assure highly effective, reliable, and timely support of the NKO portal.

Basic Qualifications:

Prefer Bachelor’s with six (6) years, ten (10) years in lieu with H.S. Diploma.

Candidate must have a comprehensive knowledge of the Navy, including its organization, relationship to other DoD agencies, communications, procedures, decision-making processes, and missions.  Knowledge of major operating characteristics of training delivery systems, associated computer equipment, networking, system software and support functions.  The candidate must have knowledge of analysis and design techniques to implement IT systems and modifications.

Key Competencies:  High proficiency with the Microsoft Office Suite including MS Project.  Familiarity with Navy Knowledge Online and with the following:  Communities of Practice, Integrated Learning Environment, Navy e-Learning, FLTTMPS, Electronic Training Jacket (ETJ), Navy College, SMART Transcripts, Navy Advancement Center, Navy Learning Centers.  Prefer basic knowledge of database concepts, call center operations, DoD classified material systems, Navy acquisition systems, software testing methods, and DMDC/DEERS systems.  Prefer Navy Master Training Specialist (MTS) certification and/or classroom experience, and/or civilian training certifications and classroom experience.

Clearance: Possess or be able to obtain and maintain a SECRET Clearance.



NETC Manning Specialist

Principle Duties and Essential Job Functions:

Exercises analytical techniques when gathering information, defining work problems, designing a solution, developing procedures to resolve issues, and quality assurance. Reviews requirements, analyzes data, compares alternatives, prepares specifications, and resolves processing problems. Provides direct client contact on deadlines, obstacles, expectations and status. 

Possess excellent number recognition, formula, and/or query skills.  Uses creativity, judgment, and initiative in solving highly complex technical problems. Demonstrates cohesive teambuilding in support of the project team.

Basic Qualifications:

Prefer Bachelor’s with four (4) years related experience or eight 8 years in lieu with H.S. Diploma.

Review Individual Augmentation (IA) requirements from BUPERS. Run reports for all viable candidates based on new IA requirements received from BUPERS. Research possible alternate designators, NEC/NOBC, and/or grades. Review nomination submission to identify qualified candidates with least impact to command. Request PRD/EAOS extensions when needed with respective detailers. Liaison, as directed, between client, BUPERS and sub-claimancy questions, issues, etc. Enter IA new entry, nominations, notes, etc into the IA tracker. Create file folders for new IA, file communications in respect to specific IA tasking. Provide support to timelines, IA daily report, and weekly updates.

Knowledge and/or experience with Navy Ratings and Rank structure, a plus. YN/PN/NC experience desired.

Skills: MS Excel, MS Office, quality assurance, and/or strong number recognition.

Clearance: Possess or able to obtain and maintain a SECRET Clearance.
