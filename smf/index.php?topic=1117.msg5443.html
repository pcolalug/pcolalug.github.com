« on: October 31, 2003, 11:32:16 pm »
Well, it's been an interesting past 24 hours for my computer...  Had a total hard drive loss, and have been working to recover.  Yesterday my machine started making severe clicking noises, which progressed to squealing/grinding noises with 2 minutes.  Then things stopped working.  Upon reboot, the hard drive was not even recognized by the BIOS, and obviously wouldn't boot.  Moved the hard drive to another machine, and got the same results.  Gonzo.  Thank $DIETY I had a 2-day old backup of my home directory...

This drive was a Western Digital 80Gb, 7200rpm, 2Mb buffer.  Has been a good drive, and I have installed/used many other WD's over the years.  In fact that's all I've used for a long time.  Anyway, went to Best Buy and got another WD, 80Gb, 7200rpm, with the newer 8Mb buffer.  Installed and worked, but had a pretty noticeable high-pitched whining noise to it.  After a few hours I decided I did not want to listen to that any more, both because it was annoying and I'm figuring there must be something wrong and it'll fail soon too.

So...., back to Best Buy and exchanged it for a Seagate (same specs).  Installed and working great, and absolutely silent.  I like silence, at least from hardware.  I used to use Seagate back in the days when a 40Mb drive was pretty big, but haven't used them since.  Seems like a very nice drive.

What do you prefer for Hard Drive brand?  Feel free to include comments, reasons, horror stories, etc...

I voted with a "don't care as long as it works" vote. But I think it needs clarification.

I have not had horror stories with drives except my two notebooks both had drive trouble. I chalk that up to the horrible heat notebooks create. Even the plastic on the case is melted and warped from the heat this computer puts out.

I never buy a drive without reading reviews first. This came after a drive my Dad bought (WD) went flakey after just a few days. Did some research and found out that there was a run that summer that yielded quite a few bad drives. Dad was able to take the drive back, and if I remember correctly, the place where he bought it offered a better, more expensive, drive in exchange.

Since then, I just take a little time to find out what is going on in the drive world. Of course, it also helps that I rarely buy anything as soon as it comes out. As far as HDs go, I usually buy the smallest drive currently available.

When I replaced the HD on my notebook, I was looking to get something similar in size to what I had. I could only find 40 GB at the time as the smallest.

I have had good luck with Fujitsu on the only drive I bought from them (still running and bought in 1996 or 7). It is just 1 GB, but it works.

I voted for Western Digital as well, since those are the type that I've had, on average, the least amount of trouble with; don't misread that as 'WD drives have never failed on me', since they have.  Just the key word here is 'on average'.  The two 100 gig drives in my primary home machine are approaching 3 years of age;  the mixed bag of 20 gigger WD's  in one of my raid machines have been running almost continuously 24/7 for the past 3 months, plus whatever unkown length of service they saw from their previous owner.
