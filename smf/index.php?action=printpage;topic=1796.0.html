Title: SSH scanning
Post by: lloydr on July 26, 2004, 04:03:53 am
seems there has been a good bit of reports lately about people's logs filling up with failed logins into machines running sshd.

http://www.dslreports.com/forum/remark,10854834~mode=flat
http://www.incidents.org/diary.php?date=2004-07-23
http://isc.sans.org/diary.php?date=2004-07-25

I'm starting to see the same thing in my logs

the main ips filling up my logs the past 3-4 days are:
216.86.221.113
trying users test and guest

66.0.8.242
trying users test, guest, and admin

156.63.113.108
trying users test, guest, admin, user, and root

212.4.172.123
trying users test and guest

but since none of these users exist except root of course, and I block root from accessing anything except physically logging in at the machine, these tries to log in aren't going to get anywhere.

Keep your eye out for things like this in your logs if you have sshd open to the outside world and be sure to take a look at the information about this. Also this is why it's also a good idea to be sure to disable root logins through ssh  :wink:

Those of you with sshd open to the world, might want to start making sure your sshd_config is setup well to prevent any problems and possibly start keeping up with your logs a little more just in case.

Also it looks like if you capture anything involving these attacks, be sure to send it over to ISC.
