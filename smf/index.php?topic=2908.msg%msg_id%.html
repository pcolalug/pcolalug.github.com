Questions 1 and 3 I have no authority on, but question 2 is a simple one. Yes, it will work. And work well. You will go out of your gateway into the WRT-54G box. Then at that point that box just works like it normally would. It does not matter where you provide the connection from, directly from the Internet, or from a box inside the network, that Linksys router will work just like normal.

The only caveat I can see though is that you will then have 2 firewalls. If you want to port forward anything or just open a port, you will need to do it on your gateway and on the Linksys box. This will allow for a much more flexible network and greater security overall (assuming that you watch your P's and Q's).

Question 1 - Search Google. That's what I always do. There are a lot of chipsets supported, but not all of them work with HostAP. I think you're on to something with using a WRT54G.

Question 2 - Yes, this would work. You have several options here. You could get the Sveasoft, HyperWRT or OpenWRT firmware and do all kinds of cool stuff. But just for a basic setup, plug the network from your system into one of the four switched ports and then your wireless clients and your linux system will all be on the same network; no NAT, no firewall, nothing between them. This can be a good thing or a bad thing. The best part about it is you would be seperating your wireless network from your internal wired network, which is a very good thing.

Question 3- netfilter.org. Also, a Google search will give you lots of these. There are also plenty of tools that will assist you with coming up with iptables rules, such as firestarter, shorewall, gnome-lokkit, guarddog, etc.
