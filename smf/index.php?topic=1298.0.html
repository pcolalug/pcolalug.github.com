Hey guys,

Again, I was pleasantly surprised to see the excellent turnout @ the meet, all seats were filled, and then some! I was also interested to hear that there were 80 registered members.. all seemingly by word of mouth, web links, postings on other sites, etc..

If the mandate, or mission statement of our LUG is to expand the local interest, use, enthusiasm, commercial application, etc of all things Linux, I was wondering to myself today.. Is the LUG operating in a vaccuum?

I have a feeling that our group, is probably one of the most technologically advanced/minded teams on the Gulf Coast.. There is a certain element of the underground culture/hacker culture associated w. Linux.. I personally felt almost subversive, taking part in the meet, as all my daily bread, finances, etc., have been tied to the microsoft juggernaut in one way or another.

I haven't completely read all the postings, but I'd like to pose this, would it be possible, to have a local community outreach? How about a way to raise the LUG's visibility in the community?  I realize that we are so involved w. our jobs, families, lives/personal activities, but would this be possible?

One of the things that I took away from the meet, aside from the great information from the presentation, was that the group seems very focused on personal pursuits, expanding the personal knowledge through the O'Reilly sponsorship for instance.

I didn't see/hear anything about ties w. UWF, PJC or other educational institutions in the area. Now, feel free to shoot me down, because I'm a newbie, but is this group completely focused on personal pursuits, or does anyone involved wish to cross pollinate our skills into the greater Pensacola community?

For instance, applying technical skills, in a voluntary manner, to help say http://www.loavesandfishessoupkitchen.com/index.html?row2col2=indexrow2col2.html under the agreement that it is PLUG that is providing the interface to help that organization w. whatever technology projects that they wish to complete, but do not have the funds or the manpower to accomplish..

OR, for a more commercial angle, how about raising the groups visibility w. www.itpensacola, or www.itflorida.com.  Perhaps have a delegation go down to the 2004 ITFlorida High Tech Summit  on February 26 - 27, 2004,
and bring back reports, pics of the participation there, and new ideas.

I realize that we are part of a subculture of sorts. To me, what characterized a bit of computing in general was a subversive tendency, w. phreaking, hacking, cracking, etc. We have to realize now though, that the technologies that we promote and use amongst ourselves personally and professionally, is poised to become more and more mainstream.

Again, I'm a newbie, I don't know the history of this org. I'd appreciate some feedback on these thoughts!

Respectfully,

Since I first started participating with the PLUG, I've seen us grow in so many ways. We've grown in knowledge, membership, and individual participation as a whole. I think we are poised at a point to take a step forward. I think the idea of reaching out the community is a great idea and the right thing to do. Here are some ideas I have for some projects:

1. LAN Parties -- I've heard people talk about organized LAN parties that are setup commercially and I thought I saw a store somewhere around Pine Forrest Road. If we could get an event with them on a Friday or Saturday night featuring the PLUG. Perhaps we could tap into our other corporate sponsors for donations for prizes and such. We could feature multiplayer games native to WIN32 and Linux.

2. Not-For-Profit Computers -- With the number of NFP organizations around the area, perhaps we start a donation system where we take in old components and systems, put them in a working order and load them with Mandrake or other easy to use desktop distribution. Most organizations only need an office suite to include word processing, a spreadsheet and possibly email. About three years back, I was asked by the Santa Rosa County Pregnancy Resource Center, run entirely by volunteers on a very limited budget to help them out with their systems. This was very rewarding and even though their systems were old (donated), they got their job done and provided them with a valuable resource they couldn't otherwise afford. This I see as potentially being more time consuming with the potential support issues.

3. INSTALLFEST!!! -- This has been the biggest thing I've seen other LUGs do. If a corporate sponsor would provide us some space in their store...say in a training office or if they have room in the sales floor to set up a couple of tables. If we could post some flyers about and advertise this, it could be a win/win for us and the store by increasing awareness and by bringing customers into their store. We would need to reassure the store, we would not be doing any repair services, just installing and configuring linux. We could also hand out copies of Live CD distros to passing customers. Could be a very good thing.

I think if we could pull one of these off, it would be great for us and our corporate sponsors.
