Title: Re: nVidia Quadro 4400G PCIE, benchmark tests question.
Post by: rugmonster on December 09, 2005, 06:13:47 am
Probably CAD programs, Maya, Lightwave or 3d Studio Max. The thing that sets the Quattros apart is the quality of their rendering. They aren't quite as fast as you might think when you look at gaming performance, but you'll never get the same accuracy of rendering out of a Geforce or Radeon.

I just found some benchmarking tools. spec.org (http://www.spec.org/gpc/downloadindex.html) Enjoy!

Quote from: microserf
Supposedly can spit out 130 million triangles (polygons?) per second.

Triangles make up all of the shapes in a rendered image. Here (http://www.beyond3d.com/forum/archive/index.php/t-12703.html) is a good discussion on the topic of triangles/quadrangles.

For reference on triangle count and their result, look at the following.

(http://www.unrealtechnology.com/clean/images/ue3_char2-s.jpg) (http://www.unrealtechnology.com/screens/character_creation2.jpg)
5,287 triangle in-game mesh in 3D Studio Max.

(http://www.unrealtechnology.com/clean/images/ue3_char1-s.jpg) (http://www.unrealtechnology.com/screens/character_creation1.jpg)
Purely geometric 2,000,000 triangle detail mesh in 3D Studio Max.

More details on the UnrealEngine3(above) here (http://www.unrealtechnology.com/html/technology/ue30.shtml)
