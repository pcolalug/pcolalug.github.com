Interesting article.  I'd like to comment on a few of the Starter Edition's "features".

*  No Aero Glass, meaning you can only use the “Windows Basic” or other opaque themes. It also means you do not get Taskbar Previews or Aero Peek.
   
    Makes some sense.  Home Basic didn't have Aero, and people seem to want XP, which didn't have Taskbar Previews or Aero Peek.  Give them what they want?

 * No Personalization features for changing desktop backgrounds, window colors, or sound schemes.
   
    This just seems silly.  I highly doubt this will be in the final release.

* No Multi-monitor support.

      Looks like linux will be running on those dual-screened netbooks!

* No DVD playback.

      DVD is an outdated format.  There will be however Blu-ray and HD-DVD support for your netbooks.

* No Windows Media Center for watching recorded TV or other media.

      This is supposed to be a bad thing?
.

* XP Mode for those that want the ability to run older Windows XP programs on Windows 7.

       No more Word Perfect 3, Netscape Navigator, or MS-DOS gaming for you!



Though they say "the Starter edition of Windows is designed for entry level PCs and available only in certain regions"  I highly doubt it's going to keep excluding some of those basic functions.  Microsoft already got rid of the 3-app limit, I imagine they'll fix those things as well.  Windows 7 when I tested it was rock-solid and a very enjoyable experience, and that was just the Beta.  I hear better things about the RC1 that's out now and expect even better things from them in the future.

If not, more linux marketshare!

And as the article pretty much stated, Apple doesn't know how to make a <$1000 computer.
