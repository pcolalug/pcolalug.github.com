Don't stop at grants. Apply for any and all financial aide. There are also many books and websites that tell you about available scholarships. The application for State FA will come back with all the money the state will give you.

Don't be afraid of *Subsidized* Student Loans (don't get the un-subsidized kind). Like Trigger said, you won't have to start paying these back until 6 mos. after you graduate (ok, you will be required to start making payments if you drop below 1/2 time for more than 6 mos, but you can defer your payments if you start up again). The state pays the interest (which is low) on the loan while you are in school so your lender is happy. What's more, you can elect a "graduated" payment schedule in which your monthly payments start out low and gradually increase every couple of years or so. Finally, you have, like, 30 years to pay it back.

Again, apply for anything and everything, even if you don't think you qualify. If you get some money with terms you don't think you like, you can always decline *after* you have been awarded but *before* the money is disbursed.

After that, all you have to do is go to class and pass your courses.

PS. Skip the laptop, live on campus, and use the labs!
