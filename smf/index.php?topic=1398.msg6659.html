If you have the SATA interfaces operating, anything with a 2.6.x kernel will identify them first as HDA-HDD, then the EIDE drives as HDE-HDH. If you were to disable the SATA interface in the BIOS, then your EIDE drives would have started at HDA like you were expecting.

I have had little to no feedback on this problem so it appears that Jesse and I are the only ones to purchase a KV7 mobo. I suspect the problem lies in the SATA support in the 2.6.x kernel. Internet and LAN connections went smoothly. At the very start of the installation, the installer installed a SATA driver, much like what is required for WindowsXP to install.
