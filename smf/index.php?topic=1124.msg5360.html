Mandrake has the URPMI auto-update function, which is very similar to Debian's apt-get and (I assume) the Slackware swaret.  There are vast repositories of Mandrake packages and urpmi works very smoothly.  Mandrake tends to stay on top of new package versions, and things are always very much up to date.

I think Mandrake is often thought of as a "noob" distro, but I just don't get that.  After all, under the hood things are pretty much the same between distros.  Linux is Linux.  The cosmetic touches (and urpmi) are what make it attractive to me.  I have found the new 9.2 release to be most excellent.
