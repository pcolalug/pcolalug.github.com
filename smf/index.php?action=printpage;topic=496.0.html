Post by: rburt3 on February 21, 2003, 09:15:44 am
But for a couple hundred more, you can get this (http://store.apple.com/1-800-MY-APPLE/WebObjects/AppleStore.woa/77/wo/zW1XkDTHvpP72lvZyl2YC4iSKDX/1.3.0.3.27.21.1.0.19.3.1.1.0?45,17). From what I understand from reading /. (I know, I know...), the iBook is a better deal. As we know, MHz isn't the whole story, and benchies seem to indicate that the processor in the Lindows machine is equivalent to a 400-500 MHz Celeron/Duron.

What is neat is that Lindows has done what other mainstream distros might want to think about: getting manufacturers to put their software on compatible, tested systems, pre-configured, and at an affordable price. I know Mandrake and Lycoris have versions of the Wal-Mart PC, and Lycoris was actually offering some notebooks and desktops pre-configured long before this Wal-Mart deal (the pc's in question were really under-powered and a few generations behind the curve, IIRC).

So, I see an opportunity for a OEM to break out by partnering with these distros and offering a complete line of rigs. I know if there were quality pre-configured PC's and laptops, at price points comparable to Dell/Gateway/whoever, the geek market would be wrapped up. Now, if RH, Mandrake, SuSE, Xandros, and the other distros (ones with a little more "street cred" in the community than Lindows) can take this page out of Lindows playbook, we could have a pc panacea!
