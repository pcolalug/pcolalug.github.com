Title: Laptop runs hot when using Mandrake Move
Post by: rugmonster on July 06, 2005, 05:10:09 am
I can tell you now why this is happening. SuSE more than likely had the ACPI (Advance Control and Power Interface) working properly for your laptop, where Mandrake Move does not. ACPI handles all of the power related issues in modern systems, CPU Frequency Scaling to be one of them. CPU Frequency Scaling is where the system adjust the speed of your processor faster or slower depending on the current load of your system, meaning you use less power when there is less load and only more when you need it. If CPU Frequency Scaling isn't working or set to automatically adjust, your system will run hotter because it will be running at full speed all of the time.

So, how you go about setting that up, I don't remember, but that should at least give you a direction to look.
