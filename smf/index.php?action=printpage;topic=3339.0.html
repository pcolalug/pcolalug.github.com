Title: Re: Asus Eee Pc
Post by: mobilemail on July 31, 2008, 11:50:24 am
Okay, does a post 2 months late still count?  :D
A couple of weeks ago I got an eeePC 900 16GB w/ linux.  I thought (and still think) it's the "bee's knees", but it had a couple of drawbacks:
1.  Getting the thing changed into a conventional linux desktop was a pain, and afterward every app I tried to install had several dependencies, and at least one dependent app would not be available.  Arrrgh!  The stock linux is Xandros, an the shipped apps were a bit out of date.  (Open Office 2.0, etc.)

2.  Battery life was....okay.  2-1/2 hours

3.  Keyboard was smallish.  I'm okay with that, as I became accustomed to smallish keyboards in the clamshell Windows CE days (okay, I'm old.)  Some people find this challenging.  If you tend to type with only two fingers anyway -- and you know who you are -- you won't be offended.  The keyboard reminds me of an old HP Jornada.

4. The eeePC has a known glitch discussed on the forums that causes it to drain its battery slightly when powered off and unplugged from AC. AFAIK, that still exists.

Long story short, it took me only a few days to dump the stock OS, repartition the SSD and load Mandriva.  I chose Mandriva based on my forum research where noobish eeePC users like myself had the most success with painless installation.  The result is Linux on a very portable "netbook" that is fully functioning w/ decent speed scalling and power adjustments; and battery life pushing 4-ish hours.

If you are willing to trade a little horsepower for portability, I highly recommend it. If you snag one of the deals currently available, you can get one for the price of a high end iPod.  I'm not particularly a gamer or photophile -- more like a forum surfer.  But I love mine.

One other glitch.  I also subscribe to several podcasts, and .m4a is apparently a common codec for the video podcasts.  Doesn't exist in Mandriva Free, and even Mandriva PowerPack doesn't include all of Fluendo's codecs.  Unless someone has an open-source suggestion, I'm getting ready to throw some money at Fluendo to appease my addiction. 

In other news:  I go out of town frequently, so I've had a hard time keeping up with your club events and schedules.  I've been trying for the last year to catch up with a meeting, but with (obviously) no success.  *Noob alert*
