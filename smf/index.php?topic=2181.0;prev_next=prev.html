What I have noticed with the MS lines of keyboards is that they all feel about the same. As for the tilt wheel function, I doubt you will get that in linux, however, I don't even use it in Windows. The wheel motion itself is very smooth and the weight of the mouse with the batteries in makes for a very smooth feel. Also, the added functions can more than likely be assigned to do functions in your favorite window manager.

I read a review on Tom's Hardware Guide about the Logitech Keyboard. They were in love with it, but admitted that it was the first keyboard they reviewed, so had nothing to compare it to. They have not reviewed an MS board.

If one of the many pages that I have read has said what the battery expectancy is on the Logitech board, I don't remember seeing it.

THG liked both mouses. They felt the Logitech mouse was a better gaming choice, but admitted that there was not much difference as far as responsiveness goes. (There are three other mouses they recommend as gaming mouses, Logitech owned two of these slots with the 3rd being a Razor mouse).

A couple of the things that they said about the Logitech board that interested me were about the shape of the keys and their feel. They keys are flat, more like a notebook keyboard, with just some slight curvature to the bottom row of keys. The feel of the keys when depressed was described in an interesting way. They said that it does not stop at the bottom of it's travel, rather it gets firm, kinda spongy at the end. So you have feedback that you have depressed the key without it being a full stop. Doesn't mean much to me other than that was THE thing that the THG team fell in love with.

Mouse is rechargeable with the Logitech. Battery life on the keyboard is a good question. I can get the Logitech for $20 cheaper, but I have to have it brought in. I can get the MS set locally though. That means I could have it now instead of a month from now.

Seeings how waiting has never been one of my strong points, that weighs heavily in MS's favor. But everything else is pushing me to the Logitech set.
