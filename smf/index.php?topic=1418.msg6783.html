Thank you for the warm welcome last night at the meeting and to win the Xandros drawing was an excellent way to join the group.  My name is Scott Stancil and you may remember me from the back of the room with the 15" Powerbook in the back of the room, not the 17" Powerbook that Dee (?) was sporting.

I am a Systems Administrator with a web development company in Chapel Hill, North Carolina, called webslingerZ ( http://www.webslingerZ.com ).  We are almost entirely Red Hat or Fedora on the server side, half Mac OS X, quarter Windows XP, and quarter Red Hat Linux (v7/9) on the client desktop and development side.

My other job is 3/4-time grad student with the Florida Institute of Technology ( http://fit.edu ) in Systems Management.  I think that is a fancy way of saying IT degree with more of a business slant.  I picked it because I have always been the techie that couldn't understand why my managers kept muttering about total cost of ownership, budgets, support, and organizational fit.

Also, I have a three-year old son and a wife that hooked me up with an afternoon sailing trip for my birthday.  Although she probably won't see this post, she done good.

The presentation last evening was excellent, technical & useful to just about any level of user. (Thanks Mr. Hoff?)  Exactly what I was hoping to see.  That was one of the best LUG meetings I have been to in probably five years.  Everyone was very hospitable with numerous offerings of books.  (Thanks for the offer, but between LDAP & school, my plate is full for now.)

Thanks again.  Hope to see everyone March 6th.  BTW, Xandros is installed and I am using it to post this now.  I might post a little review after midterms.
