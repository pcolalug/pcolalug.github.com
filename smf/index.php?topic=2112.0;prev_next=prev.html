I think you have to decide what you want from the position. If its a fair and equitable wage, wait and negotiate the new contract. If, on the other hand, you want experience and the project has some interest to you, go with that. Since you are still doing your formal education, the experience outweighs the paycheck, especially if you don't *have* to work. In either case, your clearence is gold in the government contracting field. Don't be afraid to throw that into the negotiating hat. It costs contracting companies a pretty penny to get their folks clearence and oftentimes they cannot bill the customer for that expense.
