Title: Re: Laptop PS brick
Post by: bhoff on June 07, 2008, 12:23:03 pm
Actually those specs are not what it puts out default, But more what it can supply if load demands it.
It is like a house you put a 200amp service which gives you the ability to safely draw 200 amps of power to supply your house needs.
Where as if you had an older 60amp service and suddenly add a dryer, tanning bed and new super computer well then you are going to start kicking breakers.

As for your brick as long as the power supply meets the required volts and the adapter matches up(meaning the - and + match up with the old one you should be fine. Now if you ever get a short or something in the laptop then you could have issues due to the fact  that the power supply can supply a larger load. So I think you are safe ...that coming from a long time hardware hacker and Lic. Electrician.
