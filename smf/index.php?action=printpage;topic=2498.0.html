Title: Advice on KVM switch
Post by: dpeach on June 23, 2005, 04:22:21 pm
Any KVM should work for you between the 2 OSes. The OSes will not be the problem from what I understand. It will be hardware related.

The only thing that I know you need to be careful about is keeping your mouse working between switches. When I used a KVM and switched to the other computer, my mouse would stop working. Interestingly, this has only happened with my newer computers, not my older ones. So, I suspect that there is something with the way the PS/2 port works in newer machines. I have read about this quite a bit in the last 2 years, but never followed it out to a solution.

I believe Rugmonster may have some input in this area. But until you hear from him, or someone else, do some looking to see if the KVM needs to be a powered one, or what might be the problem.

If you are using all USB components, then the issues may be different.

Title: Advice on KVM switch
Post by: rugmonster on June 24, 2005, 06:16:14 am
I have found the KVMs, as a whole, suck, unless you spend a lot of money. I have settled with one that doesn't suck quite as bad as some of the others I've tried. Basically, the problem I have run into in trying to run a Logitech MX1000 (native USB mouse) is that most KVMs don't support the extensions to the signals that are sent through it. I tried a belkin, a d-link and now have some off-brand kvm that includes usb support. So, if you have a PS/2 mouse and you have a scroll wheel, make sure the KVM will support it. I would also recommend finding one that has cables built in, or you will spend a lot of money on video and ps/2 cables. Other than that, the world of KVMs is sort of flakey. Get as much information about the product as possible before buying and be ready to throw a couple away.
