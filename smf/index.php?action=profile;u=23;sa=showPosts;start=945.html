954  Software / Games / Transgaming on: August 14, 2002, 02:56:18 pm
I had a 3 month subscription that just ran out.  I couldn't get really new games to run, especially those that didn't allow you to forgo DirectX installation (IW2).  DeusEx was a bit choppy, but that was on an older video card.  

My advice is to head on over to TuxGames and support the cause.  I seem to remember that CivIII is available as a native Linux app.  

Also, there are some neat games being developed with the Torque Engine, which was called V12 when it was used to develop Tribes 2 (also available for Linux).  This engine is cross-platform and pretty beefy.  At $US100 per developer for the engine, its really attractive to hobbysits and small developers.  The best, IMHO, has got to be Dogs of Prey, a cartoonish flight sim.  Beta is available for Windows only right now, but they say that the Linux version should be ready soon (not like Bioware's version of "soon" either!).
