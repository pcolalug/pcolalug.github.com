Has anyone ever looked at FreeSCO...?

This is a floppy disk based Linux distribution designed to mimic a Cisco router.
It requires some planning in advance, but even so, I was able to configure
a Pentium 60 with an external modem to route internet access for all my network computers, even those running Winblows, to flawlessly run any internet app as though the internet connection was local to the machine by
setting the gateway to the FreeSCO router.

It even comes with a default firewall already set up to give basic protection
which can be modified to suit your personal taste.

By the way, if you put a second network card in the router,
you can connect completely different networks such as 10baseT and 10base2.

I know it works, I ran this configuration for 6 months before my present situation caused me to scrap my router temporarily but when my situation
changes, I will use the same computer as my router once again...

Oh, and for those of you who who have cable or DSL, FreeSCO can also be
configured to use these access methods BUT you are on your own in this regard as no one has posted clear instructions on how to do this to the FreeSCO website to be included in their HOWTO.

However,  The Linux cable modem HOWTO gives some insight into the different cable ISP's and how you need to configure your FreeSCO router
to connect to them...
