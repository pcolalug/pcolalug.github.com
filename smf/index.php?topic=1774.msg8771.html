I received my Soyo barebones kit today and overall I am very happy with it.  I simply installed a spare Duron 1Ghz and low profile heatsink and fan onto the motherboard.

The Good:
+ Cool looking case.  The handle is really quite neat.
+ Everything worked right off the bat with Knoppix 3.4

The So-so:
+ The case, keyboard, cdrom, mouse, etc. all "felt" cheap.

The Ugly:
- Don't expect to put in a monster Thermaltake heatsink and fan as the power supply sits over the CPU.  If I didn't have a server grade low profile heatsink and fan, I would have been forced to wait.  AMD tends to run hot so it requires cooling.

So, I have been playing with Knoppix on this box because I didn't install a hard drive in it yet and may not.  I bought this $20 barebones kit to play with Linux on the desktop a bit more and for me, it's working great after 8 hours or so.  I picked up 512MB of PC2700 ram from Bestbuy this weekend for $59 after a $40 rebate.  I don't remember how much the duron or the heatsink costs, but they were spares, so it was real cheap for me.   The box will cost me about $100, plus or minus a few bucks.

I would say that if you are building a spare, for fun box, and forgive me if I go off thread for a moment, it was worth the "risk."  If you are building a desktop to do day-in and day-out work on, I would look for better quality parts across the board.  I may change my mind if this beast runs well over the next couple of months though...
