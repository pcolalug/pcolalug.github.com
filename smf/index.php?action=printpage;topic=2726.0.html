Title: Project Janus Announced
Post by: imnes on December 07, 2005, 08:53:30 am
Looks like Project Janus is finally coming out, supposedly by the end of the month it will be in OpenSolaris builds.  Janus was the Linux compatibility layer that Sun was talking about when Solaris 10 was coming out.  It allows applications written on RedHat to run on Solaris.  (But I suspect it wouldn't have to actually be written on RedHat, as long as it could run unmodified on RedHat it should work fine).

http://www.cbronline.com/article_news.asp?guid=E2705034-CFA2-4033-B84A-36B7AB4A0E5C

Also mentioned in the article is Sun's ZFS filesystem.  It came out in the latest build of OpenSolaris.  ZFS is a 128-bit filesystem.  It's reportedly faster than current filesystems (UFS) and has very high size limits (individual files limited to about a yottabyte and filesystems to about 16 exbibytes).  http://www.answers.com/topic/comparison-of-file-systems. 

These are considered safe limits (http://www.nabble.com/what-is-our-answer-to-ZFS--t590601.html) because:
Quote
There's a more limiting problem, energy. Assuming that the energy to set
one bit is the energy to reverse the spin of an electron, call that s.
If you have each value of 128 bit address a single byte, then
    T = s * 8 * 2^128   and   T > B
where T is the total enargy to low level format the storage, and B is
the energy to boil all the oceans of earth. That was in one of the
physics magazines earlier this year. there just isn't enough energy
usable to write that much data.

You can't currently boot from ZFS so your / root filesystem has to be on a traditional format, but support is coming (http://blogs.sun.com/tabriz).
