Here is my post from this thread about November topics. These should be simple enough for someone to put together.

Quote
I think something on basic networking/routing would be good. I would be happy to present it over VoIP, but this next month's meeting is impossible for me as I will be out of town. Maybe in the future (January or February?) It would not have to be through the VoIP phone, it could be through TeamSpeak.

If someone else wanted to take the subject here is what I think could be covered. This could take just a few minutes or an hour.

What does a router do? What advantages does a hardware router (Linksys, D-Link) have over a software/Linux router (Smoothwall) and vice versa? Hardware/Software firewalls. Why might you run a software firewall even if you are inside of a network with a hardware one? (There are a couple of good reasons to do so, but unless those reasons apply it is a bad idea). What is the difference between a switch and a hub? What is DMZ and how/why would you us it?

Those are just some ideas. Not all could be covered in one session. I think anyone that has basic networking knowledge could cover this. And, like many things, there will be lots of opinions and these could engender some great discussion of different ideas.
