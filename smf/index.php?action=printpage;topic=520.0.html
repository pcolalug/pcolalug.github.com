Title: Got a question??????
Post by: rugmonster on March 04, 2003, 03:37:44 pm
Trigger, I hate to tell you this, but your ISP may be blocking access to port 80. After the huge outbreak of Code Red and Nimda, a lot of cable providers decided to do a broad block of 80 and several other ports typically used for servers. With this said, call you tech support and ask them if they block any ports or check out their website. Cable definitely has its downfalls because most DSL providers don't do this...of course it doesn't hurt them so much since each customer has his own bandwidth, not shared like cable.

Title: Got a question??????
Post by: rugmonster on March 04, 2003, 03:51:50 pm
Well, the explanation I got from Cox (website) was that they forbid servers because you are paying for a residential service and most users don't know how to run a secure server and can wind up being more of a threat to their network. Yes, it can be a bandwidth hog, but the problem is more that you are paying for a "residential service" and they want to have a reason to charge for "business service". Honestly they are very unlikely to pay you any attention should you run a server. Another thing, nothing is stopping you from running your server on an alternate port such as 81; however, it may end up being a problem for users who are trying to access you from behind a restrictive (read properly setup) firewall.

Title: Got a question??????
Post by: rugmonster on March 04, 2003, 05:47:20 pm
First in your apache config, you will tell it to bind to port 81. Then when you tell someone your site, you give them the url http://<ip or host.domain>:81. By adding the :<port #> it tells the client to send the request to another port.
