Post by: steve on September 05, 2004, 05:24:46 pm
Quote from: dpeach
I know programs get corrupted and have to either be reinstalled or repaired. Do you think this happens to MS Word more often than other programs?


Compared to other apps as in percentage of problems per installed user base,  probably not. (You have to remember, there are a lot of computers running Office out there. How many of them have problems with Office)

I've been working at a local retailer part time for two years supporting computers and of the probably 1000+ computers I've worked on in that time, I've never had a corrupted MS Word.

I've supported 150 Users at my full time job for 5 years now and have seen corrupted Office installs not more than three times. Those were normally due to Virus infections, Users trying to clean up their drives and deleteing needed files,  or trying to add components from Office Disks that aren't the same version as they were running. ie, trying to add components to Office 2000 using Office 2003 CDs.

Prior to that I suspported 900 Users for the Navy on a closed network (No access to the internet and locked down workstations) We had 0 corruptions in Office files.

Office has had  problems in the past and will in the future. But that's why, like most apps on the market, there are knowlege bases for troubleshooting the problems.

I've read where users have continueous probalems with Office, but I normally chalk that up to incompetant users or incampatible hardware/software. Remember, If your car is broke, it doesn't matter what grade of gas you put in, it's not going to run.

Anyway, if you need any help with Office problems, let me know and I'll do what I can.
