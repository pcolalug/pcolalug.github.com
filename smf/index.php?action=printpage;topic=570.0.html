Title: O'Reilly "Safari" book subscription service
Post by: danc on March 23, 2003, 01:28:43 am
Hi all,

   I was selected to receive a trial Safari book service subscription from O'Reilly, through our Linux User Group.  I wanted to write a brief description of the service, and my impressions.
   The service allows you to read (online) any of the over 1000 books that O'Reilly publishes.  You can have up to 10 books on your "bookshelf" at any one time, and can read them in their entirety.  The available books cover all kinds of computer topics, including Linux, Windows, programming, security, and much more.  They are well categorized to help you find what you want.  In addition, there is a GREAT search capability which allows you to search for keywords in ANY of the books.
   As a beginner with Linux, I concentrated on books of that nature, and found a large collection of help and information.  I was able to learn a great deal on the topics that interested me, currently firewalls and Samba.  As a result I have my home network integrated nicely with Linux and Windows, using Samba.  Also I was able to configure a Linux machine as a router/gateway.  While these may seem trivial topics to seasoned Linux users, they are very challenging to newcomers, and I know I couldn't have done it without the aid of the books I read here on Safari.
   Overall I have been very pleased with the Safari service and would recommend it to both beginners and experts.  In fact you can give it a try yourself, for free!  There is a 14-day trial subscription (http://www.oreilly.com/safari/ug) available just for registering, and I'd recommend you check it out.  The website is:  http://safari.oreilly.com.  Go see it, and I hope you get as much from it as I have.
Dan
