Title: Military Use of Linux
Post by: rugmonster on April 21, 2004, 08:58:24 am
This (http://technology.newsforge.com/technology/04/04/20/2229245.shtml) article at Newsforge (http://newsforge.com) brought a question to mind. Basically, the president of the Los Angeles LUG resigned his position because of his objections to linux's use by the US military and other agencies in Iraq.

My view on this is that when you make a technology, there are going to be other uses than what you initially intended. The military uses products that the commercial world uses because they work. Anything that can get the job done, the military will use and since they can get it cheaper with the freedom to customize it, makes it attractive; the same as it does for the commercial world.

Actions like this make no sense to me. Uses of something for good or ill are what you get when you release something into the wild. I'll close with one question. Why did he stop now when China has been producing and using Linux for several years and they have one of the worst human rights records in the world?
