Title: Video card back from the dead
Post by: on February 08, 2004, 09:52:54 am
I just brought a video card back from the dead.  Well it wasn't quite dead, but it had one foot in the grave, and the other on a banana peel.  Here's the story and how I got it working properly again.

I just replaced my video card with an older card this morning.  After I put it in, I noticed a few odd characters during POST messages.  When Linux took over I was getting psychedelic colors and very weird characters.   I took a look at my XF86Config, and everything was set up properly there.  
I then rebooted and started looking at my bios settings.  All the characters in the bios were out of whack.  So thinking the video card was going south, I decided to pull it and put my previous card back in.  

When I pulled the card I noticed that the gold pins on the AGP connector were a little tarnished.  I remembered from my days in the Marine Corps when we had to clean up our field radios, we would use rubber erasers to clean the contact points.  So I whipped out a good old No. 2 and started erasing away on all the contact points on the video card.  It worked like a charm!  Video card now works perfect with no funky colors or characters.  

I just thought that I would share this.  I'll definitely try this again before I toss out any malfunctioning PCI or AGP card in the future.

Cheers,
