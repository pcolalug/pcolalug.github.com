19  Misc / Computer General / Re: GIAC GCFA certified forensics analyst on: September 26, 2009, 04:28:39 pm
Thanks, And you would be partly correct.

First GIAC stands for Global Information Assurance Certification they have maintained a set of standards for attaining and maintaining certifications for the different levels of IT.
 Security Administration, Security Management, IT Audit and Software Security. Mine is in the Security Management GCFA which is GIAC Certified Forensics Analyst.
Which means now that I can perform a criminal investigation on the IT side And recover sensitive data that has been altered or deleted. I can also come in and with some degree of certainty
determine who did what and when. If called to testify I am also now an expert witness vs a Lay witness which means I can express my opinion. Where others can not.
It is a highly sought after certification ranking number 3 in the list of certs.

Taking the test is pretty straight forward. 4 hours 150 questions open book......6 books +three tablets, 70% or better for silver cert. Also cost $750.00 for test that you have to re certify every 4 years.
The boot camp that my boss paid for cost $3,500 +testing and took 6 8 hour days to complete.

Hope that gives some insight to what it is and what it give you.
