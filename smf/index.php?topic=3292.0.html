Linux/Alternate OS Transition Presentation for UWF Community

I figured that I would pass this on and get some input.

Quote
Hello,

My name is Noah Stevens.  I am the President of the University of West
Florida Freethinkers.  I have a couple of questions I'd like to run past
your organization.

I'm trying to lay out our agenda for the Spring semester, and one idea
that occured to me would be to offer a workshop on switching over from
Windows or the Mac OS (proprietary) vs. some of the bajillion free *nix
flavors.  I recently switched from XP to Fedora 8, after having tried in
the past a couple of other distributions.  I think they were Knoppix,
and Peanut Linux and one or two other LiveCD distros.  Anyway, the
switch over's never been easy for me, mostly due to the fact that until
very recently my laptop was not a very "out of the box" friendly
environment for Linux.  But with the last fedora release my drivers all
kicked in without problems, net connections became easier, etc.  I'm
using a Dell Inspiron 9100 and net access required me to download the
NDIS wrappers and compile kernel modules and a host of other things I
was not prepared for.  Hence my interest in help from your organization.

Owing to work conflicts, I probably cannot make this month's meeting.
HOwever, if your organization would be amiable to it, I invite you to
send a representative to demo a couple of user-friendly Linux
installations for members of the campus community.  We don't have our
schedule lined up, but I imagine mid-February might work well for us.

I have a bunch of questions about updating and distribtuting another
LiveCD distro that I found the other day, in the interests of social
activism, but that's another email entirely...

I look forward to hearing from you, and thanks for your time.

Yours,

Noah Stevens
