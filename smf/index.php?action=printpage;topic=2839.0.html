Title: Re: Quicktime-files working for SuSE 10.0
Post by: rugmonster on April 12, 2006, 06:16:33 pm
I have had problems with Quicktime files playing through Mplayer. Unfortunately, at this time, the newer Quicktime files are not supported by any product under Linux. Thank Apple and their closed format. My suggestion would be to contact the site administrator and request they use another format. If it is audio, then ogg would be the ideal solution, mp3 being next. For video, XviD video with ogg audio encoding is ideal (both open source and fully supported), but XviD with mp3 would work too. MPlayer does support a good deal of support for most video and audio formats, just not Quicktime.

For SUSE packages of Mplayer (if they are not available in YaST), you can visit Packman's page (http://packman.links2linux.org/?action=index). He has some of the best packages available for multiple versions of SUSE.

Mplayer (http://packman.links2linux.org/?action=128)
mplayerplug-in (http://packman.links2linux.org/?action=319) (for Mozilla/Firefox to use Mplayer)
w32codecs (http://packman.links2linux.org/?action=046)
