Title: Ubuntu 5.04 (Hoary) is released
Post by: danc on April 08, 2005, 06:44:50 pm
Hoary is final and released today.  Installed and running here on my laptop.  Installation was smooth, things are all working well.  Of special note to me was that my wireless NIC (Atheros chipset, madwifi driver) was installed and working, "out of the box".  First time I've seen that from any distro.  The installer even asked me for the ESSID to use, and the WEP key.  Amazing.

The Synaptic package manager is surprisingly nice.  Used it to install Thunderbird 1.02, very painless.  This distro comes with Gnome 2.10 and kernel 2.6.10, and other stuff is very updated as well.  One thing I'd like to improve is the way fonts look, which is not quite as good as I've seen in Mandrake or Slackware.  Overall though, this is a promising release.

Dan

Post by: rugmonster on April 13, 2005, 05:52:17 am
Quote from: dpeach
Ubuntu is a great distro but they have...things against them...Debian


That distro wouldn't be even close to anything if it weren't based on Debian. Believe it or not, Debian has come a long way in the past couple of years, but the packages have lagged behind. Debian brought us apt. Ubuntu's installer that everyone says works so well (just isn't pretty) is the standard Debian installer now. Ubuntu has only updated the packages of their apt repositories to newer versions with less testing. The structure and some of the core components that make Ubuntu what it is apart from any other distro are Debian all the way.

Debian aims for stability over "cool-factor" in it's stable releases. Testing lags behind still, and SID is sometimes scary to use, but Ubuntu or any other distro aiming to use the newest packages are going to be comparably stable to the Testing or SID branches of Debian anyway. I don't know how many times I've installed officially released packages or installed from scratch and not had something work. With Debian stable, you know it's going to work (if you use it correctly), because it's had tons of testing and evaluation on almost every platform available.

Just because it is current does not mean it is wicked. Face it, we all probably did something heineous on occasion to a few toys.
