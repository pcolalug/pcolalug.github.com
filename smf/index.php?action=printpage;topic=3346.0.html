Headline:   Server Support Needed

 

Principle Duties and Essential Job Functions:

Participates in highly complex system integration projects.  Installs, generates, maintains, tests, debugs, and integrates system software programs with COTS/GOTS applications and support equipment.  Develops instructions for integration of operating system software, and modifies/maintains integration packages via releases and utilities from manufacturers in a multi-operating system and/or network operating system environment. Evaluates existing operating systems/COTS/GOTS software and recommends changes. Optimizes systems software performance. Responsible for applications dealing with the overall operating system, such as sophisticated file maintenance routines, networks, CAD or GIS system software, or mathematical/scientific software packages. Engineers highly complex software that may relate directly to operating systems, their performance or devices controlled.

 

Duties include: Monitor IIS 5 website(s) and SQL 2000 database performance; Monitor Server (Windows 2000, Windows 2003, Active Directory 2003) performance and operational status of server software applications (i.e. Exchange 2003, SQL 2000, Anti-virus, WebTrends); Collect server statistics and document server trends; Manage and validate data backup process; Apply server and workstation security patches as needed to comply with DoD/Navy security mandates; Monitor and manage anti-virus implementation; Provide server support for DANTES-developed software applications; Conduct application performance testing; Maintain and comply with configuration management and release management processes.

 

Basic Qualifications:

Prefer Bachelor’s with four (4) years related experience or eight years in lieu with H.S. Diploma.

 

Skills: IIS, SQL 2000, Windows 2000/2003, Active Directory 2003, Exchange 2003, Anti-virus software, and/or WebTrends.

 

Clearance: Must be able to handle business sensitive, unclassified data IAW federal government security regulations for contractor personnel.

 

 

Headline: Technology/Education Specialist Needed for Navy eLearning Prototype Development

 

Principle Duties and Essential Job Functions:

Senior Technology/Education Integration Specialist leads and participates in highly complex technology/education integration projects.  Is the senior technical expert specialized in integrating distance learning course content with information system components.  Ensures course content will operate effectively in the technical configurations in the integrated learning environment for the Navy.  Maintains liaison with managers and technical personnel in NETC, NETPDTC, and user commands to mediate, negotiate, or secure agreement among various entities to assure highly effective, reliable, and timely support to the distance learning initiatives.

 

Requirement is for technical support in the development and deployment of a Disconnected Solution for the Navy eLearning program. The MPT&E product will deliver courseware to sailors while they are disconnected from the central Learning Management System (LMS).  Provide support to fully disconnected users of downloadable content. Conduct analysis to identify the impact of any proposed enhancement in terms of cost, capability, performance and risk.

 

Basic Qualifications:

Prefer Bachelor’s with eight years related experience information technology, training delivery systems, and/or educational environments or twelve (12) years in lieu with H.S. Diploma.

 

Must be familiar with Sea Warrior (or compatible environment), Integrated Learning Environments, and/or Navy Training Systems.  Knowledgeable of major operating characteristics of training delivery systems, associated computer equipment, networking, system software and support functions.  Knowledgeable of analysis and design techniques to implement training systems and modifications.  Knowledgeable of training delivery systems standards, security, and system software.

 

Able to ensure that learning event data is captured, posted, and replicated to Electronic Training Jackets (ETJ). Provide a process for and conduct secure testing and reporting. Conduct field installation and testing with expeditionary forces and in OCONUS areas without network connectivity. Launch and track SCORM Learning Content while disconnected from the Learning Management System and deploy via the web or a local network.

 

Skills: LMS, LCMS, SCORM, Windows O/S, Training Delivery Systems, Delivery Systems Standards, Security, and/or System Software.

 

Travel may be required to supported Navy training sites.

 

Clearance: Possess or be able to obtain and maintain a SECRET Clearance.

 

Information Assurance Personnel

Principle Duties and Essential Job Functions:

Assist NSTC IAM by reporting security risk incidents to the Navy Cyber Defense Operations Command in accordance with program requirements.  Provide input to NSTC IAM for NSTC-wide policies and procedures to manage the information security/assurance program.  Provide advice in implementation. Assist NSTC IAM with OCRS reporting and tracking.
Provide input to NSTC IAM to identify IA training requirements.  Monitor NSTC activities' and users' compliance and completion of required IA training.  Track completion of all NSTC User Acknowledgement documents.  Assist NSTC IAM and technicians with scanning computers on both the TRANET and NMCI as required by higher authority and network owner and report findings to the IAM.  Monitor required follow up actions. Directly supports the Information Systems Security Manager (ISSM)/Information Assurance Manager (IAM).
Requires ability to directly interface with customers. Uses COTS software in performance of duties.

Qualifications:

Prefer Bachelor’s with five (5) years within the last seven (7) calendar years of intensive and progressive experience in the individual's field of study and specialization or nine (9) years in lieu with H.S. Diploma.
Specialized - The Subject Matter Specialist should possess two (2) years within the last four (4) calendar years of intensive and progressive experience in functional and ADP analysis/programming of subject matter closely related to the work to be automated.

Please note that General and Specialized Work Experience may be earned concurrently.

Key Competencies: Technical Writing, Documentation Standards, Customer Liaison, IA, DITSCAP and IT Infrastructure. Knowledge of, and ability to apply wide range of IT computer security requirements, techniques, methods, and procedures in DON, NETC and NSTC security environments. Ability to conduct pre-scripted training on information security matters

Clearance: Possess or able to obtain and maintain a SECRET Clearance
