Post by: steve on February 02, 2005, 08:36:51 pm
I just did some research over at the PHPbb support forums and found a couple of possible reasons for the "long posting times".

The first possible cause states that it may have to do with someone watching a thread and a problem with the smtp mail software. ie. PHPbb trying to send an email that the thread has been updated.

The second, and more logical reason, states to check and make sure you are running the latest version of PHP with the latest version of Apache. There are reported problems with older versions of PHP running on older versions of Apache 2.x.x.

Here  (http://www.phpbb.com/phpBB/viewtopic.php?t=142826&highlight=submit+long)is the first solution I found.

Here  (http://www.phpbb.com/phpBB/viewtopic.php?t=106159&highlight=submit+long)is the second solution I found.

Hope this helps.

Post by: dpeach on February 03, 2005, 01:49:26 pm
Just my uninformed opinion, it seems that it is probably the email situation. And, it makes sense. The reason the subsequent posts (edits) do not take as long, is that everyone that was watching that topic has already gotten the notification that it has been updated. But when Steve posts after me, it takes a bit for the mail to be sent to me as the only person who is watching the topic that did not get the notification of the previous update (since it does not send notification to the poster that caused the need for the email in the first place). So every new post to the thread that is being watched will take a bit to post, but other posts by the same person to the thread will not take any time. And posts to threads that are not being "watched" will submit quickly.

Seems logical. At least something seriously worthy of looking into.
