I'll tell you that Lindows is a fine choice, based on a co-worker's experience. He's been running it almost exclusively at home for the last 8 months. His teenage daughter and his wife both use it without any hassles and he says that its a pleasure to update and install most packages via the Click-n-Run. From what I've seen, the UI (KDE) is polished and consistant.

You can't go wrong with any of the distro's mentioned here, either. Just be aware that Linux desktop usage is really still in its infancy. There are still some things that you may have to put a bit more effort into than you would in Windows XP (think of the old Win 95 days in some cases). Also, the GUI's you have to choose from won't be exactly like the Window's interface, but you should find your way around in no time. Feel free to get some help from our membership if you run into problems.
