If you or anyone you know is interested in any of these positions feel free to contact me with any questions:

UNIX Administrator

 

Improve, maintain, manage, and operate the core infrastructure systems and storage environment. Requires an active participation in the day-to-day management and operations of the UNIX systems. Provides UNIX system support for Production and Development environments, specifically dealing with major enterprise level portal systems in classified and non-classified systems. Contributes to system architecture improvements. Analyzes and reports system performance metrics. Coordinates system service events with user community. Supports system access at local and remote sites. Writes shell scripts to automate maintenance tasks. Coordinates vendor service requests and tracks support cases. Oversees and implements system upgrade strategies. Participates in the design, implementation, and maintenance of highly complex solutions. Coordinates activities with other technical personnel as appropriate. Implements solutions and refines procedures as appropriate. Researches current systems technology and serves as subject matter expert on complex systems processes and procedures.

 

Performs installation, upgrades, configuration and administration of Solaris, HP-UX and Red Hat Linux systems. Strong knowledge of TCP/IP networking, NFS, SSH, UNIX account management, file permissions, security considerations, and/or clustering technologies required.  Responsibilities include coding and scripting using UNIX scripts, Perl, shell scripts to enable system performance, capacity planning and tuning.  Monitoring and management of infrastructure services such as system availability, DNS, BIND, Veritas NetBackup, Sendmail and/or SysLogging.

 

Prefer understanding of Storage Area Networks, Fiber Channel Switched Fabric, data replication techniques, and limitations and benefits of each. Excellent understanding of disaster recovery components and strong leadership skills desired.

 

Clearance: Possess or able to obtain and maintain a SECRET Clearance.

 

 

Content Delivery Specialist

 

Principle Duties and Essential Job Functions:

Participates and coordinates highly complex integration projects involving course content delivery. Validate and document functionality, compatibility and integration of all content.  Maintains liaison with managers and technical personnel to assure highly effective, reliable, and timely support to the distance learning initiatives. Prefer knowledge of major operating characteristics of associated computer equipment, networking, system software, and support functions.  Knowledge of analysis and design related to implementation of systems and modifications.  Knowledge of training delivery systems standards, security, and system software, a plus.

 

Requires a BS and 4 years of experience.

 

Provides support services in developing life-cycle support applications and tools for the for Navy learning content management team. Assist in coordinating the delivery of approved learning content for archival in the Navy's learning content repository.  Candidate should be competent in programming with web-based technologies such as DHTML & JavaScript as well as web-based content life-cycle management practices such as content management. Candidate will work closely with developing solutions to support the deployment and conversion of content to web-based applications used for Navy eLearning such as the Learning Management System (LMS) and the Learning Content Management System (LCMS).

 

Clearance: Possess or able to obtain and maintain a SECRET Clearance.

 

Learning Assessment System Project and Release Manager

 

Principle Duties and Essential Job Functions:

This position will provide oversight and functional recommendation for the implementation of a Learning Assessment System (LAS).  Individual will be directly responsible for ensuring configuration management standards are maintained and that established business processes are adhered to through the life-cycle of this application.  Individual will maintains a liaison with managers and technical personnel related to NETC/NETPDTC to mediate, negotiate, or secure agreement among various entities to assure highly effective, reliable, and timely support to the distance related initiatives.

 

Basic Qualifications:

Prefer Bachelor's with eight  years related experience information technology, training delivery systems, and/or educational environments or twelve years in lieu with H.S. Diploma.  Prefer at least three years experience managing complex projects.

 

Experience detailing functional requirements and creating traceability matrices for completion of project testing events.  Experience in configuration management techniques utilized associated with maintenance of applications and/or services.  Understanding of system architecture and networking (i.e., routers, firewalls, switches, etc.)  Candidate must be able to address system architecture and network configurations in detail within documentation associated with application for use when assembling a C&A package.  Knowledge of risk mitigation and contingency planning required.  Demonstration of strong communication and organization skills required.  Familiarity with IA standards and system compliancy requirements and with testing techniques and ability to manage Functional Acceptance Test (FAT), Application System Integration Test (ASIT), and Performance test events.

 

Skills:  MS Office with an emphasis on Project, Viso, PowerPoint and Word.  CMMI knowledge is a plus. 

 

Clearance: Possess or be able to obtain and maintain a SECRET Clearance.
