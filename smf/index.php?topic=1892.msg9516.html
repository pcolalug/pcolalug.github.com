Having just built a system earlier this year, I would suggest not skimping on video card. I spent a good deal on a Radeon 9800XT and plan on buying a new motherboard and pci-x video card, with plans on new processor the following spring.

My theory on building a gaming system, start with your video card and buy as much as you can afford now. Geforce 6800 GDDR 128MB (not GT or Ultra) are running about $270. Save up a little more since it seems you are buying piece by piece and it will go a long way.

If you're planning on making this a gaming system, you need to make sure you have a quality hard drive. There is a good bit of research I did in choosing my drive as I can tell a huge difference between a cheaper 80GB in one of my systems and the 200GB Seagate I have in my A64 box. It definitely makes a difference in games like Doom3.

Also, I bought a cheap power supply and had terrible power issues. Voltage was very unstable, so I spent about $100 on an Antec Truepower and haven't looked back.

When you say "gaming" system, you are opening yourself up to a higher level of spending. If you expect to run the newer games, video card and system memory are probably the two most important factors, followed by hard drive and processor. Last year, I was playing ET on a P3 550 with 512 MB SDRAM, 17 GB 5400 RPM IBM drive and GeforceFX 5200.

Gentoo has always been geared as a make it yourself. I think they still call it a metadistribution because you're basically doing LFS with the aid of Portage. The difference is you don't have to type ./configure --option --option... & make & make install a bunch. While there have been a couple of installers made for it to do binary packages (they exist), most people who use Gentoo don't care for them. If you want something that is going to be up in a short amount of time, with not as much work, go with a binary distro like SuSE on that A64.
