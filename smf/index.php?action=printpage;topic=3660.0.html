Post by: danc on March 06, 2010, 10:22:24 am
Greetings, Jim, and welcome to Pensacola and the LUG.  Glad to see new faces show up around here.

Since you are new to Linux, I would recommend that you stick with Ubuntu, and not bother with trying to load Debian.  It won't act any differently than Ubuntu, and is not nearly as user-friendly.  Once you get a little more experienced you could certainly try that, or some other Linux distribution, but for now Ubuntu will do anything that you want to do.

Using Linux does not require *ANY* programming/coding ability.  It is not needed at all.  Using commands (and man pages) is not the same thing as coding.  I recently gave a presentation to the LUG regarding (basic) use of the command line, and you can download your own copy of it (it's a slideshow in OpenOffice format) here:  http://members.cox.net/danc3/docs/cli_basic2.odp (http://members.cox.net/danc3/docs/cli_basic2.odp)   That should get you some ideas and a start into using commands in a terminal.

As for GRUB...  the most recent version of Ubuntu is using a new version of GRUB, which is quite different than the "original" GRUB that many of us learned.  It isn't real difficult to configure/edit, and here is a link to get you started on that:  https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Grub2 (https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Grub2)

Feel free to ask any further questions that you may have, and think about attending our next scheduled LUG meeting, which should be either 4/15 or 4/17 (more news on that to follow here on the forums sometime soon).

Again, welcome and enjoy!

Dan
