Title: DALNET bans Filesharing Specific Channels
Post by: steve on February 04, 2003, 06:17:45 pm
I look at this as a liability issue. Of course this brings up a whole new debate on who is liable for what. I, however, look at internet liability in this fashion. (This is all based on the assumption that the servers are hosted in the US.)

So, one asks is the phone company liable for illegal conversations over the phone lines? Is the Post Office liable for illegal activities taking place through the mail. The answer is NO because both Mail and Phone conversation are private and the hosting companies would have to invade your privacy in order to find out what you're doing.

On the other side of the coin, if drug trafficing is taking place in a bar, out in the open and the owners are aware of it, then are they Liable? YES. A bar is a public place and the owners have knowledge that it's taking place. They also have the means to stop it. In addition, if people start posting notices on the bulletin boards in Winn Dixie advertising drugs available or looking for drugs, Winn Dixie has a responsibility to remove those notices.

I look at IRC like a bar. (Some of the same type of people too....) It is a public place with illegal activities taking place out in the open. The hosting company is aware of it. It is their responsibility to try to put a stop to ALL known illegal activities taking place on their server. Oh, and just because the illegal files don't actually traverse their server IMHO they would be considered accessaories because the "deal" is being made through their server. Their server is being used as a bulletin board for setting up these deals. Solution, take down the illegal bulletins.
