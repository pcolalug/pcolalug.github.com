I'm getting out of the AF because of the cutbacks. Since the majority of the GS position in this area are associated with the AF, there are planned cuts for them as well. This means that my outlook on GS is not so bright and shiny.

GS used to be a sweet gig. The stability was rock solid, pay was pretty good and the benefits were outstanding. They've cut back on the benefits, though they are still better than most private sector jobs. The pay is okay and you get a pension, but you can make more in the private sector and build a better retirement on your own (because you'd have to do that anyway if you planned on retiring from a GS position). I'm not going to turn down a GS job, but I really don't want to work for the government any more. It wears on you and I'm tired of feeling mostly unsatisfied and less than challenged in my job.

One more thing...GS positions typically supervise the contractors, so you end up being a project manager and I enjoy being a techie.
