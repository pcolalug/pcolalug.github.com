Governor Bush Welcomes American Water Services Company To Northwest Florida ~International company chooses Escambia County, brings 220 high-value jobs to Pensacola-Region~

TALLAHASSEE - Governor Jeb Bush today welcomed the American Water Services Company, Inc., an international leader in providing water and wastewater services, to its new home in Pensacola. Locating its customer service and technical support operations center in Escambia County, American Water Services Company, has committed to create 220 high-value jobs and make a total capital investment of $16 million.

"We are thrilled American Water has chosen to locate its customer service and technical support operations in Escambia County," said Governor Bush. "Part of a reputable international company, American Water's expansion into Florida demonstrates the competitiveness and attractiveness of our communities in the world market. Certainly, this project will create many excellent jobs for the citizens of Florida's Great Northwest, and in turn, the region's dedicated workforce will enable American Water to forge great success for many years to come."

American Water Services Company Inc.'s, origin can be traced back to 1886 when the American Water Works and Guarantee Company was founded. In January 2003, the company was acquired by RWE, a major European utilities provider and became American Water Works Company. American Water Services Company, the business unit for the Escambia County project, is a subsidiary of American Water Works Company.

"The additional capacity of our customer service center in Pensacola is part of our service excellence strategy," said Jeremy Pelczer, president and CEO of American Water. "We look forward to further growing our business in Florida and to a long-term, mutually beneficial partnership with the state."

American Water serves 20 million customers in 27 states, four Canadian provinces, Puerto Rico, and South America. More than 8,000 employees provide water, wastewater, and other services to the company's customers. RWE's water and wastewater division is the third largest in the world.

"This is a great success story for the Pensacola Region and demonstrates how effectively our state and local business expansion and location incentives work," said State Representative Holly Benson. "The combination of a quality workforce and economic development incentives were the key for the company selecting Escambia County."

"This presented itself as an excellent opportunity for Escambia County to partner with the State and our local Chamber to bring quality jobs and investment to our community," said Escambia County Commission Chairman, Bill Dickson.

The Governor's Office of Tourism, Trade, and Economic Development worked closely with Enterprise Florida, Inc., Escambia County, and the Pensacola Chamber of Commerce Committee of 100 to secure this project for the region and state.
