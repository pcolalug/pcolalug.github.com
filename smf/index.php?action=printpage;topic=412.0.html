Sorry, late posting this also, but, better late than never...

Yes,  I will post the correct pinouts, but this means I will have to open up
the test cable I made and sit down with a volt-ohmeter to find out exactly
what I did, and then, clearly write out in text instructions which DB9 pins
connect to which DB25 pins.

I feel this is the best way to explain how to properly wire the cable because I have already searched the internet and found that all cable
pinout descriptions are in ASCI format and without an explanation of what wires connect where an ASCI diagram is worthless...

I will emphasize one fact, right now...

DO NOT use a 10 strand cable over 50 feet long!

The working cable connection I was using was 50 feet long and there was
a definite lag in speed, but, if you research this on your own, I was pushing the extreme limit of a serial port connection by doing this...

Oh, and by the way, the Loaves and Fishses Thrift store on  Palafox has
2 Wyse terminals without keyboards...

So, if you feel adventurous, pick up on of these terminals and find a keyboard, and you will get a glimpse of computer history...
