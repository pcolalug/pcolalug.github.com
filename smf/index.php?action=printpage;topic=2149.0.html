Title: Unreal GOTY problem.
Post by: danc on January 01, 2005, 12:58:37 am
Lee,

Seems it may be related to the "speedstep" functions of a laptop, where the CPU frequency is changed according to the current load on the system.  When you installed the game, there wasn't much load on the system, and now when running the game (CPU heavily loaded), the frequency goes up and makes everything too fast.

Not sure what the complete fix is, but might require disabling the "speedstep" functions (maybe in the BIOS?) and reinstalling the game.  Sometimes this stuff is called "CPU Scaling" I think...

You might find more helpful info in these links, which I have only skimmed over:

http://tinyurl.com/5s7w9

http://tinyurl.com/5kv8h

Good luck!

Title: Unreal GOTY problem.
Post by: rugmonster on January 01, 2005, 02:21:18 am
The key is in frequency scaling. In your kernel config you can enable or disable cpu frequency scaling under the acpi settings (under power management). You can then control your frequency scaling through the /proc or /sys fs. You should be able to find out more in your kernel documentation.

Post by: danc on January 01, 2005, 09:28:08 am
Cool!  Glad you got it working.  You may be able to correct what UT thinks your CPU speed is with a console variable.  Try opening a console in the game with the "~" key, and type in:  CPU_SPEED=2000  (where the number is in Mhz, 2000 meaning 2.0 Ghz), adjust to your machine.
