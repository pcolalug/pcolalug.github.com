Title: Re: VNC Login session question.
Post by: lloydr on January 09, 2006, 01:51:59 pm
your problem is probably similiar to what I discussed in my VNC + SSH presentation. Basically what you want is VNC to be running on display :0 and what is probably happening is vnc starts a new xsession at :1

This usually comes down to the vnc server you are using. there are specific servers that are made to run directly on :0 such as x11vnc
http://www.karlrunge.com/x11vnc/

Code:
x11vnc -display :0
will be the command you run, and away you go.

occasionally the included vnc servers with distros have issues with sharing display :0.
So far the vnc server included with Freerock Gnome for Slackware has worked great running libvncserver (http://libvncserver.sourceforge.net/)

With sharing :0 with your monitor at home, everything you do on your screen at home that you left up will show up on the vnc session and vice-versa. An example would be leaving up say xchat, gaim, and thunderbird while you are at home. When you vnc into your machine to :0 you will see xchat, gaim, and thunderbird still running and on your display exactly how you left it at home. Any changes you make while on vnc will be the same as if you were sitting at your machine.

Hopefully this is what you were talking about  :)
