What would you consider a must-have extension for Firefox? You don't have to be limited to one or two.

Here are my picks.

GoogleBar If you are satisfied with the search that is natively supplied with Firefox then you have not seen the light. The thing I use most about it is when you google a term, then the term becomes a button that allows you to quickly search the page.
Tabbrowser Preferences Advanced control of tabs.
Gmail Notifier Just got this one today. When your browser is open it will check your Gmail account and let you know of any new messages. There is an icon that is docked at the bottom right hand corner of the browser that you can click and it will take you to your account. There is a Windows based stand alone program that does this, but I have not seen anything for Linux. This takes care of both platforms.
Dictionary Search You can highlight any word on a page and then right click it. A new tab will open to www.dictionary.com and show you the definition.
Google Preview I can't really say that this is a must have, but it is kinda cool. It gives you a thumbnail of the page in your Google searches. Helpful if you have been to a page before, but don't remember which one in your search it was, but you remember the page layout.
Web Developer A nice tool that gives you several features that are helpful when building pages. Validation (HTML and CSS) as well as several other things it will do. There are other tools that do the same thing, it just puts it all in a nice package. And it basically sends everything through www.w3.org.

That is what I have on my browser. What are some of you sporting in your own customized browsers?

I would have to add adblock.  Allows you to create custom filters on iframes, urls, images, etc, and block out content from such.

Adblock also has a counter-part, flashblock.  The name pretty much describes what this one does -- it blocks flash objects from loading until you decide to toggle them.  Good for not only blocking out flash ads,  but also if you're on a slow connection and/or slow machine that can't handle flash.


Another plugin that I'm partial to is Editcss.  This extension creates a sidebar which allows you to do on-the-fly editing of css in pages that you've loaded.  Quite nifty if you deal with CSS.
