Post by: danc on September 01, 2005, 05:47:27 am
Keith,

I am using a Netgear WG511T card (PCMCIA) in my laptop, and it works great.  The key to wireless in Linux is the card chipset.  Mine uses the "Atheros" chipset.  I know you're looking for a PCI card for a desktop, and I haven't used those.  Other chipsets that are known to work well are Orinoco, Proxim, and Prism/Prism2.  It can be hard to find out the chipset prior to buying, as they don't always put that info on the box.  Best idea is to do some Googling first.

Good luck with it and ask again if  you have more questions.

Dan

Post by: rburt3 on September 03, 2005, 07:15:59 am
Definitely head danc's words. Don't go and get just any card, as linux support for the chipsets he mentioned have solid support. If you were in a pinch, you could use ndiswrapper to force a Windows driver to work, but it would not be fun, nor guarenteed to work correctly.

As for ease of setup, that is a bit of a mixed bag and depends greatly on the distrobution you are using, but will be consistant across any card you choose.

FWIW, I'm using a Toshiba MiniPCI card with an Atheros chipset and it works like a charm.
