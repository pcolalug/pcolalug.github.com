Just a couple of random comments:

* KDE's nice fonts should have "come" with the newer releases of KDE assuming
you installed the nicer fonts into your distro. (There are *alot* of different fonts for X to grab and play with if you don't like the fonts on your screen at any given moment.)

* The most probable reason your monitor shut off is because you adjusted your time forward more than the timeout for your screensaver/power management is set to (ie: 10 minutes). So as soon as the new time was set and the screensaver checked the idle time, it sees hours. This may not be it, but it's easy to test (try changing the time in smaller increments, shutting off your screen saver, etc).

* I am a WineX subscriber, and it's treated me well.  I was highly addicted to CounterStrike for a while (ie most of college), and when Valve introducted the multiplayer copyprotection patches,  WineX became my gateway back to my addiction.  I have noticed that with some games, however, I have better luck with the CVS Wine rather than WineX. Usually I try both on most programs to test the results.  Either, it's definitely worth the $5/month.

* I second Rugmonster in offering Debian support, which, of course, you can use kde, gnome, xfce (, or none of the above) with. I've recently switched off of a custom CTWM setup in lieu of oroborus + gnome2 in debian with an aqua-style theme. So far it's been pretty pleasant and almost as practical as my last setup.
