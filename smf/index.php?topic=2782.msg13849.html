As a former Aerospace engineering student, I think i can answer your question.

Lift is the upward force on the wings that allows planes to fly.  Lift is generated by thrust.  Thrust is generated via the air rushing through the turbines or propellers, creating an equal and opposite force.  These are the things that allow a plane to fly.  You must consider, the conveyor has almost no effect, as the wheels are free to rotate.  The plane will take off.  I would like to see a 747 on such a conveyor belt.  LOL, after about 20 seconds the thrust from its beastly engines it will rip up asphalt, I can just imagine what it would to some flimsy conveyor belt.

1.  THRUST 296 kN * 4 engines (woah)

2.  LIFT (derived from thrust) has to be greater than the force of gravity on the plane

3.  Weight (w = m * 9.81m/s^2)  in this case 435.4 tons

4.  Drag  Yes folks, at speeds above 60mph drag becomes a problem, which is why planes are aerodynamic, and why trucks have crapastic gas milage.

Now, with that said, I do have a couple of nitpicks with some previous comments:


Quote
2. LIFT (derived from thrust) has to be greater than the force of gravity on the plane

LIFT is not derived from thrust.  It comes from the differential air pressure above/below a wing, caused by airflow over the wing (the increased distance that air has to travel over the top part of the wing).  You can get that without "thrust".  An example would be a model plane on the ground in a wind tunnel, tied by a string to the front of the tunnel.  If you start the wind blowing, the model will become airborne, even though it has no "thrust" of its own.


Quote
4. Drag Yes folks, at speeds above 60mph drag becomes a problem, which is why planes are aerodynamic, and why trucks have crapastic gas milage.

Drag can be a problem at speeds much less than 60mph.  It is an issue at *any* speed actually, and affects an airplane from the moment it starts to move forward.  An example would be a very small single engine plane (such as a Piper Cub with a 60hp engine) which has a takeoff speed of less than 60mph.  I have actually flown one of these, and believe me, drag is an issue.
