Title: Helping friend with laptop purchase.
Post by: bhoff on November 06, 2005, 01:51:06 pm
Hey had a moment thought I would relate my experience helping my friend buy a new laptop.
We had traveled to Circuit City, Best Buy and finally to Sam's Club.....Yea you heard me I would have never guesed that one.
We looked @ sony....way over priced but by far the slimest design, gateway which has a 64bit, HP which has some nice lappy's since last time I looked.
He translates 2 dimensional house drawings to cad flat files for the people to print out. And going to a job in Montana where he is taking over a cad design job.
So his primary concerns were speed and desktop realestate. After 3 hours of shopping He finally settled on an HP from "Sam's Club"
It is a 3gighz P4,2 gig ddr dual channel ram, 100 gig hd 4200rpm *note most laptop's only have a 4800rpm hd some like tecra and toshiba have 5400 rpm the only company we found with 7200 rpm were Dell.,6-1 smart media reader, dual layer dvd r/w/cd r/w combo, 17 screen, built in 54g all the standard bells and whistles.
for a measly $1700.00 with warranty......

Well I have been playing with it and it seem real nice a little hefty at 9.6 lbs.
I also had him buy a shiny new superG wireless router for us to play with since he now has a laptop :)

Brett
