Title: CIS Major
Post by: rugmonster on January 02, 2005, 10:03:17 am
From everything I've read, it is all going to depend on what you want to do. CIS isn't going to put you into the programming world, where a lot of the jobs are being outsourced. You also won't be working a call center, so that puts you somewhere in the great realm of administration. As long as companies keep servers and networks going in their offices, you will have a job as an admin. Even if you go to work for some place that does outsourced administration (for a local area), you'll still have a job. Not everything is going to go overseas.

One thing that I see on the horizon and perhaps this is just a dream is this. Yes, outsourcing to cheap employment overseas sounds great on paper. Talking to a manager type for a software dev company, he hates it. He says you may pay a programmer in the states the same as four programmers over in India, but you get the same output from that one programmer in the states as you would from those four in India. Not to mention the difficulties with languages and the time differences and the cultural differences. I think society here in the states is going to get fed up with losing jobs to cheap labor abroad and finally realize that there are things our government can do to put pressure on these companies to keep jobs here. Either that or we will all be looking at hard times, not just in the world of computers, but across the board.

As a Fall 2004 UWF CIS Graduate, there are jobs to be found. The question is, what are you willing to accept for pay. Fortunately, my case is extremely skewed, as I have over 19 years experience in IT & programming jobs when I decided (actually, my employer requested) that I finish my degree so that my resume could be used in proposals where a degree is a requirement. The difference between a large pizza and someone with no prior experience is that the pizza will feed a family of four. In Pensacola, expect starting salaries around the $30-35k mark. When I began with my employer, even with my 14 years experience, I was still in the 30's. If you are looking to start somewhere in the $50k+ relatively close by, look towards Atlanta, St Petersburg, Orlando and Tampa. I've gotten offers in the $125k range in Tampa, but from what I can tell it is not a cheap place to live, and I had a bad vibe about the prospective employer. Otherwise I'd probably not be here in Pensacola now.

Locally, your track at school means less than the degree itself. If your major is computer-related (CIS, CS, CE, yadda^2), you could be doing just about anything from programming to admin. If you're lucky, you'll get to go over to Corey and do neat crypto stuff. If not, the worst case is that you get some experience to add to the CV when you graduate and can be reasonably assured of a job with the government if you don't/can't find anything else.
