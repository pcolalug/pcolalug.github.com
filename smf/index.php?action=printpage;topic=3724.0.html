Title: Re: I'm new to linux
Post by: sontek on November 05, 2010, 01:54:49 pm
On the command line you'll just learn to make tasks quicker as you need them... its hard to just learn tools and remember how to use them without having an actual problem to use them with.

But the big tools you'll want to play with are find, grep, cut, and awk.   

You can also read about bash scripting here: http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Bash-Prog-Intro-HOWTO.html

Outside of that, if you have any things you are doing in the GUI right now and wonder how to handle it in CLI just ask here and we'll let you know the alternative ways to do it!

Title: Re: I'm new to linux
Post by: Yossarian on November 09, 2010, 07:46:55 pm
Welcome to Linux!  A good starting point is the Linux Starter Pack, a nifty little guide for beginners.  It's got a good list of different programs, but it's not very advanced in terms of using the terminal.

http://www.tuxradar.com/linuxstarterpack

For the most part you won't really need to use the CLI if you're just getting your feet wet.  The CLI may seem intimidating at first but really it's not that difficult.  After you learn basic navigation I suggest installing a few games and other terminal-based applications.

http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/play-games-inside-your-linux-terminal/
