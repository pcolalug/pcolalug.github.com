Hi, all,

I wanted to respond to mikegrb's comments though, not being a member of Pensacola Linux User's Group, I couldn't respond to his original thread in the "Club discussions" section.

...I have decided to leave Freenode indefinitely due to a great philosophical disconnect between what Freenode set out to be and what, in reality it has become. In addition, lilo's extreme disconnect with reality has aided in this decision....

I'm the head of staff and founder of freenode, and I'm also the founder of the projects which led up to it, OPN and linpeople. Mike has never been involved in the founding or operation of the network and he does not seem to understand what it has set out to be or how it is intended to function. The philosophy and policies of freenode today are consistent with what they were in the early beginnings of the project, in 1994, and are set out on its web site ( http://freenode.net/ ). I invite you to read about us if you're interested. I won't respond to Mike's comment about my "extreme disconnect with reality" other than to say that ad hominem comments such as this are easy to make, sound awful and are difficult to refute, since it's never quite clear what they mean.

....I would like to take this opportunity to invite the group to move the IRC channel to the Open and Free Technology Community (OFTC). OFTC has been around for about four years and averages a bit over two thousand users. OFTC may be found on the web at http://www.oftc.net or via IRC at irc://irc.oftc.net....

Mike is a staff member of OFTC, a network set up by ex-OPN staffers, which competes with freenode for its user base. Freenode has been around for nine years, first as irc.linpeople.org, then as irc.openprojects.net, then in its present form as freenode since 2002. At peak times of day during the week we have over 24,000 clients. We provide channels on an official basis to the Gentoo Foundation, Red Hat, Inc., the Debian Project, the Apache Software Foundation, the Free Software Foundation and various other groups.

lilo is Rob Levin the person who all the monetary donations to Freenode go to without any form of oversight....

This is incorrect. Freenode is a program of Peer-Directed Projects Center ( http://freenode.net/pdpc.shtml ), which is an IRS 501(c)(3) (not-for-profit, tax-exempt) corporation, as documented on Guidestar ( http://www.guidestar.org/search/report/gs_report.jsp?ein=74-3033697 ), created for educational and charitable purposes.  It is a legal entity, and as such it has a board of directors.  I am one of four directors.  Our best-known director is Seth Schoen ( http://www.eff.org/about/staff/#seth_schoen ), Staff Technologist at the Electronic Frontier Foundation. The board of directors of PDPC is responsible for its oversight. Donations to PDPC go into its bank account and are disbursed solely in accordance with resolutions passed by the whole of the board of directors.

....The man is unemployed...

This is untrue as well. I'm a W-2 employee of Peer-Directed Projects Center, which is admittedly a pretty small organization. My salary is $16,000/year, and I support myself and two dependents on that salary. PDPC's budget for fiscal 2005-2006 is currently projected at about $32,000 for the year, including about $7,000 allocated from our current fundraising campaign to go to UNICEF's tsunami relief fund.

...and has a family,...

This is a correct, factual statement.

...using donations for the IRC network for personal gain,...

This is patently false. Beginning last October, I became a paid employee of PDPC, and as a direct result I abstained from all votes pertaining to our yearly budget for fiscal year 2004-2005. To vote on my own salary would be a conflict of interest. I receive no inappropriate remuneration from Peer-Directed Projects Center, and in point of fact my main "employee benefits" are EBT (food stamps) and Medicaid for my family.

...google his name you will find quite a bit of information on his lack of understanding of computers, networking, and even IRC!....

I'd be interested in knowing how long Mike has been using and working on IRC.  I've been programming computers since 1968 (I started out in Dartmouth BASIC, using a 33ASR TTY over a phone line connected to GE TymeSharing) and I've been using IRC since 1993. When our forerunner project became a distinct network in 1995, as irc.linpeople.org, I maintained our first server code (which was a modified ComStud tree). I put quite a bit of time into maintaining the modified ircu we used next, and have provided patches to our current (modified hybrid) code, as well as doing quite a bit of the feature design work.

It's true that you will find some unpleasant things written about me on the web. In particular, you can look at the "Lilo-Free Network" website ( http://www.lilofree.net/ ), which seems to have collected most of the unfriendly material which has ever been written about me. I've been working on freenode and the projects which led up to it since 1994, and have collected some people with personal grudges, about the number you'd expect considering that the network has grown to over 24,000 clients at peak times.  Lilofree is a great source of biased material about me. But if you look closely when you google, you'll also find positive comments people have made about me, and comments I've made in a variety of settings on a variety of subjects. I try to remain philosophical about the negative stuff.

It's definitely your choice whether Pensacola Linux User's Group remains on freenode, but I can tell you that we're happy to have you there. We're not the most conventional IRC network, but we've always tried to provide a friendly, cooperative environment for the groups and individuals making the network their home.  There are other, more traditional IRC networks; OFTC, whatever its origins, has a number of good channels and has a reputation for being well-maintained. Whatever you decide to do, I want to thank you on behalf of the staff for having made us your network up to this point.

Respectfully yours,

Robert Levin
Executive Director, PDPC
