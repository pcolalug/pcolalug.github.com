Title: Google - The Ultimate Linux Workplace
Post by: imnes on March 16, 2003, 09:51:58 am
If you like linux machines you'll love Google.  This article is slightly outdated by their current numbers, but it will give you an idea of what they have.

Here's some highlights:
When this article was written google was processing 13,000,000 searches a day.  That number is over 200,000,000 now.  So keep that in mind, the rest of the numbers have grown as well.

Google employs clusters of inexpensive small single processor intel x86-based machines.  These machines each of between 256mb and 1gb of Ram, and dual hard drives (either 22gb each or 40gb each).  At the time of this article they had about 3500 machines and 7000 hard drives powering their search engine.  (I think they surpassed 10,000 servers in 2001).

All these machines run on Linux.  (A modified distribution of RedHat last I heard).  Using custom load-balancing and management software engineered by google programmers.

I haven't seen a pic of their servers but they have to be tiny.  They can fit 80 servers in a 7' rackmount configuration.

Here's the article from Intel.  Google had a much better and more up-to-date one on their site a few months back but I can't find it again.
http://www.intel.com/eBusiness/casestudies/snapshots/google.htm
