I would like to add one more off topic post about Alan's resume.

Have you thought about taking your resume and performance reports/awards/decs/etc to a professional resume writer that specializes in military veteran services? I'm probably going to spend the money to have someone who specializes in that sort of thing have a once over of my resume.

I've also been hearing this as a rule of thumb for lengths of resumes. If you have less than 5 years experience, keep it one page. If you have more than 5 and less than 10, two is appropriate. More than 10, you use what you need. With that in mind, keep things brief and it may be a good idea to still have a very brief one pager and an extended one. Also, don't think a single resume will cut it. Taylor your resume to the types of jobs you are applying for. For example, if I apply for a LAN/WAN network admin job working on Cisco routers and switches, I'm not going to be emphasizing all of my Windows experience. I'll be focusing on all of the stuff I know about Cisco and networking in general. Show your diversity of knowledge, but don't over saturate them with buzzwords.

Alan,

It sure sounds to me like your resume is not written correctly.  I highly recommend you get someone to help you improve it.

There are services like that available free in some places, and for nominal fees elsewhere.  One place you should look into is the "Escarosa Job Service" (or something similar to that).  It's located down on "L" Street as I recall.  They do job-search training, resume writing, veteran's assistance, etc...  all free.

A few months ago I retired from the Navy (electronics tech), and spent a lot of time getting the resume just right.  I posted it on all the large job sites, and the job I eventually got was a result of someone seeing me on Monster.com.  Try posting to places like that, if you haven't (including usajobs.com for Civil Service jobs).

Have you thought about taking a lesser job in the meanwhile, just to keep something coming in as income?  Places like Walmart, HomeDepot, and even fast food, are always hiring.  It's not something you'd want to do forever, but it will pay a few bills.

Again, try to come to one of our LUG meetings.  We have a lot of expertise on this subject, and someone will be glad to help you.  Personally I can't be at the meeting next weekend, but I'll be at the next one if you want to bring your resume.  It sounds like we have a similar background, so perhaps I can help you get going.
