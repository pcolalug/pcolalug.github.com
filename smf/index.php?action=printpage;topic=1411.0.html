I guess I will throw in my 2 cents as well.

*The bullet points are great down the left side, although you can drop the period, I think, before New Visions and make it one complete bullet point.

*Not that I am in the thought police, but I really think that Linux advocacy should be strongly based on Linux's strengths avoiding things like "Trash Windows."  Focus on the lack of virus issues, flexibility, killer applications like Evolution, Mozilla with fantastic spam filtering and popup blockers, KDE 3.2, and a lot of the new stuff coming out of Gnome.  (Not all on one flyer, of course, I mean generally.)  Then focus on Linux's killer feature - the word free.  You can do updates for free or the minimal purchase of media.  Heck even the commercial distros are still extremely cheap compared to other platforms and their OS'.  You can typically get access to the source code and do what you want with it.  Focus on Linux's superior environment for a) learning about computing and programming and b) networking.  (All of which applies to the BSD's as well.)

I have a sense of humor and think that the graphic is both neat & funny; however, negative messages can turn off newbies and the curious -- even disgruntled Windows users.

I think I like the first flyer much better than the second, if I had my pick.

Thanks.
