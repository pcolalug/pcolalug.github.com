32000 is alot. I don't think that I would ever have a use for something like that. And if I did, a database would be a much better solution. But... in this case, where someone else has prepared the information, you don't have a choice. And if you cannot read it, then you are stuck.

I don't think this effects too many people on a regular basis. But there are two workarounds if you are building the spreadsheet yourself.

Let's say you had a sheet that was 15 columns by 47000 rows. One workaround is to have 32000 rows of 15 columns and then slide your columns over to have another 15000 rows of 15 columns. This would be "newspaper" style.

The other way is to link your spreadsheets. So that you have 32K rows in one sheet and then have it linked to another spreadsheet that contains the other 15K rows.

Those are workarounds and really don't solve the problem. The problem is that there is a 32K row limitation. They are working on it for the next version.

Personally if it gets to be over a few comlumns and about 50 rows, I get real confused and just get my wife to do it.
