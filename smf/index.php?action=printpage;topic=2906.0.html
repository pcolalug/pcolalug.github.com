Title: Re: Thumbdrive rant
Post by: rugmonster on June 07, 2006, 04:00:41 pm
In Windows, it more or less "removes the device" in that it does the same thing as ejecting the disk. It unmounts it and turns the power off to it. I guess you just have to know that you unmounted the filesystem under linux. Obviously, if you don't get an error when you give it the umount command, then you are safe. Typically, unless you are using the sync option for the fs, you won't have problems with just pulling it without unmounting it first, but it's still recommended to do so first.
