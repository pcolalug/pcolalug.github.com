Title: BASH startup process
Post by: danc on November 08, 2005, 10:17:49 am
Ever wonder what really happens when you log in to a BASH shell, or when you open a "terminal" from within Xorg?  A certain sequence of files is read (which you can edit to your preferences), to set things such as your shell prompt, PATH settings, and other useful stuff.

Below is a link that explains the process, which is quite well done and easy to understand.  It is referenced to SuSE a little, but most of it is quite portable to any other distro.  Good reading to better understand the bash login process.

Read it here:  http://developer.novell.com/wiki/index.php/Bash_-_What_happens_when_you_invoke_bash
