Title: Styles in OOo
Post by: dpeach on October 29, 2004, 09:29:48 pm
I am becoming a quick fan of styles in OOo. I have historically dealt with short documents (1000s that are only 1 page long). But have recently been dealing with larger documents that will be edited time and again it the future. Admittedly, most of these "longer" ones are only 5 pages or so, but the constant editing of these documents will be helped by using the Stylist.

Know anything about the Stylist in OOo? Let me encourage you to read and play along with this article. (http://www.osnews.com/story.php?news_id=8225&page=1) It will take obout 10 minutes to complete, but it will convince you that if you deal with documents that will be heavily edited, or grow into several pages, then you will want to use styles.

It gives an example that reminds you of a legal document, but can be easily implemented into just about any type of work.

For me, I am doing large outlines of teaching material. I know I am not using styles to their fullest, but I am making progress and will learn more as I go.
