Why 2.6 kernel?
« Reply #1 on: February 03, 2004, 05:28:50 pm »
atapi support rather than scsi emulation is a big one for some people
2.6 is also much more faster for me especially with XF86 and boot time compared to 2.4 on both the older P4B 533fsb chip i had and now the P4C 800fsb HT-SMP chip that I have now...with the new hyperthreaded p4, to me it seems like 2.6 actually handles intel's hyperthreaded chips nicely compared to 2.4....also 2.6 suppose to have a 50%+ boost in hyperthreading performance over 2.4 from what I've heard

currently running 2.6.1 on my slackware 9.1 p4c system, on the slackware 9.1 p4b system, and 2.6.1-mm5 is on the slackware 9.1 centrino laptop....all 3 run great and cdburning is much better now with atapi support  

and last thing I guess is that alsa is much easier to manage now that you can compile it directly into kernel.....for distros with oss, alsa is a huge plus and with distros running seperate compiled alsa, no more recompiling alsa after changing the kernel like had to do with 2.4

minor or possibly major things like better SATA support is there also along with better all around support for new hardware....but thats one of those things that really don't matter unless you have the hardware that I'm speaking of

From everything I've read, it is a complete rewrite. The old NTFS project was abandonned either last year or late 2002. I knew one of the guys who was working on it and he said it was hopeless. This new NTFS support is supposedly stable. I've used it some with no data loss. To top things off, I've used some NT Rescue Disks using 2.4.x and the new NTFS write support compiled in and we've been able to recover/change the Administrator password on a top of our systems here. Once again, no harm...so far.
