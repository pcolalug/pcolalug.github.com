I think that it may be a mistake to presume that the goal of a BS in an IT-related major should be to turn out a competent Computer Scientist. In the government, it has been my experience that the expectations of a newly minted programmer are pretty low. Folks with a BS are generally not (nor should they be, IMHO) expected to decided what language, platform, architectures, and methodologies to use. They should be familiar with whichever ones of those *I* pick for the project, sure, but generally, new programmers are expected to write code that's been spec'd out in advance. Ok, so sometimes the specs aren't as complete as they should be, but the general idea, I think, is that a BS should prepare a student for a professional IT carreer while weeding out those unsuited to the profession. This does not mean that they should be taught any particular set of languages (after all, language choice is many times a question of fashion), but rather the analytical skills needed to takle a wide range of common programming problems, where to go for documentation, how to *read* said documentation, and to pick up unfamiliar concepts quickly. Most of the important IT stuff, they'll generally learn OTJ or on their own anyway.
