Title: NAS -- Network Attached Storage
Post by: dpeach on October 09, 2005, 09:57:40 pm
For me this is a new type of hardware that I had no knowledge existed till yesterday. A stand alone, or external, hard drive that sits on the network solely as a storage device. It is similar to an external hard drive that connects via IEEE-1394 or USB 2.0. The biggest difference is that this does not connect to just one machine. Since it is attached to the network via standard CAT-5 cabling, it is available to all the computers on the network.

I guess I am familiar with file servers which are essentially the same thing. But this device is an enclosure that you put your own drive into. Just like a Firewire or USB 2.0 drive. Enclosure sells for about $100.

A review can be seen here: http://www.tomsnetworking.com/Reviews-223-ProdID-ADSNASKIT.php

The company and product are here: http://www.adstech.com/products/NAS-806-EF/intro/NAS_806_intro.asp?pid=NAS-806-EF

This one happens to have a bittorrent client built in. Also a web server. Not sure how robust the web server is.

Sounds like a very nice addition to a network. As an example of where this comes in handy is if you share a drive on one computer with others. That requires the computer that the shared drive is on to be powered up, even if you do not need that computer for anything else but to share files. Some of you may have file servers running. For me, I share a drive with my wife on her machine. When she cuts out at night (hours before I do), I either lose my share, or have to make the trip downstairs to shut off her computer when I am done.

Just thought I would share this product I found with you (pun intended).
