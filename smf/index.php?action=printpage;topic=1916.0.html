Title: SMP Capable Distribution
Post by: rugmonster on October 14, 2004, 04:46:33 am
I would suggest one, but I would get yelled at for it not being the easiest. By the way, when you ask for "best", it tends to start one of those very heated religious wars.

Can you tell us what you're looking for in this distro? What do you want it to do for you and what do you want to have to do to it? You can make just about anything be a mail/web/dns server, but different distros are geared a little more to being "server" distros and some go so far as to providing you tools for managing a whole bunch of servers from a central gui app. Depends on how free you want it and all that jazz.

Title: SMP Capable Distribution
Post by: rugmonster on October 14, 2004, 08:09:29 am
If you like OpenBSD, Slackware (loved by so many here) is a very BSD centric linux distro. It's simple, very giving to source compiling and can definitely be installed in one sitting. SMP support is going to be based on the kernel you compile. I believe they may even have a stock SMP kernel, but recompiling the kernel is one of the first things I do because I hate modules.
