I've just recently gotten back into desktop linux. I've always run a linux box on my network and it has always been integral to the operation of many of my home networking projects, but I wasn't so much of a fan of it on the desktop for several years. Now, I'm back and enjoying Gnome 2.10 on SuSE 9.3, yet I still have a Windows XP system because there are times that I need something over there and my girlfriend wasn't quite ready to learn something new. That was until last night...

I was working on getting my profile on the WinXP system transferred to my account for my new Samba domain and found a temporary directory that I couldn't delete. I then realized I couldn't even open it or take ownership. I did a search for the directory name and every hit made mention of spyware.

I was livid. I was running Webroot's Spysweeper to protect myself from these sorts of things. I made sure my definitions were up to date and ran a full scan. It didn't find anything, so I downloaded the Microsoft Antispyware utility that is still in beta. I ran a quick scan and it found nothing, so I made it run a full scan and it FINALLY found it!

I managed to get it cleaned off, but I was up to 2:30am trying to get it off of my system. Then I had the "Come to Jesus" moment; what had that thing collected and sent off to some far off place on the internet. I know I've put in my credit card number, social security number, bank account information and more through my browser. Luckily, the malware I had didn't include a keylogger (from what I could find out), but what if it did? I thought I was protected. Neither me nor my girlfriend use IE. I run SpySweeper and Symantec Antivirus all the time and do regularly scheduled sweeps. Why deal with that hassle at all?

After talking to my girlfriend about it this morning, I'm going to show her how to use my Linux desktop and we're going to do financial and otherwise sensitive Internet transactions on it. I'm going to do my best to harden the Linux box and lock down my outgoing network. Possibly setup a filtering web proxy. Security just became even more real to me. I never thought I would feel this insecure on my home network, but I do and I'm doing something about it. I just wanted to share.
