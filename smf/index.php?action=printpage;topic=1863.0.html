Hi everybody,

I've been to a couple of the Pcolalug meetings now, so some of you would probably recognize me if we were to run into each other.  I'm a tall, old guy with a Grey beard and a growing pot-belly.  Typical geek, I suppose, except maybe for the old part.  And the Grey beard.

I started coming to the meetings because I'm determined to shake loose from Windows and I know absolutely nothing about Linux, or anyway I didn't a few months ago.  I think I am slowly learning.

I usually upgrade my hardware every 5 or 6 years, and I have been using an old 400 MHz Pentium machine for that long at least.  So, about six months ago I started gathering the parts to put together a state-of-the-art machine.  (State-of-the-art for all of a few days, I know.)  I bought a Tyan K8W dual Opteron board, and two 246 cpus for it, with 4 Gigs of on-board ECC memory, and a 6 drive SCSI array controlled with a LSI Megaraid dual controller.  I have learned in the past that disk access is a major speed bottleneck and I wanted to implement a raid 5-0 setup, which I did.  Then I built the box to put it all in (all 1/4 inch or heaver aluminum plate) with water cooling, back-lit flow meters, CD/DVD burner, etc, and LOTS of pretty blinking lights.  I can't lift the thing, but hey, it was never intended as a laptop!  Now all it needs is SOFTWARE.  Unfortunately, the AMD64 software from everyone is just a little flaky.  Actually, it's all a LOT flaky.  It is a horribly complex problem, I know, but it will get there eventually.  Meanwhile, this is my at-work workstation.  I'll just dual-boot for the duration.

Work is a tiny little research laboratory here in P'cola called Poiesis Research, Inc.  I started this business in '91 after working as a Research Scientist for the Navy at the Navy Aerospace Medical Research Laboratory for several years.  I love the independence of “doing my own thing” and would never go back to working for someone else as long as I can keep eating.  Pot-bellies require regular maintenance, you know.  (The maintenance material of choice is, of course, BEER.)  My research emphasis has been on passive hearing protection, but I've also developed things as diverse as new production technologies for high-strength composites, a nice robot arm with true spherical joints, and even a compound bow.

I have managed to get SuSE 9.1 up on my machine with SMP and AMD64, and even though I like the idea of Gentoo much more . . . at least I can get this to work!  I also want to try OpenBSD when their 3.6 release comes out.  I've already ordered the CDs!

I'll see you all at the next meeting!

Bill Cushman
