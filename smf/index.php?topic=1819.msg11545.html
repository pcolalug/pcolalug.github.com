In my research I have found out a few things today. Been a productive day (until I tried to install Mandrake 10.1 and it borked everything, but that is another story. I was down for about 6 hours trying to get it up again). Your milage is likely to vary, but here is what I have found and plan to do.

This site has some good general information about things you can do to help your VoIP. In a nutshell they rant about 2 things. 1. How good Skype is. 2. DSL is generally better for VoIP communications than cable.

Point 2 is where you may disagree. But if you read the page and keep an open mind about the fact that the standards you are used to experiencing are not quite the standards here then you would consider alternatives too.

The section that starts out "Switch to a better Internet Service Provider if you want better voice quality" is worth reading. It is towards the top, easy to find. At the bottom in the section "Mistakes to avoid" this comment is made. "Getting cable instead of DSL (unless cable is cheaper): In my experience, DSL is much more reliable and gives better VoIP voice quality that cable modem. (Note: This generalization isn't always true, so investigate your ISP on Broadband Reports before making a decision.)"

Based on my former research, I like DSL generally better than cable. But when the DSL company that I was using in Pcola got bought out and eventually went under, Cox was there to try. Had real good experience with them, so stayed with cable. When we first got here we had very unreliable phone service (bad news for DSL). Since then I have replaced several phone lines and have waterproofed them because we lost service every time it rained. But, as it has not rained in over 2 months, it is kind of hard to know if my handyman skills were up to the task.

I will be signing up for DSL after the weekend and should have a couple of weeks to test out before the next cable bill is due to see which one I stick with.

Even with just 19 up I should be able to do a good signal that is only taking 16 when I am not using the net. But even without using the net there are times when the problem (slices of words are just gone) persists. Having run more tests, I am apparently getting better throughput up than the 19, but I am not getting any better phone connection. Further testing at broadbandreports.com show me between 67 and 85 up.

If you are interested in "hearing" the problem then PM me and I will either give you my number, or you can give me yours.

What I have understood about Asterisk is that they are a VoIP to VoIP solution. They do not have a portal to POTS lines. You still have to have a connection with someone to do that.

The deal with Asterisk is that it makes a PBX. You can get a POTS interface card to use regular phones with it. If you know phone stuff, think the old Merlin systems. Okay, now you can hook that Asterisk PBX through the internet to a VoIP service provider such as VoicePulse or Vonage. This is how you would get out to the commercial PSTN. The little router box you get for your VoIP box can be replaced with a system running Asterisk provided you are using the correct encapsulation and such to interface with their setup. It all depends on the VoIP provider.

An update. I have just finished my first month with my new provider. I am using the only DSL provider in the country that I know of. I hope they continue to work. Not too many options.

I am buying 256 down. Currently getting between 180 and 195. I have some bad phone lines that I am attributing the loss of speed on. I am working on getting a new line installed from as close to the pole as I can get all the way back to the DSL modem. Right now it is split from a connection that is a split from the connection that splits into the wiring that splits off of the main line coming from the pole. Make sense? Needles to say, I don't have much confidence in it.

My ability to talk on the phone has been tremendous! The quality of this connection is amazingly better than the cable provider. Though I was getting about the same speed with cable, apparently the quality was not there.

I have talked several hours on the phone. Even had a conversation this weekend that was 2 hours long without any problems. I try to limit my surfing while on the phone to a minimum. Can even play ET while talking. But only briefly. Eventually they start to interfere with one another. Though I have gone around 4 minutes with both of them going before I had to tell the person I was more interested in the game than them so they had to leave (what are brothers for?).

My recommendation then would be that if you are having problems with using VoIP and you meet all the requirements for a good connection, then look into another ISP. I have read that, generally, DSL is better for this than cable. I have no numbers really to back it up with, but that has definately been true for me.
