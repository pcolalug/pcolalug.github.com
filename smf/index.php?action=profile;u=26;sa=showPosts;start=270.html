Normally, I don't jump in on things and rant when someone's asking for advice.  But, here's my $0.02 on it (at the risk of inciting a holy war).  Don't go picking up any of the Basics.  They're not good even for learning tools and will just flatout teach you bad programming habits.

279  Distributions / Other Linux Distributions / Anyone else use Slack? on: October 25, 2002, 06:44:23 am
Wow, such an old thread.   Oh well, guess I'll add my $0.02 to it.

Slackware, like I think I've stated before, is what I use simply because it's what I'm most comfortable with.  The install is still quite a bit behind most (all?) other distros, but then again it's not geared towards the rh/mdk/suse crowd   .  

Package management probably qualifies as the next thing that most people find uncomfortable about slack.  In short, Slackware's package management is effectively lacking, so that puts it up there as a source-based distro.  But since I feel better compiling things from source anyways, this again is a matter of choice.

And as imnes said earlier, things such as gnome and kde are left unmolested.  This creates a sterile-looking and rather unintuitive interface.  Not that this isn't changeable, it's just something that makes slack extremely newbie-unfriendly (ie, those coming from a strictly doze-based world.)

So is there a point to this post? Not really, just sharing my opinion.
