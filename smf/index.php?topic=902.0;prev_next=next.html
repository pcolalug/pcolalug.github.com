The math will be the fun stuff soon enough when you get to Analysis of Algorithms and Theory of Computation. Pay particular attention in your discreet math classes and skip diff-eq for Linear Algebra. The Cal track is fun and interesting but you feel gyped when you learn how to integrate most anything with a matrix and a few simple operations. Oh, and hold on to your Rosen Descreet Math book, as you will need parts of it again in AoA.

This all being said, a BS is definately required if you want to get a livable wage in this area. The markets tight and its hard to find a shop where you can spread your wings and learn cool stuff. Also, folks don't seem to descriminate too terribly much around here between the CS and the CIS folks in my experience.
