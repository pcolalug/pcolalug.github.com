Yep, it exists, but isn't going outside of Google. (http://slashdot.org/comments.pl?sid=175746&cid=14609148)

Quote from: Chris DiBona
...know that Google getting into the Red Hat business would be kind of dumb, and it would distract from our moon teleporter and cold fusion [google.com] projects

As trivial as it is to customize a linux installer, it only makes sense to contribute to a current distribution rather than make a new one. There are hundreds, if not thousands of different ones out there doing things different ways, geared toward doing different things.

And it makes sense that Google would have an internal distro. Correct configs are built into the packages, management is made easier because I'm sure they automate software updates via internal server. Why go through the hassle of using stock packages and changing settings when they can make their own packages with settings already in place? Larger schools tend to have their own linux distribution to ease administration, but mostly they are just another distribution with custom packages to make administration easier.
