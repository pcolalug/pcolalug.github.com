Title: AMD's Next Step
Post by: rugmonster on February 10, 2004, 09:42:55 am
As most of us know, AMD (http://www.amd.com) released it's new x86/64 architecture in the Opteron  (http://www.amd.com/us-en/Processors/ProductInformation/0,,30_118_8796,00.html) line of processors capable of running 64 bit applications as well as 32 bit applications. Aimed at the server market and to compete with the Intel (http://www.intel.com/) Itanium (http://www.intel.com/products/server/processors/server/itanium2/index.htm?iid=ipp_srvr+proc_itanium2srvr&), a 64 bit only processor making it only able to run 64 bit applications and 32 bit applications through an emulation layer. Due to the flexibility of the Opterons, sales nearly doubled that of the Itanium last year, despite the Itanium being available longer.

Well, now it seems HP has taken notice and has decided to adopt the Opteron in it's high end servers. Article found here (http://www.newsfactor.com/perl/story/23148.html). Go AMD, go! The article also mentions Sun Microsystems and IBM signing deals with AMD.
