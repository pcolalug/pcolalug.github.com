Quote from: dpeach
I got it installed on the DeadRat 9.0 system that I will be using. But I have not been able to get any sound in or out. It says that my system is muted, but when I check on the buttons, the speakers and microphone don't unmute. I am wondering if I need to be running the same codex as the server. Anyone have a clue what that might be?

You'll have to get the sound working well with other things (CD player, xmms, xine, or whatever) before trying to make TS work.  As for the muting, check that the various sliders in the mixer (usually aumix for Gnome and kmix for KDE) are up enough and not muted.  The codecs are not important, as your client will auto-adjust to whatever the server is using.

Quote from: dpeach
Another question that I have about it is concerning the actual logging in. Do we log into the TS server before we get into the game? When we switch sides, do we have to go out of the game to log into the other team channel?

Yes, you log into TS before starting the game.  Good point about switching sides, as you can't really access the TS window when the game is full-screen.  I think the solution would be that we just stay together in the initial TS channel regardless of whether we are playing as Axis or Allies.  In other words, the channels are effectively "Pcola" and "HAM"...

Quote from: depeach
I have downloaded the new patch to the game, but get hung up on getting onto their server because I need to download a pile of maps. Will that be a problem on Thursday? I am planning on getting there 2 to 3 hours early on Thursday to work out the final kinks. But if it goes like the last several weeks, 3 hours will not be enough.

AFAIK, your ET client will attempt to download maps it doesn't have, which are in the server's map rotation scheme.  Don't think there's any way around that...
