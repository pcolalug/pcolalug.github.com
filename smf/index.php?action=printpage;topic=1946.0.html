Title: Warhammer 40,000: Dawn of War
Post by: SiliconFOXX on October 21, 2004, 05:17:41 pm
I know it doesn't technically have Linux support yet but if you find SOME way of playing this game know that you HAVE to if you even remotely like RTSes.


This isn't just a game for fans of the famous table-top wargame (I love it, too, just can't afford it anymore) but really for anyone who loves a good RTS.  I cannot describe the amount of ways this game is incredible short of saying that if you're only going to play one game this year this is it.  Yes, more than Halo 2, more than Doom 3, more than Half-Life 2.  Those are all flat-out awesome (or going to be awesome) but this is unbelievable.  Just...unbelievable.

If you've got the hardware you're going to love the graphics.  It's worth sacrificing a bit of resolution to turn up the textures (I've got it at 1024x768 with full textures on everything so you know it's not TOO taxing) and it's shocking.  Dead seirous it's like watching an animated battle of Warhammer 40K miniatures, complete with hand-to-hand combat, tracer fire, rockets, frag grenades, power fists, whirlwinds, dreadnoughts (aaaaahhh, the dreadnoughts), dropships, warp spiders, demons, cover, and much, MUCH more.  

Like RTSes but too much micromanagement of your base for you?  Hate turtlers?  Yeah, this game doesn't just kick that tired old formula's ***, it breaks its ****ing FOOT off in it!  Can't turtle 'cause your base defenses...well, suck.  That means there's only one thing to do and that's to get out there and SLUG IT OUT!  There's no capturing of a gold mine and having a stream of workers pounding on it, no, you capture strategic points on the map while you're out hunting your enemies.


The online play is a dream come true.  Bar none it's what I've always wanted;  being able to play WH40K online.  I just can't describe it, it's massive.  Totally freakin' massive.  Here's an idea of how massive.  Say your squad (units?  Heh, we build SQUADS here, not just units) limit cap is at 10 which is realistic for the beginning of the game.  If you actually had ten squads that are maxed out with units (you can beef up their ranks while they're on the field, just push a button and minutes later you'll have a new unit bolstering the squad's ranks...I CANNOT describe how nice this is) then that means 9 people per squad...ten squads, nine people per squad, 90 FREAKIN' MARINES on the field!  And that's small, but VERY easy to manage due to commanding whole squads.

There is no greater joy as of late than getting up that 90-man force, taking it over to your opponent's base while they're out looking for you, and LEVELLING IT.  Just flat-out LEVELLING it.  I wish I could make some movies but Fraps is being...well, stupid right now (HUGE files...REALLY huge) just to show off the coolness but it'd probably fill up a DVD by the time I was done with 30-second samples.



There's a demo out there, go out and grab it and find a way to play it.  Bar none it's AWESOME.






Oh, I just played my first online battle today.  2v2v2 people and me and my teammate WIPED THE FLOOR with the opposition.  Just walked all over them.  One team put up a fight against me but once my teammate held 'em off for a minute so I could get some troops up there we retalliated and devastated him.
