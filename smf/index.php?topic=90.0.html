I am not sure why people think that MS has the best UI on the market. Just because they have the majority of the market share does not mean that their UI is that great.

I do understand software providers wanting to "copy" them. If that is what you know, it makes it comfortable to make the switch. On the other hand, if there is a better way, why don't we educate the people that we are trying to convert. It is not like learning how to use a word processor is that difficult.

I could see a secretary that spends his/her whole day in MS Office having some issues with converting to another suite. But if you are talking about the majority of users, it is guys that just need to type a letter on occasion. How difficult is that to figure out. If the interface is better, then it should be relatively easy.

I have sold my soul to Lotus WordPro for 6 years. I know it and use it better than most people using MS Office. But I have had no problems using OpenOffice.org. I am using it as my main word processor now in both operating systems.

Just my 3 cents.
