Title: I see a problem with this
Post by: on February 07, 2004, 03:11:35 pm
I see a problem with this.

On packet switched networks, you can't be certain that your packets will arrive in order that your port-knock "password" requires.

If you wanted to implement this properly here's what I suggest.
Implement a seeded random port sequence that changes on a timed basis, i.e. "Secure-ID" style.  This would prevent anyone who guessed your port-knock combo to  be able to use it again.  Well, they could use it until the current combo expires, and they would need the correct seed to stay in sync.

Other than that, it's not a bad idea.  I also can see two more downsides:
1.  Increase port scans (people looking for port-knock combos), which lead to bigger log files to review.
2.  Increased firewall ruleset complexity, which is never a good thing.
