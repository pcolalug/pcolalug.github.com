Title: Are Mac's Overpriced?
Post by: scottstancil on October 22, 2004, 07:56:34 pm
I am a Mac owner and there are a couple of points to consider.

1.  Linux's desktop is getting better and better and better.  Just following in the last year blows me away.  Despite all of my advocacy for Mac, when it comes time to do the laptop thing again, I will compare hardware/software again like you did.  

2.  You need to include the software that comes with OS X like iLife and a lot of the little utils; however, I would only include the utils that you are most likely to use.

3.  If you decide Mac, wait.  Seriously.  If you can wait, wait for OS X.4 or Tiger.  The OS is $129 each time you upgrade, $99 for academic discount.

4.  You must include the APP.  Do NOT buy an Apple without the warranty, no matter if it is a laptop or desktop.  You are not buying something that you can jog down to Best Buy and replace the part necessarily.  PCs are flat out more flexible over the long term.


2 cents from a guy that will have his Powerbook 15" 1.25GHz and PowerMac Dual 2GHz pried from his cold dead hands. :)

Scott

Title: Are Mac's Overpriced?
Post by: kilar on October 23, 2004, 02:13:56 pm
I also own Apple and Linux computer (plus a Windows laptop just for UWF classes). I would never get rid of my Apple, if you want a computer that you just unpack from the box, plug in, and turn on. If you want a computer to upgrade and tinker with, then get a barebones desktop and install your favorite Linux.

You can't just compare Apples and PCs-- it's like comparing oranges and, well, apples. Because the Apple uses RISC processors, clock speed is not as important as it is with PCs. Apples maintain their value longer. They have a small share of the market (there are only about three Mac owners in the universe, one of them is the CEO of Apple).

I was sold on Apples when my friend let me play with his iMac for a day. But, if you still go to school (especially UWF), get a PC. If you take home work that requires PC software, get a PC. If you play games, get a PC. Otherwise, I would not say that Apple hardware is overpriced.
