Post by: rburt3 on October 16, 2002, 07:12:50 pm
Cygwin has saved my butt more and more lately.  I went ahead and dl'd the latest OpenSSH and built it in Cygwin, since that seemed much easier than using VC++.  Anyway, once I generated the request per the command line above, I took the key to our CA so I could get a cirtificate. The whole reason we're doing this is that we need an offline meathod of getting certs, so he pasted the output from openssl into Netscape CMS and voila, Randall had his new cert. I brought the new cert (cert.p7b) back to my desk and copied back down to the harddrive as cert.pem. I then had to do the following
Code:
openssl pkcs12 -export -in cert.pem -inkey key.pem  -name "Randall Burt" -out p12cert.p12

I then used IE to import the pkcs12 file into my local keystore.  To test, I signed a small Outlook macro and restarted Outlook. To my wonder and amazement, I was offered the opportunity to trust myself!  Thanks agiain for your help.

Now, I have to document the whole process as I am leaving this job for another one at the end of this month.  I hope to maybe post it as an article for the group.
