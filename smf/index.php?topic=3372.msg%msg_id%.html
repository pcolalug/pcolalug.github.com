So we need to generate some more interest in the LUG.  I hoped to come up with a few more ideas since I last posted, but haven't really had time to even think about it, so I'll list the ideas presented so far, and ask for discussion and more ideas.

One thought given was that we could use more social activities, nothing set in stone but perhaps just random get-togethers such as cookouts, things that are less computer-related.

One method of increasing attendance could be to move the meeting night to a weekday, when people who work in town are going to be in town anyways, so that they don't have to make a special trip for a routine meeting.  This would also remove any conflict between someone wanting to go out on Saturday and go bar hopping/partying/clubbing/dating/etc.

We could try to increase our online presence more, in an effort to make it easier for people in the area who are looking online to find us.  This could consist of posting to shared calendar sites, or possibly starting a linux-related podcast.

We do need to increase our presence locally, with fliers, affiliating with other local tech related clubs, and possibly even radio spots on local tech-related talk shows.

It was also suggested that we put together some form of crash course that people can enroll in and learn to get their hands dirty with Linux.

That's all I have for now, let's get some healthy discussion going.

Well, since I was the one asking for a response/ideas, I should probably say something.  I debated whether to reply, or not, as some will think I'm a "negative-Nelly" after reading this post, but I prefer the term "realist".

All of the ideas that have been proposed are good ones.  The problem is that they have been done/tried before, and there just was never enough interest by more than half a dozen folks to make them amount to anything.  Let me explain what I mean a little:

1.  If we can't get folks to come to a Linux meeting, why would we think anyone would be interested in coming to a non-computer event?  We all have enough of that going on already, and that is the most common reason for non-attendance ("too busy").  I don't see it happening.
2.  The presentations - we have already done both "new-user" and "advanced" presentations.  I did many of them myself.  There was very little interest shown for any of them, and of course we only ever had a dozen or so show up.  Not saying we shouldn't try them again, but *first* we gotta get some chairs filled.  It's almost a chicken-and-egg situation.  Will people come to see a presentation?  What's the point of a presentation given to 3 people...?
3.  I agree with the comments about the community projects.  Lots of talk, but no action.  Don't see that changing, to be honest.
4.  There may be some benefit to moving the meeting to a weekday evening.  I dunno.  Doesn't much matter to me either way.  Not sure how I feel about moving the location, it is after all, the "Pensacola" Linux Users Group.  Again, the real issue is getting folks from "Pensacola" to show up.
5.  As for LUG fliers and CD's...  I personally made hundreds of (color) flyers, passed them out to a few folks for further spreading, posted a bunch myself, and many of them never got posted as far as I know.  I also made 30-50 CD's with the LUG logo on them (for Brett) once.  I don't believe either effort had *ANY* effect on membership/participation.  It was a lot of work, and there was never anything positive to come out of it.
6.  Radio ads are just not gonna happen.  Who's going to pay for that?
7.  The integration with other groups/clubs is probably a good thing.  Again, it will come down to more than one/two people willing to actually do something.  The interest level may spike for a month, but will likely fade out again quickly.  I could be wrong, but experience has shown otherwise.

I could probably go on some more, but I think you get the idea.  As someone else stated, I also want the LUG to survive and grow.  Probably more than most.  I tend to be rather blunt with what I say, and sometimes that hurts people's feelings.  Sorry, but that's how it is.  Sometimes that is needed to jolt people into thinking and taking some action.  Most of my statements here are entirely based on watching the LUG deteriorate over the last 5 years.  I am very willing to help out with things, but at the same time I am "gunshy" about getting too excited only to see things fizzle.  I don't know what the answers are to fix things, and I'm gonna just sit back and watch for now.  I gave a lot of effort and time in the past to this LUG, and am not willing to do it again until I see the same level of willingness from some others.  Again, sorry for my bluntness, and if you'd like to just tell me to "F-off", then go right ahead.

I'll be around, and hopeful.

Dan
