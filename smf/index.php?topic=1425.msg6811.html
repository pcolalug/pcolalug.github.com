Features-wise, I would probably make the switch to ATI.  In the desktops we have seen an incredible increase in reliability under Windows & Mac.  My friend at Red Hat is extremely hot over hte ATI devices right now because the community support has ramped up, but I also thought they were doing their own binary driver.

On the other hand, I am extremely happy with my stock Xandros 2.0 running an included nVidia binary driver.  (Nice touch.)  UT2004 and Enemy Territory both came up fine and well, just worked.

The reason I would make the switch is because I am mostly in Windows on my desktop as it is a shared family desktop and I have games buried where my 3 year old can't play them.  "No son, no gibbing today."  Anyways, the reviews keep saying that support is kicking butt for the ATI product and my pal at RH has convinced me to make the leap of faith.

I would say that if you are willing to put up with potential delays or issues in favor of raw performance, go ATI.  If you want stability and just the usual desktop that just works, forego the 8fps and stay with the nVidia.

Good luck, sounds like a touch decision.

ATI v nVidia (non-Linux specific):
http://www.clanbase.com/finger_cache.php?plan=johnc,John+Carmack

A friend and I had an IM chat arguing ATI v. nVidia this morning.  Here are some highlights:

friend: with my NVidia on BZFlag @1024x768 I was getting 100-200 FPS. With the ATI I get about 65-90
friend: the reason the NVidia performs better is because they take hardware shortcuts in the binary-only driver
friend: OTOH, the binary -only driver produces a kernel that is unsupportable
friend: ask any kernel engineer
friend: some is off th4e cuff, some is SOP from working with the kernel team
friend: "My kernel paniced with the NVidia driver loaded" "We can't debug the NVidia driver, call them"


To which I am going to caveat this.  I have been diehard nVidia for close to five years because of its reputation for rock hard stability under Linux.  I have just not seen the issues he talks about.  I suspect from prior postings that the majority of the group will maintain the same observation.  I suggested to him that someone should do a benchmark between the two cards.

friend: we aren't going to load the NVidia modules for a benchmark, because they taint the kernels

Frankly, I could truly care less about tainting the kernels.  Since I have stopped coding nearly 8 years ago, source code v. binary discussions are meaningless to me now.  It is nice to have, but plays a very minor decision in what I choose for my hardware and or software.

Would this constitute an interesting project?  Perhaps the group could submit something like:

Distributions
Kernel (stock v. compiled and version)
CPU
Memory
Motherboard?
Video Card (model, PCI v AGP, and memory configuration)

Then perhaps the numbers for UT, UT 2003, UT2004, or Quake 3?  Or both since Q3's framerate tends to be card bound and UT tends to be CPU bound.
