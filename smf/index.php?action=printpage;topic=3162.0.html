PcolaGolfer, you may get a little more response if you give a little more information about the position (is it full-time W2, short or long term contract, etc) and some more specifics as to what aspect of .NET development they are looking for. Windows and ASP.NET application development, as well as the various other areas of .NET development, are very different between them. And while it mostly is assumed that most .NET development is done in C#, there are still other languages such as VB.NET and J# that fall under the .NET umbrella. While a competent developer/engineer should be able to transition from one to the other, it might be helpful to specify a little more as to what the candidate is expected to have experience with so they can tailor their resume and potentially give them a better feeling about the job.
